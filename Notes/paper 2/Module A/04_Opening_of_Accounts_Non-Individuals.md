Opening of Accounts – Non-Individuals

MODULE A – GENERAL BANKING OPERATIONS  
JAIIB – Detailed Concept Notes (Beginner Friendly, Conceptual & Exam-Oriented)

---

## 1. Introduction

Opening of accounts for non-individual entities is more complex than individual accounts because:

- These entities act through authorized persons.
- Legal capacity must be verified.
- Documents establishing constitution must be examined.
- Authority to operate must be clearly established.

Improper opening of such accounts may expose the bank to fraud, operational loss, or regulatory penalties.

This block covers:

- HUF
- Partnership firms
- Companies
- Trusts
- Societies
- Current account norms
- Legal Entity Identifier (LEI)

---

## 2. Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)

An HUF is a family unit governed by Hindu Law.

### Key Features:

- Head of family is called Karta.
- Only Karta can operate the account.
- Other members are called coparceners.

### Documents Required:

- HUF declaration.
- List of coparceners.
- PAN of HUF.
- KYC of Karta.
- Proof of HUF existence.

### Important Legal Point:

The Karta has implied authority to borrow and operate on behalf of HUF.

Example:

If a coparcener signs cheque without authority, bank must not honor it.

If Karta dies:

- Eldest coparcener may become new Karta.
- Fresh documentation required.

---

## 3. Partnership Firm

A partnership firm is governed by the Indian Partnership Act, 1932.

### Key Characteristics:

- Partners collectively own the firm.
- Each partner is agent of the firm.
- Partners have joint and several liability.

### Documents Required:

- Partnership deed.
- PAN of firm.
- Registration certificate (if registered).
- KYC of all partners.
- Authority letter for account operation.

### Important Points:

- If deed is silent, all partners may operate jointly.
- Bank must follow mandate strictly.
- Upon death of partner, partnership may dissolve unless deed provides otherwise.

Example:

If one partner issues cheque beyond authority, bank may face legal dispute.

---

## 4. Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

LLP is governed by LLP Act, 2008.

### Features:

- Separate legal entity.
- Liability limited to contribution.
- Designated partners manage affairs.

### Documents Required:

- Certificate of incorporation.
- LLP agreement.
- PAN of LLP.
- KYC of designated partners.
- Resolution authorizing account opening.

Important:

LLP has separate legal status unlike partnership.

---

## 5. Companies

Companies are governed by Companies Act, 2013.

### Types:

- Private Limited Company
- Public Limited Company
- One Person Company (OPC)

### Characteristics:

- Separate legal entity.
- Perpetual succession.
- Limited liability.
- Acts through Board of Directors.

### Documents Required:

- Certificate of Incorporation.
- Memorandum and Articles of Association.
- PAN of company.
- Board Resolution for opening account.
- KYC of directors and authorized signatories.

### Important Legal Concept:

Company is artificial person.  
It acts only through authorized resolution.

Bank must verify:

- Whether borrowing power exists in MOA.
- Whether resolution authorizes specific persons to operate.

Example:

If unauthorized director signs cheque without resolution, bank may not be protected.

---

## 6. Trusts

Trusts are governed by Indian Trusts Act (for private trusts).

Types:

- Private Trust
- Public Trust
- Charitable Trust

### Documents Required:

- Trust deed.
- Registration certificate (if applicable).
- PAN of trust.
- KYC of trustees.
- Resolution authorizing account opening.

Important:

Bank must verify:

- Objects of trust.
- Powers of trustees.
- Number of trustees required to operate.

If trust deed requires joint signature of two trustees, bank must not allow single signature.

---

## 7. Societies

Societies are governed by Societies Registration Act.

### Documents Required:

- Registration certificate.
- Memorandum of association.
- List of managing committee members.
- Resolution authorizing account opening.
- KYC of authorized signatories.

Society is not separate legal entity like company but operates through managing committee.

---

## 8. Current Account Norms (RBI Guidelines)

RBI has issued guidelines restricting opening of current accounts for borrowers.

Key principle:

If borrower has credit exposure above specified threshold with banking system, other banks cannot open current account without NOC from lending bank.

Purpose:

- Prevent diversion of funds.
- Improve credit discipline.
- Strengthen monitoring.

Banks must conduct due diligence before opening current accounts for business entities.

---

## 9. Legal Entity Identifier (LEI)

LEI is a 20-character alphanumeric code used to uniquely identify legal entities participating in financial transactions.

Applicable in:

- Large value transactions.
- Derivative transactions.
- Certain corporate borrowings.

Individuals generally do not require LEI.

Purpose:

- Improve financial transparency.
- Track systemic risk.
- Avoid identity confusion in financial markets.

Banks must ensure LEI compliance where applicable.

---

## 10. KYC and AML in Non-Individual Accounts

In non-individual accounts, bank must identify:

- Beneficial owner.
- Controlling persons.
- Ultimate ownership structure.

Example:

If company is owned by another company, bank must trace beneficial ownership as per KYC rules.

Failure may lead to PMLA violation.

---

## 11. Red Flags in Non-Individual Accounts

- Complex ownership structure.
- Frequent changes in authorized signatories.
- Unusual high-value transactions inconsistent with profile.
- Use of shell companies.

Such cases require enhanced due diligence.

---

## 12. Practical Case-Based Understanding

Case 1:

Partnership firm account opened without verifying partnership deed.  
Dispute arises between partners. Bank may face litigation.

Case 2:

Company account opened without proper board resolution.  
Unauthorized withdrawal occurs.

Case 3:

Trust account operated by single trustee despite requirement of joint operation.  
Transaction may be challenged.

---

## 13. Important JAIIB Concepts

- Company has separate legal personality.
- Partnership partners have unlimited liability.
- LLP has limited liability.
- HUF operated by Karta.
- Trust must follow trust deed strictly.
- Current account opening subject to RBI discipline.
- Beneficial ownership identification mandatory.

---

## 14. Conceptual Clarity

Non-individual account opening requires:

- Verification of constitution.
- Verification of authority.
- Verification of ownership.
- Compliance with KYC and AML.
- Compliance with RBI guidelines.

If documentation and authority are properly established at beginning, future disputes and fraud risks are minimized.
