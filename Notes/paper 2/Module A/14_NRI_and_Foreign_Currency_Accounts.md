Customer Service, Grievance Redressal & RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme 2021

MODULE A – GENERAL BANKING OPERATIONS  
JAIIB – Detailed Notes (Regulatory + Legal + Practical Approach)

---

## 1. Introduction

Customer service is a core regulatory obligation of banks.  
Banks are not merely financial intermediaries — they are service institutions operating under strict customer protection norms.

Poor customer service can lead to:

- RBI penalties
- Reputational loss
- Ombudsman complaints
- Legal disputes

This block covers:

- Customer service guidelines
- Duties and rights of banker and customer
- Grievance redressal mechanism
- RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme, 2021

---

## 2. Customer Service in Banks

RBI has issued detailed guidelines on customer service standards.

Banks must ensure:

- Fair treatment
- Transparent charges
- Timely services
- Proper disclosure
- Non-discrimination

Customer service is monitored through:

- Internal audits
- RBI inspections
- Ombudsman complaints

---

## 3. Policy for General Management of Branches

Each bank must have:

- Board-approved customer service policy.
- Standard operating procedures.
- Grievance handling mechanism.
- Time-bound service delivery standards.

Branches must maintain:

- Complaint register.
- Display of grievance redressal contact details.
- Transparency in service charges.

---

## 4. ATM Operations – Customer Service Norms

Banks must ensure:

- Proper functioning of ATMs.
- Quick reversal of failed transactions.
- Compensation for delay in reversal.
- Display of helpline numbers.

If ATM transaction fails and amount is debited:

- Reversal must happen within prescribed time.
- Compensation payable if delay beyond regulatory timeline.

---

## 5. Service at the Counters

Banks must ensure:

- Reasonable waiting time.
- Separate queues for senior citizens and differently abled persons.
- Proper behaviour by staff.
- Accessibility to grievance officer.

Customer dignity and fairness are emphasized.

---

## 6. Information to Customers

Banks must clearly disclose:

- Interest rates
- Service charges
- Terms and conditions
- Penalties
- KYC requirements

Non-disclosure may be treated as unfair practice.

Transparency reduces disputes.

---

## 7. Special Arrangements at Branches

Banks must provide:

- Ramp access for disabled persons.
- Braille-enabled ATMs where feasible.
- Priority services for senior citizens.
- Assistance for visually impaired customers.

These are part of inclusive banking obligations.

---

## 8. Duties and Rights of Banker

### Duties of Banker

- Duty of Secrecy
- Duty of reasonable care
- Duty to honor cheques (if valid)
- Duty to follow customer mandate
- Duty to comply with court orders

### Rights of Banker

- Right of lien
- Right of set-off
- Right of appropriation
- Right to charge service fees

These rights must be exercised lawfully.

---

## 9. Rights of Customers

Customers have right to:

- Fair treatment
- Transparency
- Privacy
- Grievance redressal
- Compensation for deficiency in service

Customer awareness is encouraged through regulatory guidelines.

---

## 10. Internal Grievance Redressal Mechanism

Banks must establish:

- Nodal Officer for grievance handling.
- Complaint resolution timelines.
- Escalation matrix.

Complaints must be resolved within specified time.

If unresolved, customer may approach RBI Ombudsman.

# RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme, 2021

---

## 11. Objective of the Scheme

The RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme, 2021 was introduced to:

- Simplify grievance redressal.
- Integrate previous ombudsman schemes.
- Provide “One Nation, One Ombudsman”.

The scheme covers:

- Banks
- NBFCs
- Payment system participants

---

## 12. Grounds for Complaint

Customers may file complaint for:

- Non-payment or delay in payment.
- Excess charges.
- ATM/debit card issues.
- Loan-related disputes.
- Failure to follow RBI guidelines.
- Deficiency in service.

Complaint must first be made to the bank.

If not resolved within 30 days, customer may approach Ombudsman.

---

## 13. Filing Procedure

Complaint can be filed:

- Online
- Through email
- By post

No fee is charged.

Ombudsman office is centralized under RBI.

---

## 14. Powers of Ombudsman

Ombudsman can:

- Facilitate settlement through mediation.
- Pass award against bank.
- Direct compensation.

Compensation may include:

- Financial loss
- Mental harassment (within prescribed limits)

Award is binding if accepted by complainant.

---

## 15. Rejection of Complaint

Complaint may be rejected if:

- Frivolous or malicious.
- Already settled.
- Filed after limitation period.
- No deficiency of service.

Customer may appeal against Ombudsman decision.

---

## 16. Appeal Mechanism

If customer or bank is aggrieved:

- Appeal lies to Appellate Authority (Deputy Governor of RBI).
- Appeal must be filed within prescribed period.

Decision of Appellate Authority is final.

---

## 17. Case-Based Understanding

Case 1:

ATM transaction failed but bank delayed reversal beyond regulatory time.  
Customer may approach Ombudsman.

Case 2:

Bank levies charges not disclosed in schedule of charges.  
Customer can complain.

Case 3:

Bank fails to resolve complaint within 30 days.  
Ombudsman complaint maintainable.

---

## 18. Important JAIIB Points

- RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme, 2021 replaced earlier schemes.
- Complaint must first be made to bank.
- 30-day waiting period before approaching Ombudsman.
- No fee required.
- Ombudsman can award compensation.
- Appeal lies to Deputy Governor of RBI.

---

## 19. Conceptual Clarity

Customer service is not optional — it is regulatory mandate.

Internal grievance mechanism acts as first layer of protection.

RBI Ombudsman acts as external independent redressal forum.

Strong grievance redressal system:

- Builds trust
- Reduces litigation
- Enhances bank reputation
- Ensures regulatory compliance

Understanding this framework is essential for JAIIB and for real-world banking practice.
