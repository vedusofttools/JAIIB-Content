## 1. Introduction

Agriculture plays a critical role in India’s economy.

Banks provide agricultural finance to:

- Promote rural development
- Increase farm productivity
- Support farmers’ income
- Achieve Priority Sector Lending targets

Agricultural loans are subject to specific schemes and policy guidelines.

---

# PART I – TYPES OF AGRICULTURAL LOANS

---

## 2. Short-Term Loans

Short-term agricultural loans are granted for:

- Crop cultivation
- Purchase of seeds
- Fertilizers
- Labour expenses

These are typically repaid after harvest.

Crop loans fall under this category.

---

## 3. Medium-Term Loans

Medium-term loans are granted for:

- Purchase of agricultural equipment
- Minor irrigation projects
- Dairy and allied activities

Repayment period usually ranges from 3 to 5 years.

---

## 4. Long-Term Loans

Long-term agricultural loans are used for:

- Land development
- Farm mechanization
- Construction of storage facilities
- Major irrigation systems

Tenure may extend beyond 5 years.

---

# PART II – CROP LOAN

---

## 5. Meaning of Crop Loan

Crop loan is a short-term loan granted for raising crops.

Assessment based on:

- Scale of finance
- Area under cultivation
- Cropping pattern

Repayment aligned with harvesting cycle.

---

## 6. Scale of Finance

Scale of finance means:

Estimated cost of cultivation per acre for a particular crop.

Fixed by District Level Technical Committee.

Loan amount = Scale of finance × Area cultivated.

---

## 7. Repayment of Crop Loan

Repayment is linked to:

- Harvest period
- Marketing cycle

Timely repayment may entitle farmer to interest subvention benefits.

---

# PART III – KISAN CREDIT CARD (KCC)

---

## 8. Revised Kisan Credit Card Scheme

KCC provides:

- Flexible credit facility
- Revolving limit
- Simplified documentation

Covers:

- Crop production
- Post-harvest expenses
- Allied activities

---

## 9. Features of KCC

- Validity for multiple years
- ATM-enabled RuPay card
- Working capital for agriculture
- Interest subvention for prompt repayment

Enhances convenience for farmers.

---

# PART IV – ALLIED AGRICULTURAL ACTIVITIES

---

## 10. Selected Activities Under Agricultural Financing

Banks finance:

- Dairy
- Poultry
- Fisheries
- Sericulture
- Beekeeping

These diversify income of farmers.

---

# PART V – GOVERNMENT SUPPORT MECHANISMS

---

## 11. Minimum Support Price (MSP) Scheme

MSP ensures:

Government purchases certain crops at fixed price.

It provides price assurance to farmers.

Banks consider MSP while assessing repayment capacity.

---

## 12. Prime Minister Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

Crop insurance scheme covering:

- Natural calamities
- Pest attacks
- Crop failure

Farmers pay small premium.

Banks often link crop loans with crop insurance.

Insurance reduces credit risk.

---

# PART VI – RISK AND CHALLENGES

---

## 13. Risks in Agricultural Finance

- Monsoon dependence
- Price volatility
- Natural disasters
- Limited financial literacy
- Political loan waivers

Agriculture is inherently risky sector.

---

## 14. Credit Monitoring in Agriculture

Banks must:

- Verify land records
- Monitor end-use
- Ensure crop insurance coverage
- Track repayment after harvest

Monitoring reduces slippage to NPA.

# PART VII – CASE-BASED UNDERSTANDING

---

## 15. Case 1

Farmer takes crop loan but crop fails due to flood.

Insurance under PMFBY may cover loss.

---

## 16. Case 2

Farmer repays crop loan on time.

Eligible for interest subvention benefits.

---

## 17. Case 3

Loan granted without verifying land ownership.

Recovery risk increases.

---

## 18. Case 4

Farmer uses KCC for non-agricultural purpose.

Increases default risk.

---

# PART VIII – IMPORTANT JAIIB POINTS

---

## 19. Key Concepts

- Agricultural loans qualify under PSL.
- Crop loans are short-term loans.
- Scale of finance determines loan amount.
- KCC provides revolving credit.
- PMFBY provides crop insurance coverage.
- MSP supports price stability.
- Agriculture carries high natural risk.

Agricultural finance supports:

- Rural development

Banks must balance:
