# Securitization of Assets

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Securitization

Securitization is:

Process of converting
Illiquid financial assets
Into marketable securities.

Banks pool loans
And sell them to investors.

============================================================

## 2. Why Securitization is Done

- Improve liquidity
- Reduce credit risk exposure
- Improve capital adequacy
- Free up funds for new lending
- Transfer risk

============================================================

## 3. Assets Commonly Securitized

- Home loans
- Auto loans
- Credit card receivables
- Personal loans

Retail loan pools are commonly securitized.

============================================================

## 4. Process of Securitization

Step 1:
Bank pools similar loans.

Step 2:
Loans transferred to
Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV).

Step 3:
SPV issues securities
Backed by loan cash flows.

Step 4:
Investors buy these securities.

Loan repayments collected
And distributed to investors.

============================================================

## 5. Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV)

SPV is:

Separate legal entity
Created to hold assets.

Purpose:

Isolate financial risk.

============================================================

## 6. Types of Securitization

Pass-Through Certificates (PTCs):

Investors receive
Proportionate share of cash flows.

Asset-Backed Securities (ABS):

Backed by loan receivables.

Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS):

Backed by home loans.

============================================================

## 7. Benefits of Securitization

For Banks:

- Improves liquidity
- Reduces risk
- Enhances capital efficiency

For Investors:

- Regular income
- Diversified risk

============================================================

## 8. Risks in Securitization

- Credit risk
- Prepayment risk
- Liquidity risk
- Market risk

Poor loan quality affects investors.

============================================================

## 9. Regulatory Framework

Securitization in India governed by:

- RBI guidelines
- SARFAESI Act provisions

Banks must comply with:

Capital adequacy norms.

============================================================

## 10. Importance in Retail Banking

Retail loan securitization helps:

- Manage asset-liability mismatch
- Maintain liquidity
- Expand retail lending

============================================================

- Meaning of securitization
- Role of SPV
- PTC concept
- ABS vs MBS
- Benefits for banks
- Risk transfer concept

============================================================
