# Recovery of Retail Loans

============================================================

## 1. Repayment in Retail Loans

Retail loans are repaid through:

- EMIs (Equated Monthly Instalments)
- Bullet repayment (in some cases)
- Structured repayment (step-up / step-down)

EMI consists of:

- Principal component
- Interest component

Timely repayment maintains:

Good credit history.

============================================================

## 2. Default in Retail Loans

Default occurs when:

Borrower fails to pay EMI
On due date.

Persistent non-payment leads to:

Account becoming overdue.

============================================================

## 3. Re-Scheduling of Retail Loans

In genuine hardship cases, banks may:

- Extend tenure
- Reduce EMI
- Offer moratorium
- Restructure loan

Restructuring must follow:

RBI guidelines.

============================================================

## 4. Monitoring of Loan Accounts

Banks monitor retail loans through:

- EMI tracking systems
- SMS reminders
- Field visits
- Credit bureau updates
- Early warning signals

Regular monitoring reduces NPAs.

============================================================

## 5. Classification of Irregular Loan Accounts

Accounts are classified as:

SMA (Special Mention Account)
Based on overdue days.

General classification:

0–30 days overdue → Regular reminder  
31–60 days → SMA-1  
61–90 days → SMA-2  
Above 90 days → NPA

After 90 days overdue:

Loan becomes Non-Performing Asset (NPA).

============================================================

## 6. Recovery Policy of Banks

Banks follow structured recovery policy:

- Soft recovery (reminders)
- Tele-calling
- Personal visits
- Legal notice
- SARFAESI action (for secured loans)

Recovery policy must be:

Fair and compliant with regulations.

============================================================

## 7. SARFAESI Act, 2002

Full form:

Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002.

Purpose:

Allows banks to recover secured loans
Without court intervention.

------------------------------------------------------------

Key Features:

- 60-day notice to borrower
- Possession of secured asset
- Sale of asset to recover dues

Applicable only for:

Secured loans.

============================================================

## 8. Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT)

DRT handles:

Cases involving recovery of bank dues.

Used when:

Legal proceedings required.

Faster than civil courts.

============================================================

## 9. Recovery through Lok Adalat

Lok Adalat provides:

Settlement mechanism
For small loan disputes.

Advantages:

- Faster resolution
- Lower legal cost
- Mutually agreed settlement

============================================================

## 10. Engaging Direct Recovery Agents

Banks may appoint:

Recovery agents
For follow-up and collection.

Agents must follow:

Fair practices code.

No harassment allowed.

============================================================

## 11. Importance of Recovery in Retail Banking

- Reduces NPAs
- Protects bank profitability
- Improves asset quality
- Maintains credit discipline

============================================================

- 90 days rule for NPA
- SARFAESI purpose

- Recovery policy steps
- Difference between soft recovery and legal recovery

============================================================
