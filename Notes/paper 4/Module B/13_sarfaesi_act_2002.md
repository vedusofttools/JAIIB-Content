# SARFAESI Act, 2002

============================================================

## 1. Full Form

SARFAESI =

Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002

============================================================

## 2. Purpose of SARFAESI Act

The Act was enacted to:

- Enable banks and financial institutions
- To recover NPAs
- Without intervention of court
- Through enforcement of security

It strengthened recovery mechanism in India.

============================================================

## 3. Applicability

Applicable to:

- Scheduled Commercial Banks
- Financial Institutions
- Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs)

Applicable only for:

Secured loans.

Not applicable for:

- Unsecured loans
- Agricultural land
- Small loans below prescribed limit

============================================================

## 4. When SARFAESI Can Be Invoked

Conditions:

1. Loan classified as NPA.
2. Outstanding amount above prescribed threshold.
3. Secured asset available.

============================================================

## 5. Process under SARFAESI

------------------------------------------------------------

Step 1: 60-Day Notice

Bank issues demand notice
To borrower under Section 13(2).

Borrower given 60 days
To repay dues.

------------------------------------------------------------

Step 2: Enforcement Action (If no payment)

Under Section 13(4), bank may:

- Take possession of secured asset
- Take over management of business
- Appoint manager
- Sell asset to recover dues

============================================================

## 6. Role of District Magistrate (DM)

If resistance occurs,
Bank may approach DM
For assistance in taking possession.

============================================================

## 7. Sale of Secured Asset

Bank may sell asset through:

- Public auction
- Private treaty
- Tender

Proceeds used to:

Adjust outstanding dues.

============================================================

## 8. Borrower’s Rights

Borrower may:

- Make representation against notice
- Approach Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT)
- File appeal

============================================================

## 9. Role of Asset Reconstruction Companies (ARCs)

ARCs:

Purchase NPAs from banks.

Recover dues
Or restructure loans.

Improves bank balance sheet.

============================================================

## 10. Advantages of SARFAESI Act

- Faster recovery
- No court intervention required initially
- Improves asset quality
- Strengthens banking system
- Reduces NPAs

============================================================

## 11. Limitations

- Applicable only to secured loans
- Legal challenges possible
- Social resistance during asset seizure

============================================================

## 12. Importance in Retail Banking

Used in:

- Home loans
- Loan against property
- Secured business loans

Very important recovery tool
For mortgage-backed loans.

============================================================
