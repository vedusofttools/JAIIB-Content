# Credit Scoring and Credit Information

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Credit Score

Credit Score is:

A numerical representation
Of a borrower’s creditworthiness.

It indicates:

Probability of default.

Higher score → Lower credit risk  
Lower score → Higher credit risk

============================================================

## 2. Evolution of Credit Scoring

Earlier:

Loan approval based on judgement and manual assessment.

Now:

Banks use statistical models and credit bureau data
To evaluate borrower risk.

Credit scoring became important due to:

- Growth in retail lending
- Need for faster loan processing
- Risk management requirements

============================================================

## 3. What is a Good Credit Score?

Generally:

750 and above → Excellent  
700–749 → Good  
650–699 → Moderate  
Below 650 → Risky

Banks prefer borrowers with higher credit score.

============================================================

## 4. Credit Scoring Model

Credit score is calculated based on:

- Payment history
- Credit utilisation ratio
- Length of credit history
- Types of credit used
- New credit inquiries

Models are based on:

Statistical and predictive techniques.

============================================================

## 5. Managing the Credit Score

Borrowers can improve score by:

- Paying EMIs on time
- Avoiding excessive loan applications
- Maintaining low credit utilisation
- Not defaulting
- Checking credit report regularly

============================================================

## 6. Positive Side of Credit Score

- Faster loan approval
- Lower interest rate
- Higher loan eligibility
- Better credit limit

============================================================

## 7. Warning Signs in Credit Score

- Frequent late payments
- High credit card utilisation
- Multiple loan inquiries
- Loan settlements
- Write-offs

These reduce score significantly.

============================================================

## 8. Credit Information Companies (CICs) in India

Credit information is maintained by:

- CIBIL
- Experian
- Equifax
- CRIF High Mark

Banks report borrower data to CICs regularly.

============================================================

## 9. Issues in Credit Scoring

- Data inaccuracies
- Identity theft
- Delay in data update
- Technical errors

Borrower can dispute incorrect entries.

============================================================

## 10. Common Mistakes in Credit Scoring

- Not checking credit report
- Ignoring small defaults
- Closing old credit accounts unnecessarily
- Applying for too many loans

============================================================

## 11. Troubleshooting Credit Score Issues

Steps:

1. Obtain credit report
2. Identify errors
3. Raise dispute with credit bureau
4. Inform lender
5. Monitor score regularly

============================================================

## 12. Importance in Retail Banking

Credit scoring helps banks:

- Reduce NPAs
- Speed up loan processing
- Standardise lending decisions
- Improve risk management
