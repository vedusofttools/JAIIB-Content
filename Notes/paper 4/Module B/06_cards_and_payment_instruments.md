# Cards and Payment Instruments

============================================================

## 1. Introduction

Cards are:

Electronic payment instruments
Issued by banks
To facilitate cashless transactions.

Cards improve:

Convenience
Security
Digital payments

============================================================

# 2. Credit Cards

## Meaning

Credit card allows customer to:

Purchase goods/services
On credit
Up to a pre-approved limit.

Bank pays merchant.
Customer repays bank later.

============================================================

## Features

- Credit limit assigned
- Billing cycle (usually monthly)
- Minimum payment option
- Interest charged on unpaid balance
- Reward points

============================================================

## Revenue for Bank

- Interest income
- Annual fees
- Late payment charges
- Merchant discount rate (MDR)

============================================================

## Risks

- Default risk
- Fraud risk
- Over-indebtedness

============================================================

# 3. Charge Cards

Similar to credit card
But:

Full payment must be made
On due date.

No revolving credit facility.

============================================================

# 4. Debit Cards

Linked to:

Savings or Current Account.

Transaction amount:

Directly debited from account.

No credit facility.

============================================================

## Features

- ATM withdrawal
- POS transactions
- Online payments
- Daily withdrawal limits

============================================================

# 5. Prepaid Cards

Pre-loaded with money.

Customer can spend
Only up to loaded amount.

Types:

- Gift cards
- Travel cards
- Forex cards

============================================================

# 6. Co-Branded Cards

Issued jointly by:

Bank + Business partner

Example:

Airline credit card
Retail store credit card

Offer specific benefits.

============================================================

# 7. Contactless Cards

Enable tap-and-pay.

Use NFC technology.

Fast and convenient
For small transactions.

============================================================

# 8. Security Features

- PIN protection
- OTP authentication
- Chip & PIN technology
- Two-factor authentication
- Transaction alerts

============================================================

# 9. Difference Between Credit and Debit Card

Credit Card:

Borrowed money.
Interest applicable.

Debit Card:

Own funds.
No interest.

============================================================

# 10. Importance in Retail Banking

Cards generate:

- Fee income
- Interest income
- Customer loyalty
- Data analytics insights
