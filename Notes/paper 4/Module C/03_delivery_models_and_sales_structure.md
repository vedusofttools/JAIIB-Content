# Delivery Models and Sales Structure in Retail Banking

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Delivery Model

Delivery model refers to:

Organisational structure
And system used by bank
To deliver products and services.

It defines:

- Who sells?
- How it is sold?
- Through which channel?

============================================================

# 2. Types of Delivery Models

------------------------------------------------------------

## A. Branch-Centric Model

Traditional model.

Branch is centre of:

- Customer acquisition
- Deposit mobilisation
- Loan processing
- Relationship management

Staff at branch play major role.

------------------------------------------------------------

## B. Centralised Processing Model

Sales done at branch
But processing done centrally.

Advantages:

- Faster processing
- Standardisation
- Better risk control

------------------------------------------------------------

## C. Hybrid Model

Combination of:

Branch + Centralised processing + Digital channels.

Most banks follow this model.

============================================================

# 3. Internal Customers – Staff at Branch Level

Internal customers include:

- Branch managers
- Relationship officers
- Loan officers
- Customer service staff

Their role:

- Identify leads
- Cross-sell products
- Maintain customer relationships
- Achieve sales targets

Staff performance directly impacts branch profitability.

============================================================

# 4. Dedicated Marketing Managers

Banks appoint:

Specialised marketing managers
For focused product promotion.

Role:

- Promote specific retail products
- Achieve sales targets
- Coordinate with branches
- Manage campaigns

Common in:

Home loans, credit cards, wealth products.

============================================================

# 5. Direct Selling Agents (DSAs)

DSAs are:

External agents
Who source customers for bank.

Commonly used for:

- Personal loans
- Credit cards
- Home loans

------------------------------------------------------------

Advantages:

- Wider reach
- Faster customer acquisition

Risks:

- Mis-selling
- Compliance issues

Banks must monitor DSAs strictly.

============================================================

# 6. Tie-ups with Institutions / OEMs / Dealers

Banks enter partnerships with:

- Automobile dealers
- Real estate developers
- Educational institutions
- Corporate employers

Purpose:

- Generate retail loan leads
- Increase product penetration

Example:

Auto loan through car dealer tie-up.

============================================================

# 7. Importance of Delivery Models

- Improves efficiency
- Reduces cost
- Enhances market reach
- Increases sales volume
- Improves risk management

============================================================

# 8. Challenges in Delivery Models

- Maintaining service quality
- Monitoring external agents
- Ensuring regulatory compliance
- Managing operational cost
