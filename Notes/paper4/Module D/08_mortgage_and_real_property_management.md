# Mortgage and Real Property Management

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Mortgage

Mortgage is:

Transfer of interest
In immovable property
As security for a loan.

Ownership remains with borrower,
But lender gets security interest.

============================================================

## 2. Types of Mortgage

- Simple Mortgage
- Equitable Mortgage (by deposit of title deeds)
- Registered Mortgage
- English Mortgage
- Anomalous Mortgage

In home loans,
Equitable mortgage is common.

============================================================

# 3. Mortgage Advice

Borrowers must evaluate:

- Interest rate (fixed vs floating)
- Tenure
- EMI affordability
- Prepayment conditions
- Loan-to-Value ratio
- Processing charges

Advisory helps avoid over-leverage.

============================================================

# 4. Development of Home Information Packs

Home Information Packs include:

- Property title documents
- Valuation report
- Legal clearance
- Encumbrance certificate

Ensures transparency in property transaction.

============================================================

# 5. Time Value of Money (TVM)

Money today is worth more
Than same amount in future.

Due to:

- Interest earning potential
- Inflation
- Opportunity cost

TVM concept used in:

EMI calculation
Loan valuation

============================================================

# 6. Interest and Annuities

Annuity:

Series of equal payments
Made at regular intervals.

EMI is example of:

Ordinary annuity.

Loan amortisation uses:

Annuity formula.

============================================================

# 7. Loan Amortization Schedule

Amortization schedule shows:

- EMI amount
- Interest component
- Principal component
- Outstanding balance

Interest portion reduces over time,
Principal portion increases.

============================================================

# 8. Capital Gains in Real Property

When property sold:

Capital gain arises.

If held beyond prescribed period:

Long-term capital gain.

Tax applicable as per income tax provisions.

============================================================

# 9. Valuation of Real Property

Valuation is done by:

Approved valuers.

Valuation depends on:

- Location
- Market demand
- Construction quality
- Age of building
- Comparable sales

============================================================

# 10. Land with Building

Value of property includes:

- Land value
- Building value

Land does not depreciate.
Building has limited life.

============================================================

# 11. Life of Structures

Buildings have:

Estimated useful life.

Affects:

Depreciation and valuation.

============================================================

# 12. Sinking Fund

Sinking fund is:

Fund created
To replace asset
After useful life ends.

Used in property management.

============================================================

# 13. Reverse Mortgage

Reverse mortgage is:

Loan available to senior citizens
Against house property.

Bank pays:

Periodic payments to borrower.

Loan recovered after:

Death or sale of property.

============================================================

# 14. Importance in Wealth Management

Real estate is:

Major wealth component.

Mortgage planning helps:

- Asset creation
- Tax efficiency
- Retirement planning
- Liquidity management
