# Home Loan Appraisal and Documentation

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Lender’s Appraisal Procedure

Lender’s appraisal procedure refers to:

Systematic evaluation
Of borrower and property
Before sanctioning home loan.

Purpose:

- Assess repayment capacity
- Verify property title
- Reduce credit risk

============================================================

# 2. Application Form

Borrower submits:

- Loan application form
- Personal details
- Employment details
- Income details
- Property details

Application must be complete and signed.

============================================================

# 3. Documents Required for Home Loan

------------------------------------------------------------

## Identity & Address Proof

- PAN
- Aadhaar
- Passport

------------------------------------------------------------

## Income Proof

For salaried:

- Salary slips
- Form 16
- Bank statements

For self-employed:

- ITR
- Balance sheet
- Profit & Loss statement

------------------------------------------------------------

## Property Documents

- Sale agreement
- Title deed
- Approved building plan
- Encumbrance certificate

============================================================

# 4. Application Received through Agent

If loan sourced through DSA or agent:

Bank must:

- Verify authenticity
- Prevent mis-selling
- Conduct independent appraisal

============================================================

# 5. Appraisal of Loan Request

Appraisal includes:

A. Financial Appraisal  
(Income, repayment capacity, debt-to-income ratio)

B. Legal Appraisal  
(Clear title, ownership verification)

C. Technical Appraisal  
(Property valuation, construction quality)

============================================================

# 6. Other Relevant Issues at Pre-Sanction Stage

- Credit score verification
- Existing liabilities check
- Employment stability
- Age and repayment tenure
- Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio

============================================================

# 7. Documentation

After sanction:

Borrower signs:

- Loan agreement
- Mortgage deed
- Hypothecation documents (if applicable)
- ECS/NACH mandate

============================================================

# 8. Mortgage

Mortgage is:

Transfer of interest in property
As security for loan.

Types:

- Simple mortgage
- Equitable mortgage
- Registered mortgage

============================================================

# 9. Registration of Documents

Certain documents must be:

Registered with Sub-Registrar office.

Ensures:

Legal enforceability.

============================================================

# 10. Detection of Forged or Fabricated Title Deeds

Banks must:

- Verify chain of title
- Conduct legal opinion
- Check encumbrance records
- Confirm approvals

Prevents fraud.

============================================================

# 11. Other Securities

Additional security may include:

- Co-obligant
- Guarantor
- Insurance cover

============================================================

# 12. Registration of Charges with CERSAI

Banks must register:

Security interest with CERSAI
(Central Registry of Securitisation Asset Reconstruction and Security Interest).

Prevents multiple financing.

============================================================

# 13. Monitoring of Home Loan

After disbursement:

- EMI tracking
- Insurance validity
- Property condition monitoring
- Account review

============================================================

# 14. Closure of Account

On full repayment:

- Issue No Due Certificate
- Return original property documents
- Release mortgage charge

============================================================

# 15. Home Loan Frauds

Common frauds:

- Fake property documents
- Overvaluation
- Identity fraud
- Multiple financing

Strong due diligence required.

============================================================

# 16. Important Exam Focus Areas

- Appraisal stages

- CERSAI registration

============================================================

Appraisal = Financial + Legal + Technical
