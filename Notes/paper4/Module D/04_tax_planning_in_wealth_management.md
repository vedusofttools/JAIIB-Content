# Tax Planning in Wealth Management

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Tax Planning

Tax planning means:

Arranging financial affairs
In a lawful manner
To minimise tax liability.

It is different from:

Tax evasion (illegal).

============================================================

# 2. Classification of Tax Structure in India

Taxes in India are broadly:

A. Direct Taxes  
(Income Tax, Corporate Tax)

B. Indirect Taxes  
(GST, Customs Duty)

In wealth management,
Income Tax is most relevant.

============================================================

# 3. Financial Year (FY), Previous Year (PY) and Assessment Year (AY)

Financial Year (FY):

1 April to 31 March.

Previous Year (PY):

Year in which income is earned.

Assessment Year (AY):

Year in which income of previous year is assessed and taxed.

Example:

Income earned in FY 2024-25  
Assessed in AY 2025-26.

============================================================

# 4. Residential Status for Income Tax

Tax liability depends on:

Residential status.

Categories:

- Resident
- Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident (RNOR)
- Non-Resident (NRI)

Residents taxed on:

Global income.

NRIs taxed only on:

Income earned in India.

============================================================

# 5. Important Terms in Income Tax

- Gross Total Income (GTI)
- Total Income
- Exempt Income
- Deductions
- Taxable Income
- Advance Tax
- TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)

============================================================

# 6. Heads of Income

Total income classified under:

1. Salary
2. Income from House Property
3. Profits and Gains from Business/Profession
4. Capital Gains
5. Income from Other Sources

============================================================

# 7. Old vs New Tax Regime

Old Regime:

- Higher tax rates
- Allows deductions (80C, 80D, etc.)

New Regime:

- Lower tax rates
- Limited deductions allowed

Taxpayer must evaluate
Which regime is beneficial.

============================================================

# 8. Investment Products for Tax Saving

Common tax-saving investments:

- ELSS mutual funds
- PPF
- Life insurance premium
- NPS
- Tax-saving FD

Used mainly under old regime.

============================================================

# 9. Capital Gains Tax

Capital gain arises on:

Sale of capital asset.

Types:

Short-Term Capital Gain (STCG)
Long-Term Capital Gain (LTCG)

Tax rate depends on:

Asset type and holding period.

============================================================

# 10. Estate Planning – Tax Planning through Wills and Trusts

Estate planning ensures:

Efficient wealth transfer
With minimal tax burden.

Tools:

- Will
- Trust
- Gift planning

Helps avoid legal disputes.

============================================================

# 11. Tax Planning Objectives in Wealth Management

- Minimise tax legally
- Maximise post-tax return
- Avoid penalties
- Ensure compliance
- Plan succession

============================================================

# 12. Importance in Wealth Management

Effective tax planning:

- Improves net returns
- Enhances long-term wealth growth
- Aligns with investment strategy
