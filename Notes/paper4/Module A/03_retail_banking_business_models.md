# Retail Banking Business Models

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Business Model in Retail Banking

Business Model refers to:

The way a bank structures, delivers and earns revenue
From retail banking services.

It defines:

- Customer segment
- Distribution channel
- Revenue source
- Cost structure

============================================================

## 2. Traditional Branch-Based Model

This is the conventional model.

Features:

- Physical branches
- Face-to-face interaction
- Manual documentation
- Relationship-based service

Advantages:

- Personalised service
- Trust building

Limitations:

- High operational cost
- Limited geographical reach

============================================================

## 3. Hub-and-Spoke Model

Centralised processing at hub
Branches act as spokes.

Advantages:

- Cost efficiency
- Faster processing
- Standardisation

Used widely in:

Loan processing and operations.

============================================================

## 4. Universal Banking Model

Bank offers:

Retail + Corporate + Investment + Insurance services.

One-stop financial solution.

Advantages:

- Cross-selling opportunities
- Diversified revenue streams

============================================================

## 5. Digital / Online Banking Model

Services delivered through:

- Mobile apps
- Internet banking
- ATMs
- Digital platforms

Features:

- 24×7 access
- Lower cost per transaction
- Faster service

Growing rapidly in India.

============================================================

## 6. Relationship-Based Model

Focus on:

Customer lifetime value.

Relationship managers handle:

High-net-worth individuals (HNI)
Priority banking customers.

============================================================

## 7. Product-Centric Model

Focus on:

Specific retail products.

Examples:

- Housing loan-focused banks
- Auto loan-focused NBFCs

============================================================

## 8. Partnership Model

Banks collaborate with:

- Fintech companies
- Insurance companies
- Payment aggregators

Purpose:

- Expand reach
- Improve technology
- Enhance product offerings

============================================================

## 9. Omni-Channel Model

Customer can access services through:

- Branch
- Mobile
- ATM
- Internet
- Call centre

Seamless integration across channels.

This is the future model.

============================================================

## 10. Revenue Model in Retail Banking

Revenue mainly from:

- Interest income
- Processing fees
- Service charges
- Cross-selling commission
- Penalties and charges

============================================================

## 11. Cost Structure in Retail Banking

Major costs include:

- Branch operations
- Technology infrastructure
- Employee cost
- Marketing expenses
- Compliance cost

============================================================

## 12. Important Exam Focus Areas

- Traditional vs Digital model
- Universal banking concept
- Hub-and-spoke model
- Omni-channel model
- Revenue sources in retail banking

============================================================

Branch model → Physical presence
