# Product Development and Product Management in Retail Banking

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Product Development

Product Development refers to:

Designing and introducing
New banking products
To meet customer needs
And increase bank profitability.

Retail banking products include:

- Deposit products
- Loan products
- Fee-based services
- Digital services

============================================================

# 2. Product Life Cycle (PLC)

Every product passes through stages:

1. Introduction
2. Growth
3. Maturity
4. Decline

------------------------------------------------------------

Introduction:
Low sales, high promotion cost.

Growth:
Increasing demand and profitability.

Maturity:
Stable demand, intense competition.

Decline:
Falling demand.

Banks must innovate during maturity stage.

============================================================

# 3. Product Lines of a Banker

Banks generally offer:

A. Deposit (Liability) Products  
B. Credit (Asset) Products  
C. Fee-Based Products

============================================================

# 4. Deposit Products (Liability Products)

These mobilise funds.

Examples:

- Savings accounts
- Current accounts
- Fixed deposits
- Recurring deposits

Purpose:

Provide low-cost funds to bank.

============================================================

# 5. Credit Products (Asset Products)

These generate interest income.

Examples:

- Home loans
- Personal loans
- Vehicle loans
- Education loans
- MSME loans

Purpose:

Income generation.

============================================================

# 6. Other Products and Services

- Insurance distribution
- Mutual fund distribution
- Demat services
- Wealth management
- Credit cards
- Digital payment services

These generate:

Fee income.

============================================================

# 7. New Product Development (NPD)

Banks develop new products to:

- Meet emerging customer needs
- Compete with fintech
- Improve profitability
- Diversify revenue

============================================================

# 8. Stages in New Product Development

1. Idea generation
2. Screening of ideas
3. Feasibility study
4. Product design
5. Pilot testing
6. Commercial launch
7. Monitoring and feedback

============================================================

# 9. Constraints in New Product Development

- Regulatory restrictions
- Risk management issues
- Technology limitations
- Cost considerations
- Market competition

============================================================

# 10. Product Management

Product Management involves:

- Monitoring performance
- Pricing decisions
- Risk assessment
- Customer feedback analysis
- Product modification

Product manager ensures:

Profitability + compliance.

============================================================

# 11. Product Policy

Product policy defines:

- Product mix
- Pricing strategy
- Risk level
- Target segment
- Marketing approach

Banks must align product policy with:

Overall business strategy.

============================================================

# 12. Importance in Retail Banking

Effective product development ensures:

- Customer satisfaction
- Competitive advantage
- Higher revenue
- Risk diversification

============================================================
