# Company Accounts – Basics

============================================================

## 1. Definition of a Company

A company is:

An artificial legal person
Created under the Companies Act
Having separate legal identity
And perpetual succession.

Important Features:

- Separate legal entity
- Limited liability
- Perpetual existence
- Transferability of shares

============================================================

## 2. Types of Companies

------------------------------------------------------------

### (A) Based on Liability

1. Company Limited by Shares
   Liability limited to unpaid share capital.

2. Company Limited by Guarantee
   Members guarantee a fixed amount.

3. Unlimited Company
   No limit on liability.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (B) Based on Members

1. Private Company
    - Minimum 2 members
    - Restricts transfer of shares
    - Cannot invite public to subscribe

2. Public Company
    - Minimum 7 members
    - Can invite public
    - Shares freely transferable

------------------------------------------------------------

### (C) One Person Company (OPC)

- Single member
- Limited liability
- Suitable for small entrepreneurs

============================================================

## 3. Partnership vs Limited Liability Company

Partnership:

- No separate legal entity
- Unlimited liability
- Maximum partners limited
- Partnership Act governs

Company:

- Separate legal entity
- Limited liability
- Perpetual succession
- Governed by Companies Act

Key Difference:
In company, shareholders’ liability is limited.

============================================================

## 4. Share Capital – Meaning

Share Capital is:

Capital raised by issuing shares
To shareholders.

It represents ownership.

============================================================

## 5. Classes of Share Capital

------------------------------------------------------------

### (1) Equity Share Capital

- Voting rights
- Dividend not fixed
- Residual claim on profits
- Risk-bearing capital

------------------------------------------------------------

### (2) Preference Share Capital

- Fixed dividend
- Priority in dividend payment
- Priority at time of winding up
- Usually no voting rights

Types of Preference Shares:

- Cumulative
- Non-cumulative
- Redeemable
- Convertible
- Participating

============================================================

## 6. Components of Share Capital

------------------------------------------------------------

Authorised Capital:
Maximum capital company can issue.

Issued Capital:
Portion offered to public.

Subscribed Capital:
Portion accepted by investors.

Called-up Capital:
Amount demanded from shareholders.

Paid-up Capital:
Amount actually paid.

============================================================

## 7. Issue of Shares

Shares may be issued:

- At Par (face value)
- At Premium (above face value)
- At Discount (restricted by law)

------------------------------------------------------------

### Issue at Par Example

Face value ₹10
Issued at ₹10

Bank A/c Dr  
To Share Capital A/c

------------------------------------------------------------

### Issue at Premium Example

Face value ₹10
Issued at ₹12

Bank A/c Dr 12  
To Share Capital A/c 10  
To Securities Premium A/c 2

Premium is shown under Reserves.

============================================================

## 8. General Illustration

Company issues 10,000 shares of ₹10 each at ₹12.

Total collection = ₹1,20,000

Entry:

Bank A/c Dr 1,20,000  
To Share Capital A/c 1,00,000  
To Securities Premium A/c 20,000

============================================================

## 9. Non-Voting Shares

Non-voting shares:

- Do not carry voting rights
- May carry higher dividend
- Used to raise capital without diluting control

Usually preference shares fall in this category.
