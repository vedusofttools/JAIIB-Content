# Company Accounts – Form of Balance Sheet & Impact of Ind AS

============================================================

## 1. Form of Balance Sheet (Company Format)

Companies prepare Balance Sheet as per:

Companies Act (Schedule III format).

It is presented in vertical format.

Main Heads:

1. Equity and Liabilities
2. Assets

============================================================

## 2. Equity and Liabilities (Structure)

------------------------------------------------------------

### (A) Shareholders’ Funds

- Share Capital
- Reserves & Surplus
- Other Comprehensive Income

------------------------------------------------------------

### (B) Non-Current Liabilities

- Long-term borrowings
- Deferred tax liabilities
- Long-term provisions

------------------------------------------------------------

### (C) Current Liabilities

- Short-term borrowings
- Trade payables
- Outstanding expenses
- Short-term provisions

============================================================

## 3. Assets (Structure)

------------------------------------------------------------

### (A) Non-Current Assets

- Property, Plant & Equipment
- Intangible assets
- Long-term investments
- Deferred tax assets

------------------------------------------------------------

### (B) Current Assets

- Inventory
- Trade receivables
- Cash and bank balance
- Short-term loans & advances

============================================================

## 4. Important Classification Concept

Non-current = More than 12 months  
Current = Within 12 months

This classification is very important for exam questions.

============================================================

## 5. Impact of Ind AS on Financial Statements

Ind AS = Indian Accounting Standards  
Aligned with IFRS.

Ind AS brought major changes:

------------------------------------------------------------

### (1) Fair Value Measurement

Earlier:
Historical cost basis.

Now:
Many assets measured at fair value.

Example:
Financial instruments marked to market.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (2) Revenue Recognition

More structured guidelines.
Revenue recognised based on performance obligations.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (3) Financial Instruments Classification

Assets classified as:

- Amortised cost
- Fair value through P&L (FVTPL)
- Fair value through OCI (FVOCI)

------------------------------------------------------------

### (4) Lease Accounting

Under Ind AS:

Most leases are capitalised.
Lease liability and right-of-use asset recognised.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (5) Expected Credit Loss (ECL) Model

Provision for doubtful debts now based on:

Expected future losses
(Not only incurred losses)

Very important for banks.

============================================================

## 6. Presentation Differences Under Ind AS

- More disclosures required
- Detailed notes to accounts
- Other Comprehensive Income (OCI) shown separately
- Emphasis on transparency

============================================================

## 7. Profit & Loss Statement Under Ind AS

Now called:

Statement of Profit and Loss.

Includes:

- Revenue
- Expenses
- Finance costs
- Tax
- Profit after tax
- Other Comprehensive Income

============================================================

## 8. Importance for Banking Companies

Ind AS affects banks in:

- Loan provisioning (ECL model)
- Investment valuation
- Derivatives accounting
- Fair value measurement

Basel norms and Ind AS interact in reporting.

============================================================

## 9. Common Exam Focus Areas

- Schedule III format
- Current vs Non-current classification
- Fair value concept
- Expected Credit Loss model
- Ind AS vs traditional accounting

============================================================

Quick Recall:

Vertical format  
Equity & Liabilities vs Assets  
Current = within 12 months  
Fair value under Ind AS
