# Equipment Leasing / Lease Financing

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Lease

A Lease is:

A contractual agreement
Where one party (Lessor)
Allows another party (Lessee)
To use an asset
For a specified period
In return for periodic payments.

Ownership remains with lessor.
Right to use is given to lessee.

============================================================

## 2. Parties to a Lease

Lessor → Owner of asset  
Lessee → User of asset

============================================================

## 3. Features of a Lease

- Asset ownership remains with lessor
- Lessee pays periodic lease rentals
- Fixed lease term
- Legal agreement between parties
- May include maintenance clause

============================================================

## 4. Types of Leases

============================================================

### (1) Finance Lease

- Long-term
- Non-cancellable
- Transfers substantially all risks and rewards
- Lessee bears maintenance cost

Similar to purchase through borrowing.

============================================================

### (2) Operating Lease

- Short-term
- Cancellable
- Ownership risk remains with lessor
- Maintenance usually by lessor

Suitable for temporary use.

============================================================

### (3) Sale and Leaseback

Owner sells asset
Then leases it back.

Used to release funds.

============================================================

## 5. Rationale for Leasing

Reasons:

- 100% financing possible
- Preserves working capital
- Tax benefits
- Flexibility
- Avoids obsolescence risk

============================================================

## 6. Contents of Lease Agreement

- Lease period
- Lease rentals
- Maintenance responsibility
- Termination clause
- Insurance clause
- Option to purchase (if any)

============================================================

## 7. Accounting Treatment (Basic Idea)

============================================================

### In Books of Lessee

Finance Lease:

Asset recorded
Lease liability recorded

Operating Lease:

Lease rent treated as expense.

============================================================

### In Books of Lessor

Finance Lease:

Records lease receivable.

Operating Lease:

Asset remains in books
Depreciation charged.

============================================================

## 8. Leasing as Financing Decision

Leasing vs Buying decision depends on:

- Cost comparison
- Tax impact
- Cash flow impact
- Balance sheet impact

Often analysed using:

Present Value comparison.

============================================================

## 9. Advantages of Leasing

- No large upfront investment
- Tax shield on lease rental
- Flexibility
- Off-balance sheet (earlier concept)

============================================================

## 10. Disadvantages of Leasing

- Higher long-term cost
- No ownership
- Contractual rigidity
- Loss of residual value benefit

============================================================

## 11. Legal Aspects of Leasing

- Governed by contract law
- Stamp duty applicable
- Proper documentation required
- Asset ownership rights defined clearly

============================================================

## 12. Important Exam Focus Areas

- Finance vs Operating lease difference
- Ownership vs usage
- Sale and leaseback concept
- Leasing as financing decision
- Basic accounting treatment

============================================================

# Quick Recall

Finance lease → Long-term, asset recognised  
Operating lease → Short-term, rent expense

Lease = Use without ownership
