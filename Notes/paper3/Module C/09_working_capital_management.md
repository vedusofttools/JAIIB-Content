# Working Capital Management

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Working Capital

Working Capital = Current Assets – Current Liabilities

It represents:

Short-term financial strength of a business.

Working capital is required for:

- Day-to-day operations
- Payment of salaries
- Purchase of raw materials
- Utility expenses
- Inventory holding

============================================================

## 2. Types of Working Capital

------------------------------------------------------------

### (1) Gross Working Capital

Total Current Assets.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (2) Net Working Capital

Current Assets – Current Liabilities.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (3) Permanent Working Capital

Minimum level of working capital required continuously.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (4) Temporary Working Capital

Extra working capital required during seasonal demand.

============================================================

## 3. Working Capital Cycle (Operating Cycle)

Working Capital Cycle shows:

Time taken to convert raw materials into cash.

Cycle:

Cash → Raw Materials → Work in Progress → Finished Goods → Debtors → Cash

Longer cycle → Higher working capital requirement  
Shorter cycle → Better efficiency

============================================================

## 4. Components of Working Capital

- Cash
- Marketable Securities
- Inventory
- Debtors
- Accruals
- Trade Credit

============================================================

## 5. Cash and Marketable Securities

Cash required for:

- Daily transactions
- Precautionary motive
- Speculative motive

Marketable securities:

- Short-term investments
- Easily convertible into cash

============================================================

## 6. Accruals

Accruals are:

Expenses incurred but not yet paid.

Example:

- Outstanding wages
- Outstanding rent

Accruals are spontaneous source of finance.

============================================================

## 7. Trade Credit

Trade credit means:

Credit given by suppliers.

It is a short-term source of finance.

Increases current liabilities.

============================================================

# 8. Working Capital Finance by Commercial Banks

Banks provide:

- Cash Credit
- Overdraft
- Bill Discounting
- Short-term loans

============================================================

## 9. Cash Budget Method of Lending

Bank analyses:

Expected cash inflows
Expected cash outflows

Loan sanctioned based on projected cash gap.

============================================================

## 10. Regulation of Bank Finance

Earlier RBI followed:

Maximum Permissible Bank Finance (MPBF) method.

Working capital assessed based on:

Inventory and receivables levels.

============================================================

## 11. Other Sources of Working Capital

- Public Deposits
- Inter-Corporate Deposits
- Commercial Paper
- Factoring
- Forfaiting

============================================================

## 12. Commercial Paper (CP)

Short-term unsecured promissory note.

Issued by:

Creditworthy companies.

Maturity:

Usually up to 1 year.

============================================================

## 13. Factoring

Factoring means:

Selling receivables to a factor
For immediate cash.

Factor provides:

- Financing
- Collection services
- Credit protection

============================================================

## 14. Forfaiting

Used mainly in:

International trade.

Exporter sells receivables
To forfaiter without recourse.

============================================================

## 15. Importance of Working Capital Management

Poor working capital management may lead to:

- Liquidity crisis
- Insolvency
- Loss of creditworthiness

Efficient management ensures:

- Smooth operations
- Better profitability
- Lower borrowing cost
