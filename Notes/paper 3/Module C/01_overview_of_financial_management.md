# Overview of Financial Management

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Financial Management

Financial Management refers to:

Planning, organising, directing and controlling
Financial activities of a firm.

It involves:

- Procurement of funds
- Allocation of funds
- Utilisation of funds

Main question it answers:

Where to get money?
Where to invest money?
How to manage money efficiently?

============================================================

## 2. Forms of Business Organisation

------------------------------------------------------------

### Sole Proprietorship
- Single owner
- Unlimited liability
- Simple structure

### Partnership
- Two or more partners
- Shared profits
- Unlimited liability (generally)

### Company
- Separate legal entity
- Limited liability
- Perpetual existence

Financial management becomes more complex in companies.

============================================================

## 3. Financial Decision Making in a Firm

There are three major decisions:

------------------------------------------------------------

### (1) Investment Decision (Capital Budgeting)

Where should the firm invest?

Examples:
- Purchase of machinery
- Expansion project
- New branch opening

This decision determines asset structure.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (2) Financing Decision

How should the firm raise funds?

Options:
- Equity
- Debt
- Preference shares

This determines capital structure.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (3) Dividend Decision

How much profit to distribute?
How much to retain?

This affects shareholder satisfaction and growth.

============================================================

## 4. Objectives of Financial Management

Primary objective:

Maximisation of shareholder wealth.

Earlier view:
Profit maximisation.

Modern view:
Wealth maximisation.

Why wealth maximisation?

Because it considers:
- Time value of money
- Risk
- Future cash flows

============================================================

## 5. Fundamental Principles of Finance

------------------------------------------------------------

### Risk-Return Trade-off

Higher risk → Higher expected return  
Lower risk → Lower expected return

Investors demand compensation for risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Time Value of Money

Money today is worth more than money tomorrow.

Reason:
- Earning capacity
- Inflation
- Risk

------------------------------------------------------------

### Diversification Principle

Do not put all investments in one asset.

Diversification reduces risk.

============================================================

## 7. Agency Problem in Financial Management

Agency problem arises when:

Owners (shareholders)
And
Managers (agents)
Have different interests.

Managers may:
- Focus on personal benefits
- Avoid risky but profitable projects

Solution:
- Performance-based incentives
- Corporate governance
- Monitoring mechanisms

============================================================

## 8. Business Ethics and Social Responsibility

Financial decisions must consider:

- Legal compliance
- Fair treatment of stakeholders
- Environmental responsibility
- Transparency

Unethical decisions may:
- Damage reputation
- Reduce shareholder value

============================================================

## 9. Organisation of Finance Function

Finance function includes:

- Treasurer

- Financial analyst

Activities:

- Capital budgeting
- Fund raising
- Budgeting
- Financial reporting
- Risk management

============================================================

## 10. Relationship of Finance with Other Disciplines

Finance & Economics:

- Demand and supply
- Market structure
- Interest rates
- Inflation

Finance & Accounting:

- Accounting provides data
- Finance uses data for decision making

Accounting = Recording past  
Finance = Planning future

============================================================

- Handle foreign exchange exposure

Financial manager now plays strategic role.
