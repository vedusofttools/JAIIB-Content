# Derivatives

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Derivatives

A Derivative is:

A financial instrument
Whose value is derived
From an underlying asset.

Underlying asset can be:

- Equity shares
- Bonds
- Interest rates
- Currencies
- Commodities

============================================================

## 2. Characteristics of Derivatives

- Value depends on underlying asset
- Used for hedging risk
- Can be exchange traded or OTC
- May require margin
- High leverage possible

============================================================

## 3. Functions of Derivatives

- Risk management (Hedging)
- Speculation
- Arbitrage
- Price discovery

============================================================

## 4. Users of Derivatives

- Hedgers → Reduce risk
- Speculators → Take risk for profit
- Arbitrageurs → Exploit price differences
- Financial institutions → Risk management

============================================================

# 5. Types of Derivatives

============================================================

## (1) Forward Contract

An agreement:

To buy or sell asset
At future date
At predetermined price.

Features:

- Customised
- OTC (Over the Counter)
- Counterparty risk exists

Example:
Exporter locks exchange rate today for future payment.

============================================================

## (2) Futures Contract

Standardised forward contract
Traded on exchange.

Features:

- Standard contract size
- Daily settlement (mark-to-market)
- Lower default risk

Used for:

Currency hedging
Commodity trading
Stock index hedging

============================================================

## (3) Forward Rate Agreement (FRA)

Contract between two parties:

To fix interest rate
For future borrowing or lending.

Used to hedge:

Interest rate risk.

============================================================

## (4) Swaps

Swap means:

Exchange of cash flows between two parties.

Common types:

Interest Rate Swap:
Fixed rate exchanged for floating rate.

Currency Swap:
Exchange of principal and interest in different currencies.

============================================================

## (5) Options

Option gives:

Right but not obligation
To buy or sell asset
At predetermined price.

Two types:

Call Option → Right to buy  
Put Option → Right to sell

Important Terms:

Strike Price  
Premium  
Expiry Date

============================================================

## 6. Hedging Concept

Hedging means:

Taking opposite position
To reduce risk.

Example:

Importer expecting dollar payment
Buys dollar forward to avoid depreciation risk.

============================================================

## 7. Derivatives in Banking

Banks use derivatives for:

- Interest rate risk management
- Forex risk management
- Treasury operations
- Asset-liability management

============================================================

## 8. Risks in Derivatives

- Market risk
- Credit risk
- Liquidity risk
- Operational risk

High leverage increases risk.

============================================================

## 9. Important Exam Focus Areas

- Forward vs Futures difference
- Option: right not obligation
- Call vs Put
- Hedging meaning
- FRA concept
- Interest rate swap

============================================================
