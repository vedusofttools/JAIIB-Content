# Income Tax, TDS & Deferred Tax – Basics

============================================================

## 1. Overview of Income Tax Act

Income Tax in India is governed by:

Income Tax Act, 1961

Income Tax is:

A direct tax
Levied on income of individuals and entities.

Charged for:

Each previous year
Assessed in the assessment year.

------------------------------------------------------------

Previous Year:
Year in which income is earned.

Assessment Year:
Year in which income is assessed and taxed.

============================================================

## 2. Basic Heads of Income

Income classified under 5 heads:

1. Salary
2. House Property
3. Business or Profession
4. Capital Gains
5. Other Sources

Total Income = Income from all heads – Deductions

============================================================

## 3. Deductions under Chapter VI-A (Important Sections)

------------------------------------------------------------

### Section 80QQB

Deduction for royalty income of authors.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Section 80RRB

Deduction for royalty income of patent holders.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Section 80TTA

Deduction up to ₹10,000
For interest on savings account
(Non-senior citizens).

------------------------------------------------------------

### Section 80U

Deduction for persons with disability.

Fixed deduction based on disability level.

============================================================

## 4. Tax Deducted at Source (TDS)

TDS means:

Tax deducted at the time of payment.

Collected by:
Payer

Deposited with:
Government

------------------------------------------------------------

Purpose:

- Ensure regular tax collection
- Reduce tax evasion
- Spread tax burden

------------------------------------------------------------

Examples:

- Salary
- Interest on FD
- Commission
- Contractor payments

============================================================

## 5. Tax Collected at Source (TCS)

TCS is:

Tax collected by seller
From buyer
At time of sale of specified goods.

Example:

- Sale of scrap
- Sale of motor vehicles (above specified limit)

============================================================

## 6. Filing of Returns

Every eligible taxpayer must file:

Income Tax Return (ITR)

Return includes:

- Total income
- Tax payable
- TDS deducted
- Refund claim

============================================================

## 7. Refund

Refund arises when:

Tax paid > Tax liability

Excess tax is refunded by government.

============================================================

## 8. Recovery of Tax

If tax not paid:

Government can:

- Impose penalty
- Charge interest
- Attach property
- Initiate legal proceedings

============================================================

## 9. Deferred Tax (Basic Concept)

Deferred Tax arises due to:

Timing differences
Between accounting income
And taxable income.

------------------------------------------------------------

Deferred Tax Liability (DTL):

Tax payable in future.

------------------------------------------------------------

Deferred Tax Asset (DTA):

Tax benefit receivable in future.

Common reason:

Different depreciation rates
Under Companies Act and Income Tax Act.

============================================================

## 10. Importance in Banking

Banks must:

- Deduct TDS on interest payments
- File TDS returns
- Recognise deferred tax in financial statements

Non-compliance leads to penalties.
