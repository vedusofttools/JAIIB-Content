# Standard Costing & Variance Analysis

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Standard Costing

Standard Costing is:

A technique of cost control
Where predetermined standards are set
And actual costs are compared with standards.

Difference between standard and actual is called:

Variance.

============================================================

## 2. Objectives of Standard Costing

- Cost control
- Performance measurement
- Efficiency improvement
- Budgetary control
- Management reporting

============================================================

## 3. Types of Standards

------------------------------------------------------------

Ideal Standard:
Based on perfect conditions (no loss).

Practical Standard:
Based on normal operating conditions.

Basic Standard:
Long-term standard.

Current Standard:
Short-term realistic standard.

============================================================

## 4. Installation of Standard Costing System

Steps:

1. Set standards for:
    - Material
    - Labour
    - Overheads

2. Record actual cost.

3. Compare actual with standard.

4. Analyse variances.

============================================================

# 5. Variance Analysis

Variance = Standard Cost – Actual Cost

Favourable Variance (F):
Actual cost < Standard cost.

Adverse Variance (A):
Actual cost > Standard cost.

============================================================

# MATERIAL VARIANCES

============================================================

## (1) Material Cost Variance (MCV)

MCV = (Standard Price × Standard Quantity)
– (Actual Price × Actual Quantity)

OR

MCV = Material Price Variance + Material Usage Variance

============================================================

## (2) Material Price Variance (MPV)

MPV = Actual Quantity × (Standard Price – Actual Price)

If Actual Price < Standard Price → Favourable.

============================================================

## (3) Material Usage Variance (MUV)

MUV = Standard Price × (Standard Quantity – Actual Quantity)

============================================================

# LABOUR VARIANCES

============================================================

## (1) Labour Cost Variance (LCV)

LCV = (Standard Rate × Standard Hours)
– (Actual Rate × Actual Hours)

OR

LCV = Labour Rate Variance + Labour Efficiency Variance

============================================================

## (2) Labour Rate Variance (LRV)

LRV = Actual Hours × (Standard Rate – Actual Rate)

============================================================

## (3) Labour Efficiency Variance (LEV)

LEV = Standard Rate × (Standard Hours – Actual Hours)

============================================================

# OVERHEAD VARIANCES

============================================================

Overheads divided into:

- Variable Overheads
- Fixed Overheads

------------------------------------------------------------

Variable Overhead Variance:

Difference between standard variable overhead
And actual variable overhead.

------------------------------------------------------------

Fixed Overhead Variance:

Difference between standard fixed overhead
And actual fixed overhead.

============================================================

## 6. Accounting Treatment of Variances

Variances may be:

- Transferred to Costing P&L Account
- Adjusted in stock valuation

============================================================

## 7. Benchmarking

Benchmarking means:

Comparing performance
With best industry standards.

Helps in setting realistic standards.

============================================================

## 8. Variance Reporting

Variance reports are submitted to:

Management.

Purpose:

- Identify inefficiencies
- Take corrective action
- Improve performance

============================================================

## 9. Importance of Standard Costing

- Identifies cost deviations
- Improves operational efficiency
- Supports budget control
- Assists performance evaluation

============================================================

# Quick Formula Sheet

MCV = SP×SQ – AP×AQ  
MPV = AQ (SP – AP)  
MUV = SP (SQ – AQ)

LCV = SR×SH – AR×AH  
LRV = AH (SR – AR)  
LEV = SR (SH – AH)
