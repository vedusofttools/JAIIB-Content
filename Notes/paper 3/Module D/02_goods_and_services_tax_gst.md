# Goods and Services Tax (GST)

============================================================

## 1. Meaning of Direct and Indirect Tax

------------------------------------------------------------

Direct Tax:

Tax paid directly by person
On whom it is imposed.

Example:
Income Tax

Burden cannot be shifted.

------------------------------------------------------------

Indirect Tax:

Tax collected by intermediary
And paid to government.

Example:
GST

Burden can be shifted to consumer.

============================================================

## 2. Introduction to GST

GST stands for:

Goods and Services Tax

It is:

A comprehensive indirect tax
Levied on supply of goods and services.

Implemented in India on:

1 July 2017

Replaced:

- VAT
- Service Tax
- Excise Duty
- CST

============================================================

## 3. Objective of GST

- One Nation, One Tax
- Eliminate cascading effect
- Simplify tax structure
- Improve tax compliance
- Promote ease of doing business

============================================================

## 4. Types of GST in India

------------------------------------------------------------

CGST:
Central GST (collected by Central Government)

SGST:
State GST (collected by State Government)

IGST:
Integrated GST (on inter-state supply)

============================================================

## 5. Concept of Supply

GST is levied on:

Supply of goods or services.

Supply includes:

- Sale
- Transfer
- Exchange
- Lease
- Import of services

============================================================

## 6. Input Tax Credit (ITC)

Input Tax Credit means:

Tax paid on purchases
Can be set off against tax payable on sales.

This removes cascading effect.

Example:

Tax paid on raw materials
Deducted from tax collected on finished goods.

============================================================

## 7. GST Registration

Mandatory registration required:

If turnover exceeds prescribed limit.

Registered person receives:

GSTIN (GST Identification Number)

============================================================

## 8. GST Returns

Registered persons must file:

Periodic GST returns
Showing:

- Sales
- Purchases
- Tax payable
- ITC claimed

============================================================

## 9. Composition Scheme

Small taxpayers may opt for:

Composition scheme.

Features:

- Lower tax rate
- Simple compliance
- Limited turnover eligibility

============================================================

## 10. Advantages of GST

- Unified tax system
- Transparency
- Reduced tax evasion
- Efficient logistics
- Boost to GDP

============================================================

## 11. Challenges of GST

- Compliance burden
- Technology dependency
- Frequent rate changes

============================================================

## 12. Importance for Banking Sector

Banks must:

- Pay GST on services
- File GST returns
- Handle GST compliance
- Charge GST on fees & commissions

============================================================
