# Final Accounts of Banking Companies

============================================================

## 1. Definition of a Bank

As per Banking Regulation Act, 1949:

Banking means accepting deposits for the purpose of lending or investment,
Repayable on demand or otherwise,
Withdrawable by cheque, draft, order or otherwise.

Main functions:

- Accept deposits
- Grant loans and advances
- Create credit
- Provide payment services

============================================================

## 2. Special Features of Bank Accounting

Bank accounting differs because:

- Deposits are liabilities
- Loans are assets
- Interest is main income
- Heavy regulatory control
- Strict provisioning norms
- RBI reporting mandatory

============================================================

## 3. Legal Requirements as to Accounts and Audit

Banking companies must:

- Prepare Balance Sheet and Profit & Loss Account
- Follow prescribed format
- Get accounts audited
- Submit returns to RBI
- Follow Banking Regulation Act

============================================================

## 4. Principal Books of Account in Banks

Banks maintain:

- Cash Book
- General Ledger
- Customer Ledger
- Loan Ledger
- Investment Register
- Day Books
- Subsidiary Books

All entries are processed through Core Banking System (CBS).

============================================================

# 5. Form of Balance Sheet of a Bank

Bank Balance Sheet has special format.

Main Heads:

------------------------------------------------------------

### A. Capital and Liabilities

1. Capital
2. Reserves and Surplus
3. Deposits
4. Borrowings
5. Other Liabilities and Provisions

------------------------------------------------------------

### B. Assets

1. Cash and balances with RBI
2. Balances with banks and money at call
3. Investments
4. Advances
5. Fixed Assets
6. Other Assets

============================================================

## 6. Important Balance Sheet Items (Exam Focus)

------------------------------------------------------------

### Deposits (Liability)

Types:

- Demand deposits
- Savings deposits
- Term deposits

Deposits form major liability of bank.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Advances (Asset)

Includes:

- Cash credit
- Overdraft
- Term loans
- Bills purchased and discounted

Advances generate interest income.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Investments

Banks invest in:

- Government securities
- Bonds
- Treasury bills

Classification:

- HTM
- AFS
- HFT

============================================================

# 7. Profit and Loss Account of Bank

Major Income Items:

- Interest earned
- Other income (commission, exchange, brokerage)

Major Expenses:

- Interest expended
- Operating expenses
- Provisions and contingencies

Net Profit transferred to reserves.

============================================================

# 8. Important P&L Concepts in Banks

------------------------------------------------------------

Interest Income:

Recognised on accrual basis
Except for NPAs.

------------------------------------------------------------

Provisioning:

Banks must create provisions for:

- NPAs
- Standard assets
- Depreciation on investments

Provision reduces profit.

============================================================

# 9. Accounting Treatment of Specific Items

------------------------------------------------------------

### NPA (Non Performing Asset)

Interest on NPA:
Not recognised on accrual basis.
Recognised only when received.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Provision for NPAs

Provision A/c Dr
To Provision for NPA A/c

------------------------------------------------------------

### Rebate on Bills Discounted

Unearned discount must be adjusted at year end.

============================================================

# 10. Disclosure Requirements (Notes to Accounts)

Banks must disclose:

- Capital adequacy ratio
- NPA classification
- Provision details
- Exposure to sensitive sectors
- Segment reporting
- Risk management practices

============================================================

# 11. Basel III Disclosures

Banks must disclose:

- Capital structure
- Tier I and Tier II capital
- Risk-weighted assets
- Capital adequacy ratio (CAR)
- Leverage ratio

Basel norms strengthen banking stability.

============================================================

# 12. Banks Listed on Stock Exchange

Must comply with:

- SEBI regulations
- Additional disclosure norms
- Corporate governance requirements

============================================================

# 13. Implementation of Ind AS in Banks

Ind AS impacts banks in:

- Expected Credit Loss (ECL) model
- Fair value measurement
- Financial instruments classification
- Derivatives accounting

Provisioning now forward-looking.

============================================================

# 14. Key Differences: Bank vs Normal Company

Normal Company:
Inventory is major asset.

Bank:
Advances are major asset.

Normal Company:
Revenue from sales.

Bank:
Revenue from interest.

============================================================

# 15. Common Exam Focus Areas

- Structure of bank balance sheet
- Deposits as liabilities
- Advances as assets
- NPA treatment
- Provisioning concept
- Basel III disclosures
- ECL model

============================================================
