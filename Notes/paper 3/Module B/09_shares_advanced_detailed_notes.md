# SHARES – ADVANCED DETAILED NOTES

============================================================

## 1. Equity Shares – Detailed Understanding

Equity shares represent:

- Ownership in the company
- Residual claim on profits
- Voting rights

Equity shareholders bear:

- Highest risk
- Highest return potential

============================================================

## 2. Issue of Equity Shares

### (A) Issue at Par

Issued at face value.

Example:
Face Value = ₹10  
Issue Price = ₹10

Journal Entry:
Bank A/c Dr  
To Share Capital A/c

------------------------------------------------------------

### (B) Issue at Premium

Issued above face value.

Example:
Face Value = ₹10  
Issue Price = ₹15  
Premium = ₹5

Journal Entry:
Bank A/c Dr 15  
To Share Capital A/c 10  
To Securities Premium A/c 5

Premium is transferred to:

Securities Premium Reserve.

------------------------------------------------------------

### (C) Issue at Discount

Generally not permitted
Except under specific legal provisions.

============================================================

## 3. Bonus Shares – Complete Explanation

Bonus shares are:

Free shares issued
To existing shareholders.

No cash is received.

------------------------------------------------------------

### From Where Are Bonus Shares Issued?

Bonus shares are issued from:

- Free Reserves
- Securities Premium
- Capital Redemption Reserve

This process is called:

Capitalisation of Profits.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Why Issue Bonus Shares?

- Reward shareholders
- Reduce market price per share
- Increase liquidity
- Signal strong financial position

------------------------------------------------------------

### Effect on Balance Sheet

Before Bonus:
High reserves
Lower share capital

After Bonus:
Reserves decrease
Share capital increases

Total net worth remains same.

============================================================

## 4. Rights Shares – Complete Explanation

Rights shares are:

Offered to existing shareholders
In proportion to their holding
At a discounted price.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Purpose of Rights Issue

- Raise fresh capital
- Avoid dilution of control
- Faster than public issue

------------------------------------------------------------

### Example

If company announces 1:2 rights:

For every 2 shares held,
Shareholder can buy 1 additional share.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Pricing

Usually issued at discount to market price.

Example:
Market Price = ₹200  
Rights Issue Price = ₹150

------------------------------------------------------------

### Shareholder Options

- Subscribe
- Renounce (sell entitlement)
- Ignore

------------------------------------------------------------

### Accounting Entry

Bank A/c Dr  
To Share Capital A/c  
To Securities Premium A/c (if any)

Rights issue brings fresh cash.

============================================================

## 5. Bonus vs Rights – Key Difference

Bonus:

- No cash inflow
- Issued from reserves
- Free of cost
- Capitalisation of profits

Rights:

- Fresh cash inflow
- Issued at discount
- Optional
- Capital raising method

============================================================

## 6. Preference Shares – Detailed

Preference shares have:

- Priority in dividend
- Priority in repayment of capital

------------------------------------------------------------

### Types

1. Cumulative  
   Unpaid dividend accumulates.

2. Non-Cumulative  
   Unpaid dividend does not accumulate.

3. Participating  
   Share extra profits.

4. Non-Participating  
   Only fixed dividend.

5. Convertible  
   Can convert to equity.

6. Redeemable  
   Redeemable after specific period.

------------------------------------------------------------

### Redemption of Preference Shares

Can be redeemed from:

- Profits available for dividend
- Fresh issue of shares

If redeemed from profits:

Amount transferred to:

Capital Redemption Reserve.

============================================================

## 7. Sweat Equity Shares

Issued to:

- Directors
- Employees

For:

- Technical know-how
- Intellectual property
- Value addition

May be issued at discount.

============================================================

## 8. Buyback of Shares

Company purchases its own shares.

Allowed from:

- Free reserves
- Securities premium
- Proceeds of fresh issue (not same type)

Purpose:

- Increase EPS
- Support share price
- Return surplus cash

Buyback reduces outstanding shares.

============================================================

## 10. Face Value vs Market Value

Face Value:

Nominal value printed on share.

Market Value:

Price at which share trades on stock exchange.

Market value fluctuates daily.
