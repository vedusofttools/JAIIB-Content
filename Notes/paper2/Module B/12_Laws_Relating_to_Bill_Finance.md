Laws Relating to Bill Finance

MODULE B – FUNCTIONS OF BANKS  
JAIIB – Detailed Notes (Negotiable Instruments + Trade Finance + Legal Position)

---

## 1. Introduction

Bill Finance is a traditional and important form of short-term credit.

Banks provide finance against:

- Bills of Exchange
- Trade Bills
- Supply Bills

Bill finance is governed mainly by:

- Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881
- Indian Contract Act
- RBI guidelines on bill discounting

Understanding legal position of banker in bill transactions is crucial.

---

# PART I – CLASSIFICATION OF BILLS

---

## 2. Meaning of Bill of Exchange

Under Negotiable Instruments Act:

A Bill of Exchange is an instrument in writing containing an unconditional order, signed by the maker, directing a certain person to pay a certain sum of money to a specified person or bearer.

Parties:

- Drawer
- Drawee
- Payee

When drawee accepts, he becomes Acceptor.

---

## 3. Classification of Bills

Bills can be classified as:

### (a) Inland Bill
Drawn and payable in India.

### (b) Foreign Bill
Drawn in one country and payable in another.

---

### (c) Demand Bill
Payable on demand.

### (d) Usance Bill
Payable after specified period.

---

### (e) Trade Bill
Arises from genuine trade transaction.

### (f) Accommodation Bill
Drawn without genuine trade transaction.

Banks prefer trade bills.

---

# PART II – VARIOUS TYPES OF BILL FINANCE

---

## 4. Bill Discounting

Bank purchases bill before maturity at discount.

Bank deducts discount (interest) for unexpired period.

At maturity, bank collects full amount.

This is fund-based facility.

---

## 5. Bill Purchasing

Similar to discounting, but generally refers to immediate purchase of sight bills.

Used in export transactions.

---

## 6. Bills for Collection

Bank acts as agent to collect bill amount.

No immediate fund outflow.

Collection charges may be earned.

---

## 7. Supply Bill Finance

Used when goods supplied to government departments.

Payment usually delayed.

Bank finances supplier against supply bills.

Risk depends on credibility of department.

---

# PART III – LEGAL POSITION OF A BANKER

---

## 8. Banker as Holder in Due Course

If bank acquires bill:

- For value
- In good faith
- Before maturity
- Without notice of defect

Bank becomes Holder in Due Course.

Holder in Due Course gets better title than transferor.

This gives strong legal protection.

---

## 9. Rights of Holder in Due Course

- Can sue all prior parties.
- Protected against certain defects.
- Entitled to full amount.

This strengthens bank’s position in bill discounting.

---

## 10. Liability of Parties

Drawer:
Primarily liable if drawee does not accept or pay.

Acceptor:
Primarily liable after acceptance.

Endorsers:
Liable if bill dishonored and notice given.

Bank must give proper notice of dishonor to preserve rights.

---

## 11. Dishonor of Bill

Bill is dishonored if:

- Not accepted (non-acceptance)
- Not paid at maturity (non-payment)

Bank must:

- Give notice of dishonor
- Protest (in foreign bills where required)

Failure to give notice may discharge prior parties.

---

# PART IV – RBI GUIDELINES ON BILL FINANCE

---

## 12. Preference for Bill Finance

RBI encourages:

- Genuine trade bill finance
- Discourages accommodation bills

Bill finance promotes discipline and liquidity.

---

## 13. Risks in Bill Finance

- Accommodation bills
- Circular trading
- Fake invoices
- Delayed payment
- Counterparty risk

Banks must verify underlying transaction.

---

# PART V – BILL REDISCOUNTING

---

## 14. Bill Rediscounting

Bank may rediscount bill with:

- Other banks
- Financial institutions

Improves liquidity.

Common in inter-bank markets.

---

# PART VI – CASE-BASED UNDERSTANDING

---

## 15. Case 1

Bank discounts genuine trade bill before maturity.

Bank becomes Holder in Due Course and can sue drawer if drawee defaults.

---

## 16. Case 2

Bill obtained through fraud but bank acquired in good faith before maturity.

Bank protected as Holder in Due Course.

---

## 17. Case 3

Bank fails to give notice of dishonor.

Endorser may be discharged.

---

## 18. Case 4

Accommodation bill discounted without verifying trade transaction.

Higher credit risk involved.

---

# PART VII – IMPORTANT JAIIB POINTS

---

## 19. Key Concepts

- Bill defined under Negotiable Instruments Act.
- Trade bills preferred over accommodation bills.
- Discounting is fund-based facility.
- Bank becomes Holder in Due Course if conditions satisfied.
- Notice of dishonor is mandatory.
- Acceptor is primarily liable.
- Drawer and endorsers liable on dishonor.

---

## 20. Conceptual Clarity

Bill finance:

- Supports short-term trade credit
- Enhances liquidity
- Reduces working capital burden

Legal protection depends on:

- Proper endorsement
- Good faith acquisition
- Timely notice of dishonor

Understanding negotiable instruments is critical for trade finance operations.
