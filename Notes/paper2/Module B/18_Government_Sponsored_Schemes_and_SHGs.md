Government Sponsored Schemes & Self-Help Groups (SHGs)

MODULE B – FUNCTIONS OF BANKS  
JAIIB – Detailed Notes (Policy + Inclusion + Operational Perspective)

---

## 1. Introduction

Government Sponsored Schemes (GSS) are credit-linked development programs implemented through banks to promote:

- Financial inclusion
- Self-employment
- Poverty alleviation
- Women empowerment
- Rural and urban livelihood

Banks act as implementing agencies and channel funds to beneficiaries.

This block covers:

- DAY-NRLM
- DAY-NULM
- PMJDY
- MUDRA
- KVIC
- SHG–Bank Linkage Programme

---

# PART I – DAY-NRLM

---

## 2. Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Rural Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NRLM)

Objective:

To reduce rural poverty through self-employment and organization of poor households into Self-Help Groups (SHGs).

Key Features:

- Promotion of SHGs
- Capacity building
- Bank linkage
- Interest subvention support

Banks provide credit to SHGs under this scheme.

---

## 3. Role of Banks under DAY-NRLM

Banks must:

- Open SHG accounts
- Provide credit linkage
- Extend working capital loans
- Monitor repayment

Loans qualify under Priority Sector.

---

# PART II – DAY-NULM

---

## 4. Deendayal Antyodaya Yojana – National Urban Livelihoods Mission (DAY-NULM)

Objective:

To uplift urban poor by promoting self-employment and skill development.

Components:

- Individual micro-enterprise loans
- SHG formation in urban areas
- Skill training

Banks finance eligible urban beneficiaries.

---

# PART III – PRADHAN MANTRI JAN DHAN YOJANA (PMJDY)

---

## 5. Meaning of PMJDY

PMJDY is a national financial inclusion program.

Objective:

Universal access to banking services.

---

## 6. Key Features of PMJDY

- Zero balance savings account
- RuPay debit card
- Overdraft facility (subject to conditions)
- Insurance coverage
- Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT)

Supports formalization of economy.

---

# PART IV – MUDRA LOANS

---

## 7. Meaning of MUDRA

MUDRA stands for:

Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency.

Objective:

To support micro enterprises through refinance support.

---

## 8. Categories of MUDRA Loans

1. Shishu – Smallest loans
2. Kishor – Medium category
3. Tarun – Higher loan limit

Targeted at non-corporate, non-farm small businesses.

---

## 9. Role of Banks in MUDRA

Banks:

- Sanction loans under categories
- Follow eligibility guidelines
- Report data as per regulatory norms

Loans qualify under Priority Sector.

---

# PART V – KHADI AND VILLAGE INDUSTRIES COMMISSION (KVIC)

---

## 10. Role of KVIC

KVIC promotes:

- Rural industries
- Traditional crafts
- Self-employment

Banks finance projects sponsored by KVIC.

# PART VI – SELF-HELP GROUPS (SHGs)

---

## 11. Meaning of SHG

Self-Help Group is a voluntary association of individuals (usually 10–20 members) who:

- Pool savings
- Provide mutual support
- Access bank credit collectively

SHGs promote financial discipline and inclusion.

---

## 12. Need for SHGs

SHGs help:

- Provide collateral-free credit
- Promote women empowerment
- Encourage thrift habits
- Reduce dependence on moneylenders

---

## 13. Formation of SHGs

Steps:

1. Group formation
2. Regular savings
3. Internal lending
4. Grading
5. Bank linkage

Banks assess credit history of group before lending.

---

## 14. SHG–Bank Linkage Programme

Launched to integrate SHGs into formal banking system.

Three models:

1. SHG financed directly by bank
2. SHG financed through NGO
3. SHG promoted by bank itself

Widely successful in rural inclusion.

---

# PART VII – CREDIT FLOW & MONITORING

---

## 15. Credit Assessment in GSS

Banks must assess:

- Eligibility
- Viability of activity
- Repayment capacity
- Scheme guidelines

Though government-backed, loans remain bank credit risk.

---

## 16. Risk Factors

- Political interference
- Loan waiver expectations
- Poor financial literacy
- Monitoring challenges

Proper supervision is necessary.

---

Rural SHG seeks loan under NRLM.

# PART IX – IMPORTANT JAIIB POINTS

---

## 21. Key Concepts

- DAY-NRLM focuses on rural livelihoods.
- DAY-NULM focuses on urban poor.
- PMJDY promotes universal banking access.
- MUDRA supports micro enterprises.
- SHG–Bank linkage strengthens financial inclusion.
- Government schemes often qualify under PSL.
- Banks remain responsible for credit risk.

---

## 22. Conceptual Clarity

Government sponsored schemes:

- Expand banking reach
- Promote inclusive growth
- Support entrepreneurship

However:

They are not grants unless specified.  
Loans must be repaid.

Banks must balance:

- Social mandate
- Prudential credit management
