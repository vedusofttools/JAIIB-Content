Remittances & Ancillary Banking Services

MODULE A – GENERAL BANKING OPERATIONS  
JAIIB – Detailed Notes (Legal + Operational + Practical Approach)

---

## 1. Introduction

Apart from accepting deposits and lending, banks provide various remittance and ancillary services to customers.

These services involve:

- Transfer of funds
- Payment instruments
- Government business handling
- Digital payment systems
- Safe deposit lockers
- Merchant banking services

These services create different legal relationships depending on the nature of transaction.

---

## 2. Remittances – Meaning

Remittance refers to transfer of funds from one place to another.

It may be:

- Within India
- Across countries (covered separately under FEMA)

Remittances may be done through:

- Demand Draft (DD)
- Banker’s Cheque (BC)
- Mail Transfer (MT)
- Telegraphic Transfer (TT)
- NEFT
- RTGS
- Electronic payment systems

---

## 3. Demand Draft (DD)

A Demand Draft is:

A negotiable instrument drawn by one branch of a bank on another branch of the same bank.

Features:

- Issued against payment.
- Safer than cheque (cannot be dishonored for insufficient funds).
- Generally payable on demand.

If draft is lost:

- Bank may issue duplicate against indemnity.

Legal Nature:

Bank is primarily liable to pay draft.

---

## 4. Banker’s Cheque (BC)

A Banker’s Cheque (also called Pay Order):

- Issued by bank on itself.
- Used for local payments.
- Prepaid instrument.

Difference between DD and BC:

- DD usually payable at different branch.
- BC payable within same city.

---

## 5. Mail Transfer (MT) & Telegraphic Transfer (TT)

MT and TT are traditional remittance methods.

Mail Transfer:

- Payment instructions sent through mail.

Telegraphic Transfer:

- Faster transfer via telecommunication system.

In modern banking, these are largely replaced by electronic fund transfers.

---

## 6. NEFT (National Electronic Funds Transfer)

NEFT is electronic fund transfer system.

Features:

- Operates in half-hourly batches.
- No minimum limit.
- Available to individuals, firms, companies.
- Can be initiated through branch or online banking.

Settlement is done through RBI.

NEFT provides nationwide coverage.

---

## 7. RTGS (Real Time Gross Settlement)

RTGS is designed for large-value transactions.

Features:

- Real-time settlement.
- Gross basis (no netting).
- Minimum amount threshold applicable (as per current RBI norms).

Suitable for urgent high-value payments.

Settlement is final and irrevocable.

---

## 8. Electronic Benefit Transfer (EBT)

EBT is used for:

- Government subsidy payments.
- Pension disbursement.
- Welfare scheme payments.

Funds are directly credited into beneficiary accounts.

This supports financial inclusion.

---

## 9. Mobile Banking & Digital Payments

Banks provide:

- UPI services.
- Mobile app-based transfers.
- Internet banking.
- IMPS.
- Debit card payments.

Banks must comply with:

- RBI digital payment security guidelines.
- Two-factor authentication norms.
- Cybersecurity standards.

Failed digital transactions must be reversed within prescribed time.

---

## 10. Safe Deposit Lockers

Locker facility creates lessor–lessee relationship.

Key Points:

- Rent payable.
- Bank must maintain reasonable security.
- Customer responsible for contents.
- Nomination facility available.

Banks must comply with RBI guidelines on locker operations including:

- Locker agreement.
- Inventory in case of break open.
- Compensation in case of negligence.

---

## 11. Portfolio Management Services (PMS)

Banks may offer PMS to:

- High net-worth individuals.
- Institutional investors.

Under PMS:

- Bank manages investment portfolio.
- Acts as agent or manager.
- Subject to SEBI regulations.

Risk disclosure is mandatory.

---

## 12. Merchant Banking

Merchant banking services include:

- Issue management.
- Underwriting.
- Corporate advisory.
- Loan syndication.

Merchant banking activities are regulated by SEBI.

Not all banks are permitted unless authorized.

---

## 13. Government Business

Banks act as agents of RBI for:

- Collection of taxes.
- Payment of pensions.
- Handling government receipts.
- Treasury operations.

Banks must follow strict reconciliation procedures.

Failure may lead to audit observations.

---

## 14. Levying of Service Charges

Banks may levy charges for:

- Remittance services.
- Duplicate instruments.
- Locker rent.
- Account maintenance.

However:

- Charges must be transparent.
- Disclosed in advance.
- As per fair practice code.

RBI emphasizes customer transparency.

---

## 15. Legal Risks in Remittance Services

Risks include:

- Wrong credit.
- Fraudulent transfer.
- Cyber attack.
- Delay in settlement.

Banks must maintain:

- Proper authentication.
- Strong internal controls.
- Reconciliation systems.

If bank is negligent, it may be liable for loss.

---

## 16. Case-Based Understanding

Case 1:

Demand Draft lost before delivery.  
Bank may issue duplicate after indemnity.

Case 2:

RTGS wrongly credited to wrong account due to wrong IFSC entered by customer.  
Liability depends on facts and verification process.

Case 3:

Locker contents stolen due to bank’s failure in maintaining security.  
Bank may be liable for negligence.

Case 4:

NEFT delayed due to system failure.  
Customer may be entitled to compensation as per regulatory norms.

---

## 17. Important JAIIB Points

- DD is drawn by one branch on another.
- Banker’s Cheque payable locally.
- NEFT operates in batches.
- RTGS settles in real time.
- Locker creates lease relationship.
- Service charges must be transparent.
- Digital payments require strong authentication.

---

## 18. Conceptual Clarity

Remittance and ancillary services expand the role of banks beyond deposits and lending.

Each service creates:

- Different legal relationship.
- Different risk exposure.
- Different compliance obligation.

Understanding the operational and legal framework helps in solving scenario-based JAIIB questions effectively.
