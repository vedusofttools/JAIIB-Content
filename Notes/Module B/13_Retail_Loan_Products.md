## 1. Introduction

Retail lending forms a major portion of bank advances.

Unlike corporate loans, retail loans are:

- Standardized
- High volume
- Lower ticket size
- Risk-diversified

This block covers:

- Credit Cards
- Home Loans

Understanding product structure, risk factors, and regulatory aspects is important for JAIIB.

---

# PART I – CREDIT CARDS

---

## 2. Meaning of Credit Card

A credit card is a payment instrument that allows customer to:

- Purchase goods/services
- Withdraw limited cash
- Repay later within billing cycle

It is a revolving credit facility.

---

## 3. Key Features of Credit Cards

- Pre-approved credit limit
- Interest-free period (if paid within due date)
- Minimum amount due concept
- Revolving credit
- Annual fee (may apply)

If full amount not paid, interest is charged on outstanding balance.

---

## 4. Risk in Credit Cards

Risks include:

- Over-spending
- High delinquency
- Fraud
- Identity theft

Banks use:

- Credit scoring
- CIBIL reports
- Income verification

to manage risk.

---

## 5. Interest & Charges

Interest rates are usually higher than term loans.

Charges may include:

- Late payment fee
- Cash withdrawal fee
- Over-limit fee

Transparency in disclosure is mandatory.

---

# PART II – HOME LOANS

---

## 6. Meaning of Home Loan

Home loan is a long-term secured loan granted for:

- Purchase of house
- Construction
- Renovation

Security is mortgage of property.

---

## 7. Features of Home Loan

- Long tenure (often 15–30 years)
- EMI-based repayment
- Floating or fixed interest
- Tax benefits to borrower (as per income tax laws)

Banks assess:

- Income stability
- Loan-to-Value (LTV) ratio
- Property title

---

## 8. Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

LTV = Loan amount / Property value

Higher LTV means higher risk.

Banks generally finance only a percentage of property value.

---

## 9. Risks in Home Loans

- Property overvaluation
- Title defects
- Income instability
- Interest rate fluctuations

Proper legal and technical verification required.

---

# PART III – PERSONAL LOANS

---

## 10. Meaning of Personal Loan

Personal loan is unsecured loan granted for:

- Marriage
- Medical emergency
- Travel
- Personal expenses

No collateral required.

---

## 11. Features of Personal Loan

- Short to medium tenure
- Fixed EMI
- Higher interest rate (due to unsecured nature)
- Based on income and credit score

Risk entirely dependent on borrower’s repayment capacity.

---

## 12. EMI-to-Income Ratio

Banks ensure EMI burden does not exceed safe proportion of income.

Higher debt burden increases default risk.

---

# PART IV – CONSUMER LOANS

---

## 13. Consumer Durable Loans

Loans granted for:

- Appliances
- Electronics
- Furniture

May be structured as:

- EMI scheme
- Zero-cost EMI (interest embedded in pricing)

Security usually hypothecation of asset.

---

## 14. Vehicle Loans

Though often treated separately, vehicle loans are retail secured loans.

Security:

- Hypothecation of vehicle
- Endorsement in Registration Certificate

Vehicle depreciates over time.

---

# PART V – OPERATIONAL & RISK ASPECTS

---

## 15. Credit Scoring in Retail Loans

Banks use:

- CIBIL score
- Credit bureau reports
- Past repayment history
- Employment profile

Automated underwriting systems common in retail credit.

---

## 16. Documentation in Retail Loans

Includes:

- KYC documents
- Income proof
- Bank statements
- Employment proof
- Loan agreement

Home loans require additional:

- Property documents
- Valuation report
- Legal opinion

---

## 17. Fair Practices Code in Retail Lending

Banks must:

- Clearly disclose interest rate
- Provide sanction letter
- Mention prepayment charges
- Avoid unfair practices

Customer protection is emphasized in retail segment.

# PART VI – CASE-BASED UNDERSTANDING

---

## 18. Case 1

Customer pays only minimum amount due on credit card.

Interest charged on remaining balance.

---

## 19. Case 2

Property found to have defective title after home loan disbursement.

Bank’s security becomes weak.

---

## 20. Case 3

Borrower with low CIBIL score applies for personal loan.

Higher interest or rejection likely.

---

## 21. Case 4

Vehicle sold by borrower without bank consent.

Bank may seize vehicle due to hypothecation rights.

# PART VII – IMPORTANT JAIIB POINTS

---

## 22. Key Concepts

- Credit card is revolving credit facility.
- Home loan secured by mortgage.
- Personal loan unsecured.
- LTV ratio measures exposure level.
- Retail loans rely heavily on credit scoring.
- Fair disclosure mandatory.
- Hypothecation common in vehicle loans.

---

## 23. Conceptual Clarity

Retail lending provides:

- Diversified income
- Lower concentration risk
- High operational volume

However:

Unsecured retail loans carry higher default risk.

Proper credit scoring, documentation, and monitoring are essential.

This block connects lending principles with retail credit operations.
