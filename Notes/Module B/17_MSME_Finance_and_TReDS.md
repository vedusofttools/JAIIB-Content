---

## 1. Introduction

MSMEs form the backbone of India’s economy.

They:

- Generate employment
- Promote entrepreneurship
- Contribute significantly to GDP and exports

Banks play a major role in financing MSMEs, and this sector forms an important part of Priority Sector Lending (PSL).

Understanding MSME framework is essential for JAIIB.

---

# PART I – MSMED ACT FRAMEWORK

---

## 2. MSMED Act, 2006

Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Development (MSMED) Act, 2006 provides:

- Legal recognition to MSMEs
- Classification criteria
- Protection against delayed payments
- Policy support measures

The Act provides structured development mechanism.

---

## 3. Classification of MSMEs

Enterprises are classified based on:

- Investment in plant and machinery/equipment
- Annual turnover

Categories:

- Micro
- Small
- Medium

Correct classification is important for PSL eligibility.

---

# PART II – POLICY PACKAGE FOR MSMEs

---

## 4. Credit/Finance Support

Banks provide:

- Working capital finance
- Term loans
- Bill discounting
- Trade finance

MSMEs often face liquidity constraints, so timely credit is critical.

---

## 5. Measures for Promotion and Development

Government promotes MSMEs through:

- Skill development
- Cluster development
- Technology upgradation
- Credit guarantee support

Banks align lending with these initiatives.

---

# PART III – DELAYED PAYMENTS PROTECTION

---

## 6. Delayed Payments under MSMED Act

If buyer delays payment to MSME beyond agreed period:

- Interest becomes payable at prescribed rate.
- MSME can approach Micro and Small Enterprises Facilitation Council (MSEFC).

This protects MSMEs from working capital stress.

---

# PART IV – TRADE RECEIVABLES DISCOUNTING SYSTEM (TReDS)

---

## 7. Meaning of TReDS

TReDS is an electronic platform for:

- Financing trade receivables of MSMEs

MSME uploads invoice.  
Financiers bid to discount invoice.

Improves liquidity.

---

## 8. Benefits of TReDS

- Faster payment cycle
- Reduced dependence on buyer credit
- Transparent pricing
- Improved cash flow

Banks participate as financiers.

---

# PART V – CREDIT GUARANTEE SCHEME

---

## 9. Credit Guarantee Fund Scheme for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE)

CGTMSE provides:

- Collateral-free credit to MSMEs
- Guarantee cover to banks

Encourages banks to lend without collateral.

---

## 10. Guarantee Coverage

In case of default:

Trust covers portion of loss.

Reduces risk for bank.

---

# PART VI – CLUSTER BASED APPROACH

---

## 11. Cluster Financing

Cluster approach means:

Financing a group of MSMEs engaged in similar activities in a geographical area.

Benefits:

- Better monitoring
- Shared infrastructure
- Reduced risk

---

# PART VII – COMMON GUIDELINES FOR MSME LENDING

---

## 12. Working Capital Assessment

Working capital may be assessed based on:

- Turnover method
- Operating cycle

Timely renewal required.

---

## 13. Monitoring of MSME Accounts

Banks must:

- Track sales turnover
- Monitor cash flows
- Identify stress early
- Use SMA classification

Early restructuring may prevent NPA.

---

## 14. Restructuring of MSME Loans

Under stress, MSME loans may be:

- Restructured
- Provided relief measures

Subject to regulatory guidelines.
