# Microfinance System in India

## Meaning of Microfinance
Microfinance refers to the **provision of small-value financial services** to:
- Low-income households
- Poor and vulnerable sections
- Micro and small entrepreneurs

These services include:
- Micro-credit
- Savings
- Insurance
- Remittance facilities

The objective is **financial inclusion and poverty reduction**.

---

## Evolution of Microfinance in India

### Early Phase (Pre-1990s)
- Informal lending dominated by moneylenders
- Limited access of poor to institutional finance
- High interest rates and exploitation

This created the need for **institutional microfinance**.

---

### Formal Microfinance Phase (Post-1990s)
- Focus on organised microfinance
- Involvement of banks, NGOs, and NBFCs
- Support from RBI and NABARD

---

## Grameen Bank Model

### Origin
- Developed in Bangladesh by **Muhammad Yunus**
- Based on group lending without collateral

---

### Key Features
- Small loans to poor households
- Joint liability of group members
- High repayment discipline
- Focus on women borrowers

---

### Relevance in India
- Influenced Indian microfinance models
- Adapted to Indian conditions
- Basis for group-based lending systems

---

## Delivery Models of Microfinance in India

Microfinance in India is delivered mainly through:
- SHG-Bank Linkage Programme
- Microfinance Institutions (MFIs)
- Joint Liability Groups (JLGs)

---

## Self Help Group (SHG) – Bank Linkage Programme

### Launch
- Initiated by **NABARD in 1992**

---

### Structure
- Small groups (10–20 members)
- Mostly women members
- Regular savings
- Internal lending
- Bank credit linkage

---

### Importance
- Largest microfinance programme in the world
- Promotes savings discipline
- Empowers women
- Enhances financial inclusion

---

## Joint Liability Groups (JLGs)

### Meaning
A Joint Liability Group consists of:
- 4 to 10 members
- Jointly responsible for loan repayment

---

### Purpose
- Credit access to individuals without collateral
- Common among farmers and informal workers

---

## Microfinance Institutions (MFIs)

### Meaning
MFIs are institutions that provide microfinance services as their **primary business**.

They include:
- NBFC-MFIs
- NGOs
- Societies
- Trusts

---

## Regulation of Microfinance Institutions

### RBI as Regulator
- RBI regulates **NBFC-MFIs**
- NGOs and societies regulated under respective laws

---

## RBI Directions on Microfinance Loans – 2022

### Effective From
- **April 2022**

---

### Key Provisions
- Removal of interest rate cap
- Emphasis on household income assessment
- Limits on borrower indebtedness
- Flexible repayment structure
- Transparency in pricing

---

### Income Criteria
- Defined household income limits for:
    - Rural borrowers
    - Urban borrowers

---

## Fair Practices Code for NBFC-MFIs

NBFC-MFIs must:
- Disclose loan terms clearly
- Avoid coercive recovery practices
- Ensure transparency in interest rates
- Provide grievance redressal mechanism

---

## Priority Sector Lending to Microfinance

### Inclusion Under PSL
- Bank lending to MFIs is eligible under:
    - Priority Sector Lending
    - Weaker Sections category

---

### Purpose
- Encourage flow of institutional credit
- Reduce dependence on informal lenders

---

## Role of Microfinance in Indian Economy
- Financial inclusion
- Poverty alleviation
- Women empowerment
- Support to self-employment
- Rural and semi-urban development

---

## Issues in Microfinance
- Over-indebtedness
- Multiple borrowing
- Recovery-related concerns
- Sustainability of MFIs

---

## Recent Reforms and Focus
- Harmonised regulatory framework
- Borrower-centric approach
- Emphasis on responsible lending
- Strengthened supervision

---

## Relevance for Banking and JAIIB
- Important for priority sector questions
- RBI 2022 directions frequently tested
- SHG-Bank linkage is a favourite exam area
- Focus on regulation and borrower protection

---

## Key Facts for JAIIB
- SHG-Bank Linkage started in **1992**
- Grameen model inspired Indian MFIs
- RBI microfinance directions issued in **2022**
- NABARD plays key role in SHG programme

---

## Common Exam Traps
- Treating MFIs as banks
- Ignoring RBI 2022 framework
- Confusing SHGs with JLGs
- Assuming interest rate caps still exist
