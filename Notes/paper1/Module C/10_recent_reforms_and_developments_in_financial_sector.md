# Recent Reforms and Developments in the Banking Sector

## Introduction
The Indian banking sector has undergone major reforms in recent years to:
- Improve asset quality
- Strengthen capital base
- Enhance governance
- Support infrastructure financing
- Improve operational efficiency

These reforms aim to ensure **financial stability and sustainable growth**.

---

# Bad Bank Concept

## Meaning of Bad Bank
A Bad Bank is a financial entity created to:
- Take over stressed assets (NPAs)
- Clean up bank balance sheets
- Enable banks to focus on fresh lending

It does not engage in regular banking activities.

---

## National Asset Reconstruction Company Limited (NARCL)

### Announced
- Union Budget **2021-22**

### Structure
- NARCL (Bad Bank) acquires stressed assets
- India Debt Resolution Company Limited (IDRCL) manages resolution

---

## Purpose
- Reduce NPAs of banks
- Improve credit flow
- Strengthen banking system

---

## Significance
- Addresses large corporate stressed assets
- Improves balance sheet health
- Enhances banking efficiency

---

# Infrastructure Financing

## Importance
Infrastructure requires:
- Long-term capital
- Large funding
- Stable financing structure

Commercial banks face:
- Asset-liability mismatch
- Long gestation risk

This creates need for specialised infrastructure financing.

---

# National Bank for Financing Infrastructure and Development (NaBFID)

## Established
- **2021**

---

## Objective
- Provide long-term finance for infrastructure projects
- Reduce burden on commercial banks

---

## Role
- Mobilise capital
- Support PPP projects
- Facilitate infrastructure investment

---

## Significance
- Revives DFI model for infrastructure
- Supports economic growth

---

# EASE Reforms

## Meaning
EASE stands for:
Enhanced Access and Service Excellence.

---

## Introduced
- Launched in **2018**

---

## Objective
- Reform public sector banks
- Improve governance
- Enhance customer service
- Strengthen risk management

---

## Key Areas of EASE

- Responsible banking
- Customer responsiveness
- Financial inclusion
- Digitisation
- Credit monitoring
- Data-driven decision making

---

## Outcomes
- Improved transparency
- Faster loan processing
- Better NPA monitoring
- Digital transformation

---

# Consolidation of Public Sector Banks

## Objective
