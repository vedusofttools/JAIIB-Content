# Banking System in India and Narasimham Reforms

## Banking System in India – Meaning
The banking system in India refers to the **network of banks and institutions** that provide:
- Deposit facilities
- Credit
- Payment services
- Financial intermediation

Banks act as a **link between savers and borrowers** and play a key role in economic development.

---

## Structure of Indian Banking System
The Indian banking system broadly consists of:
- Commercial banks
- Cooperative banks
- Regional Rural Banks
- Development-oriented institutions
- New specialised banks

---

## Background Before Reforms (Pre-1991)

### Key Conditions
- Highly regulated banking environment
- Administered interest rates
- High Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR)
- High Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)
- Directed credit programmes
- Dominance of public sector banks

---

### Major Problems
- Low profitability of banks
- Poor asset quality
- Rising non-performing assets (NPAs)
- Limited competition
- Inefficient credit allocation

These weaknesses became evident by the late 1980s.

---

## Trigger for Banking Reforms

### Balance of Payments Crisis – 1991
- Severe foreign exchange shortage
- Low forex reserves
- High fiscal deficit
- External payment difficulties

This crisis led to **comprehensive economic and banking sector reforms**.

---

## Narasimham Committee on Banking System – 1991

### Background
- Appointed in **1991**
- Chairman: M. Narasimham
- Objective: Improve efficiency, stability, and competitiveness of banking system

---

## Key Recommendations of Narasimham Committee (1991)

### Reduction in Statutory Pre-emptions
- Gradual reduction of:
    - Statutory Liquidity Ratio (SLR)
    - Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR)

Purpose:
- Increase lendable resources
- Improve credit flow to productive sectors

---

### Introduction of Prudential Norms
- Income recognition norms
- Asset classification norms
- Provisioning norms

Purpose:
- Improve transparency
- Reflect true financial position of banks

---

### Capital Adequacy Norms
- Introduction of Capital to Risk-Weighted Assets Ratio (CRAR)
- Minimum requirement: **8%**

Purpose:
- Strengthen bank balance sheets
- Absorb potential losses

---

### Deregulation of Interest Rates
- Phasing out of administered interest rates
- Banks given flexibility to set lending and deposit rates

Purpose:
- Improve efficiency
- Enhance competition

---

### Entry of Private Sector Banks
- Permission for new private sector banks
- Increased competition and innovation

Purpose:
- Improve customer service
- Promote technology adoption

---

### Reduction in Government Control
- Reduced interference in day-to-day banking operations
- Focus on professional management

---

## Banking Sector Reforms (1992–2008)

### Implementation Phase
- Reforms implemented gradually from **1992 onwards**
- Supported by further committee recommendations

---

## Major Reform Measures

### Strengthening Asset Quality
- Improved NPA recognition
- Better recovery mechanisms
- Focus on credit appraisal

---

### Technology Reforms
- Introduction of Core Banking Solutions
- Computerisation of branches
- Electronic payment systems

---

### Risk Management
- Introduction of risk-based supervision
- Better internal controls
- Improved disclosure norms

---

### Consolidation and Competition
- Mergers and restructuring
- Entry of foreign banks
- Increased competition across banking sector

---

## Outcomes of Banking Reforms

### Positive Outcomes
- Improved efficiency and profitability
- Better capital adequacy
- Enhanced transparency
- Improved customer service
- Stronger regulatory framework

---

### Emerging Challenges
- Credit cycles
- Asset quality stress during downturns
- Increased exposure to market risks

---

## Present Status of Indian Banking System

### Key Features
- Dominance of public sector banks continues
- Strong regulatory oversight
- Growing role of private sector banks
- Rapid digital transformation

---

### Current Focus Areas
- Asset quality improvement
- Capital strengthening
- Governance reforms
- Technology and cyber security
- Financial inclusion

---

## Role of Banking System in Economy
- Mobilises savings
- Provides credit to productive sectors
- Supports government borrowing
- Facilitates monetary policy transmission
- Promotes inclusive growth

---

## Key Facts for JAIIB

- Narasimham Committee appointed in **1991**
- CRAR introduced post-reforms
- Interest rate deregulation post-1991
- Private sector banks allowed after reforms

- Assuming reforms ended in 1991
