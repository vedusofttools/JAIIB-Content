# Priority Sector Lending (PSL) & Agriculture / MSME Contribution

## Introduction
Priority Sector Lending (PSL) refers to **mandatory lending by banks to certain identified sectors** of the economy that are crucial for **inclusive growth and employment generation**, but may not receive adequate credit otherwise.

PSL guidelines are issued and monitored by the **:contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}**.

---

## Origin and Background of PSL

## Establishment of PSL
- Priority Sector Lending was **introduced in 1972**
- Objective was to ensure **institutional credit** to neglected sectors
- Initially focused mainly on agriculture

Later, coverage was expanded to include MSMEs, education, housing, etc.

---

## Objectives of Priority Sector Lending
- Promote inclusive growth
- Support agriculture and allied activities
- Encourage MSMEs
- Reduce dependence on moneylenders
- Promote employment generation
- Ensure balanced regional development

---

## Categories under Priority Sector Lending

## Agriculture
Includes:
- Farm credit
- Allied activities (dairy, fisheries, poultry)
- Agriculture infrastructure
- Ancillary activities

## MSME
Includes:
- Micro enterprises
- Small enterprises
- Medium enterprises

## Other Priority Sectors
- Education
- Housing
- Weaker sections
- Renewable energy
- Social infrastructure

---

## PSL Targets and Sub-targets (IMPORTANT)

## Overall Target
- **40% of Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC)**  
  OR
- Credit Equivalent of Off-Balance Sheet Exposure (whichever is higher)

Applicable to:
- Scheduled Commercial Banks

---

## Agriculture Target
- **18% of ANBC**

### Sub-target
- **8% for Small and Marginal Farmers**
- Small farmer: landholding up to 2 hectares

---

## MSME Target
- **7.5% of ANBC** for Micro Enterprises

---

## Weaker Sections Target
- **12% of ANBC**

---

## Priority Sector Lending Certificates (PSLCs)

## Introduction of PSLC
- Introduced in **2016**
- Enables banks to buy and sell PSL achievement

### Objective
- Improve PSL compliance
- Provide flexibility to banks
- Promote efficient credit distribution

---

## Role of Agriculture in Indian Economy

## Importance of Agriculture
- Largest source of employment
- Backbone of rural economy
- Ensures food security
- Supports agro-based industries

Despite declining GDP share, agriculture remains **critical for inclusive growth**.

---

## Contribution of Agriculture to Banking
- Major component of PSL
- Kisan Credit Cards (KCC)
- Crop loans and allied activity loans
- Interest subvention schemes

### Example
Crop loans up to a specified limit attract interest subvention for prompt repayment.

---

## Role of MSMEs in Indian Economy

## Importance of MSMEs
- Generate large-scale employment
- Promote entrepreneurship
- Support exports
- Aid regional development

MSMEs act as a **link between agriculture and large industries**.

---

## MSME Classification (Revised)

Based on **investment and turnover** criteria.

## Micro Enterprises
- Lowest investment and turnover limits

## Small Enterprises
- Moderate investment and turnover

## Medium Enterprises
- Higher investment and turnover

(Revised classification effective from **2020**)

---

## Contribution of MSMEs to Banking
- Major demand for working capital
- Term loans for expansion
- Covered under PSL
- Credit Guarantee schemes reduce risk

---

## Challenges in PSL
- Credit risk in agriculture
- Regional imbalance
- NPAs in MSME sector
- Inadequate financial literacy
