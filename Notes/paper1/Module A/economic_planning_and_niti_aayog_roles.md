# NITI Aayog 

## Introduction
 (National Institution for Transforming India) is the premier **policy think tank of the Government of India**.

It was **established in 2015**, replacing the Planning Commission.

---

## Establishment Details
- Year of establishment: **2015**
- Formed by an executive resolution of the Government of India
- Replaced: Planning Commission (1950)

---

## Nature of NITI Aayog
- Policy think tank
- Advisory body
- Does not prepare Five Year Plans
- Does not allocate funds

---

## Objectives
- Promote cooperative federalism
- Encourage competitive federalism
- Foster innovation and reforms
- Support outcome-based policy making
- Provide strategic policy inputs

---

## Composition
- Chairperson: Prime Minister of India
- Vice-Chairperson
- Full-time Members
- Part-time Members
- Ex-officio Members (Union Ministers)
- Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

---

## Key Functions
- Formulates long-term development strategies
- Provides policy and technical advice
- Monitors implementation of government programs
- Acts as a platform for Centre–State cooperation
- Encourages best practices among states

---

## Difference from Planning Commission
- Planning Commission followed top-down approach
- NITI Aayog follows bottom-up approach
- Planning Commission allocated plan funds
- NITI Aayog has no financial allocation powers
