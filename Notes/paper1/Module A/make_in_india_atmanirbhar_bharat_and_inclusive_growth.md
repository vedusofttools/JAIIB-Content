# Make in India, Atmanirbhar Bharat & Inclusive Growth

## Introduction
India’s recent development strategy focuses on **strengthening domestic manufacturing**, **reducing import dependence**, and **ensuring growth benefits all sections of society**.

Three key policy ideas shape this approach:
- Make in India
- Atmanirbhar Bharat
- Inclusive Growth

These initiatives are closely linked with **industrial policy, MSMEs, employment, exports, and banking credit**.

---

## Make in India

## Meaning
Make in India is a national initiative aimed at **encouraging manufacturing in India** and making the country a **global manufacturing hub**.

## Launch Details (IMPORTANT)
- Launched on: **25 September 2014**
- Launched by: Government of India
- Objective: Increase manufacturing share in GDP and generate employment


::contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}


---

## Objectives of Make in India
- Increase manufacturing sector growth
- Create large-scale employment
- Promote skill development
- Attract foreign direct investment (FDI)
- Improve ease of doing business
- Increase exports

---

## Key Focus Areas
- 25 identified manufacturing sectors
- Simplification of regulations
- Infrastructure development
- Investment facilitation
- Innovation and design

---

## Impact of Make in India
- Improvement in Ease of Doing Business ranking
- Growth in FDI inflows
- Boost to sectors like electronics and automobiles
- Increased focus on domestic production

---

## Atmanirbhar Bharat

## Meaning
Atmanirbhar Bharat means **self-reliant India**, where the economy becomes **resilient, competitive, and self-sustaining**, without being isolated from global trade.

It focuses on **self-reliance through competitiveness**, not protectionism.

---

## Launch Details (IMPORTANT)
- Announced in: **May 2020**
- Announced as part of: COVID-19 economic response
- Atmanirbhar Bharat Package size: **₹20 lakh crore**

---

## Pillars of Atmanirbhar Bharat
- Economy
- Infrastructure
- System (technology-driven)
- Vibrant demography
- Demand

---

## Key Measures under Atmanirbhar Bharat
- Support to MSMEs through collateral-free loans
- Production Linked Incentive (PLI) schemes
- Promotion of local manufacturing
- Reforms in agriculture, labour, and power sector
- Strengthening supply chains

---

## Difference Between Make in India and Atmanirbhar Bharat

## Make in India
- Focus on manufacturing growth
- Attracts domestic and foreign investment
- Launched in 2014

## Atmanirbhar Bharat
- Focus on self-reliance and resilience
- Emphasises domestic capability building
- Launched in 2020

Both initiatives are **complementary**, not contradictory.

---

## Inclusive Growth

## Meaning
Inclusive growth refers to **economic growth that benefits all sections of society**, especially:
- Poor
- Marginalised
- Rural population
- MSMEs
- Weaker sections

The goal is **growth with equity**.

---

## Features of Inclusive Growth
- Reduction in poverty and inequality
- Employment generation
- Financial inclusion
- Access to education and healthcare
- Balanced regional development

---

## Role of Banking in Inclusive Growth
- Priority Sector Lending
- Financial inclusion through bank accounts
- Credit to agriculture and MSMEs
- Support for SHGs and small borrowers
- Digital banking and DBT

Banks act as **key enablers** of inclusive growth.

---

## Link Between Make in India, Atmanirbhar Bharat & Inclusive Growth
- Manufacturing creates jobs
- MSME growth supports income generation
- Reduced import dependence improves stability
- Credit flow ensures participation of weaker sections
- Long-term sustainable development
