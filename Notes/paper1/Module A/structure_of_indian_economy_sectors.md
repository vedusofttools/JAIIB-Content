# Structure of Indian Economy (Sectors & GDP Composition)

## Introduction
The structure of the Indian economy refers to the **composition and contribution of different sectors** to national income (GDP) and employment.

India is a **mixed economy**, where both **public and private sectors** coexist, and economic activities are broadly classified into three main sectors:
- Primary Sector
- Secondary Sector
- Tertiary Sector

Understanding this structure is important for bankers as it explains **credit flow, employment patterns, GDP trends, and policy focus**.

---

## Classification of Sectors in Indian Economy

## Primary Sector
The primary sector involves **extraction and use of natural resources**.

### Activities Included
- Agriculture
- Forestry
- Fishing
- Mining
- Animal husbandry

### Characteristics
- Depends heavily on nature
- Seasonal in nature
- Employs a large portion of population
- Low productivity compared to other sectors

### Example
Crop cultivation, dairy farming, coal mining.

---

## Secondary Sector
The secondary sector involves **processing of raw materials into finished or semi-finished goods**.

### Activities Included
- Manufacturing
- Construction
- Industrial production

### Characteristics
- Uses output of primary sector
- Higher productivity than agriculture
- Capital intensive
- Generates industrial employment

### Example
Textile factories, steel plants, construction of roads and buildings.

---

## Tertiary Sector
The tertiary sector provides **services** that support production and distribution.

### Activities Included
- Banking and insurance
- Transport and communication
- Trade and commerce
- Education and healthcare
- IT and tourism

### Characteristics
- Service-oriented
- Fastest growing sector in India
- Major contributor to GDP
- Less dependent on natural conditions

### Example
Banks providing loans, hospitals, IT services, logistics companies.

## Contribution to GDP
In India:
- Tertiary sector contributes the **largest share to GDP**
- Secondary sector shows steady growth
- Primary sector contributes the **least to GDP** but employs the **maximum workforce**

### Key Insight
There is a **structural imbalance** where:
- Agriculture employs many people
- Services generate most income

---

## Employment Pattern in Indian Economy

- Primary sector employs the largest share of population
- Secondary sector employs fewer people but adds more value
- Tertiary sector employs comparatively fewer people but contributes highest to GDP

This mismatch is known as **jobless growth**, especially in services.

## Role of Agriculture in Indian Economy

- Backbone of rural economy
- Major source of livelihood
- Supplies raw material to industries
- Contributes to food security
- Important for priority sector lending

Despite declining GDP share, agriculture remains **socially and economically critical**.

---

## Role of Industry in Indian Economy

- Promotes economic growth
- Generates employment
- Encourages urbanisation
- Supports exports

Industrial growth is essential for **balanced development**.

---

## Role of Services Sector in Indian Economy

- Largest contributor to GDP
- Drives modern economic growth
- Includes banking, IT, telecom, trade
- Major source of foreign exchange earnings

India is often described as a **service-led economy**.

---

## Structure of Indian Economy and Banking

- Credit demand differs by sector
- Agriculture and MSMEs receive priority lending
- Services sector drives retail banking growth
- Sectoral exposure norms are important for banks

Understanding sector structure helps banks in **risk management and credit planning**.

- Assuming agriculture contributes most to GDP
- Confusing employment share with income share
- Ignoring role of services in modern economy
- Treating industrial growth as uniform across India
