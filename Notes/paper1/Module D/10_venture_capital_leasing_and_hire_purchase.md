# Venture Capital, Leasing and Hire Purchase

# Venture Capital

## Meaning of Venture Capital

Venture Capital (VC) is:

- Long-term risk capital
- Provided to start-ups and growth-stage companies
- In exchange for equity participation

It is used for innovative and high-risk projects.

---

## Evolution of Venture Capital in India

- Concept gained importance in 1980s
- SEBI (Venture Capital Funds) Regulations, 1996
- Growth after liberalisation in 1991
- Now regulated under Alternative Investment Funds (AIF) framework

---

## Characteristics of Venture Capital

- High risk
- High return potential
- Equity participation
- Long investment horizon
- Active involvement in management

---

## Stages of Venture Capital Financing

### Seed Capital
- Early stage idea funding

### Start-up Financing
- Product development stage

### Expansion Financing
- Business growth stage

### Bridge Financing
- Pre-IPO stage funding

---

## Process of Venture Capital Financing

1. Proposal submission
2. Due diligence
3. Investment decision
4. Monitoring and mentoring
5. Exit

---

## Modes of Venture Capital Financing

- Equity shares
- Convertible debentures
- Preference shares

---

## Exit Routes for Venture Capital

- Initial Public Offer (IPO)
- Sale to strategic investor
- Buyback by promoters
- Secondary sale

---

## Advantages

- Provides capital without collateral
- Managerial expertise
- Supports innovation

---

## Disadvantages

- Loss of control
- High cost of capital
- Dilution of ownership

---

# Leasing

## Meaning of Lease Finance

Leasing is a contract where:

- Lessor owns asset
- Lessee uses asset
- Periodic lease rentals are paid

Ownership remains with lessor.

---

## Evolution of Leasing in India

- Started in 1970s
- Expanded after economic reforms
- Popular for equipment financing

---

## Types of Leasing

### Finance Lease
- Long-term
- Non-cancellable
- Lessee bears maintenance cost

---

### Operating Lease
- Short-term
- Cancellable
- Lessor bears maintenance cost

---

### Sale and Lease Back
- Owner sells asset and leases it back

---

## Advantages of Leasing

- No large initial investment
- Tax benefits
- Flexible financing

---

## Disadvantages

- Long-term cost may be higher
- No ownership benefit
- Lease obligations fixed

---

## Impact on Financial Ratios

- Affects debt-equity ratio
- Influences asset turnover
- Impacts profitability ratios

---

# Hire Purchase

## Meaning

Hire Purchase is a contract where:

- Buyer takes possession of asset
- Pays in instalments
- Ownership transfers after final payment

---

## Evolution in India

- Popular for consumer goods and vehicles
- Governed by contract law principles

---

## Legal Aspects

- Ownership remains with seller until full payment
- Seller can repossess asset on default

---

## Parties to Hire Purchase Contract

- Hirer (buyer)
- Owner (seller)
- Sometimes financier

---

# Leasing vs Hire Purchase

Leasing:
- Ownership remains with lessor
- No transfer of ownership
- Lease rentals paid

Hire Purchase:
- Ownership transferred after final instalment
- Instalments include principal and interest
- Buyer becomes owner eventually

---

# Importance in Banking

Banks and NBFCs provide:

- Lease financing
- Hire purchase financing
- Asset-based lending

Supports MSMEs and retail financing.

---

# Relevance for JAIIB

- Venture capital stages common MCQ
- Leasing vs hire purchase difference frequently asked
- Exit routes for VC important
- Finance lease vs operating lease difference

---

# Key Facts

- VC regulated under AIF framework
- Lease does not transfer ownership
- Hire purchase transfers ownership after final payment
- Sale and lease back is common corporate practice

---

# Common Exam Traps

- Confusing lease with hire purchase
- Treating VC as loan
- Assuming ownership transfers in lease
- Ignoring exit routes in VC
