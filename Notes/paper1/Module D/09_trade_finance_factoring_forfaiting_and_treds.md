# Factoring, Forfaiting and Trade Receivables Discounting System (TReDS)

# Factoring

## Meaning of Factoring

Factoring is a financial service in which:

- A business sells its receivables (invoices)
- To a financial institution called a Factor
- In return for immediate cash

It improves working capital.

---

## History of Factoring

- Originated in Europe
- Gained importance in international trade
- Introduced formally in India in 1980s
- Factoring Regulation Act enacted in 2011

---

## Mechanism of Factoring

1. Seller supplies goods to buyer
2. Invoice generated
3. Seller assigns invoice to Factor
4. Factor pays advance (usually 70â€“90%)
5. Factor collects payment from buyer

---

## Types of Factoring

### With Recourse Factoring
- Seller bears default risk

### Without Recourse Factoring
- Factor bears credit risk

### Domestic Factoring
- Buyer and seller in same country

### International Factoring
- Cross-border transactions

---

## Fees Involved in Factoring

- Factoring commission
- Discount charge (interest)
- Service charges

---

## Advantages of Factoring

- Immediate liquidity
- Improved cash flow
- Credit risk management
- Outsourcing of receivables management

---

## Bills Discounting vs Factoring

Bills Discounting:
- Only financing
- Bank discounts bill
- No collection service

Factoring:
- Financing + collection
- May include credit protection

---

# Forfaiting

## Meaning

Forfaiting is a financing technique where:

- Exporter sells medium to long-term receivables
- To a forfaiter
- Without recourse

Used mainly in international trade.

---

## Mechanism of Forfaiting

1. Exporter sells goods
2. Buyer issues bill/promissory note
3. Exporter sells receivable to forfaiter
4. Forfaiter pays exporter after discount
5. Forfaiter collects from importer

---

## Features

- Non-recourse basis
- Medium to long-term
- Used in export transactions
- Usually involves bank guarantee

---

## Fees in Forfaiting

- Discount rate
- Commitment fee
- Documentation fee

---

## Advantages of Forfaiting

- Eliminates credit risk
- Improves exporter liquidity
- No collection burden
- Converts credit sale into cash sale

---

# Difference Between Factoring and Forfaiting

Factoring:
- Short-term
- Domestic or international
- May be with or without recourse

Forfaiting:
- Medium to long-term
- Mainly international
- Always without recourse

---

# Trade Receivables Discounting System (TReDS)

## Meaning

TReDS is an electronic platform for:

- Discounting trade receivables
- Of MSMEs
- Through multiple financiers

---

## Objective

- Improve MSME liquidity
- Reduce payment delays
- Provide competitive financing rates

---

## Participants under TReDS

- MSME sellers
- Corporate buyers
- Banks and NBFC financiers

---

## Process Flow under TReDS

1. MSME uploads invoice
2. Buyer accepts invoice
3. Financiers bid for discounting
4. MSME receives funds
5. Financier collects from buyer

---

## Eligibility to Set Up TReDS

- Authorisation by RBI
- Operated by recognised entities
- Must meet regulatory and capital requirements

---

## Importance of TReDS

- Strengthens MSME ecosystem
- Reduces working capital stress
- Promotes transparency
- Enhances digital trade finance

---

# Relevance for JAIIB

---

# Key Facts

- Factoring Regulation Act: 2011
- Forfaiting used in exports
- TReDS for MSME receivable discounting
- Forfaiting always without recourse

---

# Common Exam Traps

- Confusing bills discounting with factoring
- Treating forfaiting as short-term
- Assuming TReDS limited to banks
- Ignoring credit risk transfer differences
