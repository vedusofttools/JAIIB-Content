# Merchant Banking Services

## Meaning of Merchant Banking

Merchant Banking refers to:

- Providing financial advisory and capital raising services
- Assisting companies in issue management
- Managing corporate restructuring and advisory functions

Merchant banks act as intermediaries between:

- Issuing companies
- Investors

They do not primarily accept deposits like commercial banks.

---

## Historical Background

- Merchant banking originated in Europe
- Introduced in India during 1960s
- Gained importance after capital market reforms (1991 onwards)

---

## Development of Merchant Banking in India

- Formal regulation under SEBI
- Growth with expansion of capital markets
- Increased role in IPOs and public issues
- Professionalisation of issue management

---

## Merchant Banking vs Commercial Banking

Merchant Banking:

- Focus on capital market services
- Advisory and underwriting
- Corporate restructuring

Commercial Banking:

- Accept deposits
- Provide loans
- Handle payment services

Merchant banks are fee-based service providers.

---

## Licensing Requirements

- Merchant bankers must register with SEBI
- SEBI (Merchant Bankers) Regulations, 1992
- Minimum net worth requirements
- Compliance with SEBI norms mandatory

---

## Activities of Merchant Banks

- Issue management (IPO, FPO)
- Underwriting of securities
- Portfolio management
- Corporate advisory services
- Mergers and acquisitions advisory
- Corporate restructuring
- Loan syndication

---

## SEBI Regulations on Merchant Banking

SEBI regulates merchant bankers to:

- Protect investor interests
- Ensure transparency
- Maintain capital adequacy
- Prevent market manipulation

---

## Key Policy Requirements as per SEBI

- Adequate capital base
- Due diligence before public issue
- Disclosure norms
- Compliance reporting
- Appointment of compliance officer

---

## Code of Conduct as per SEBI

Merchant bankers must:

- Act honestly and fairly
- Exercise due skill and care
- Avoid conflict of interest
- Maintain confidentiality
- Ensure investor protection
- Avoid misleading statements

---

## Role in Primary Market

Merchant bankers act as:

- Lead managers
- Book running lead managers
- Underwriters

They manage:

- Prospectus preparation
- Pricing
- Allotment process
- Regulatory approvals

---

## Importance of Merchant Banking

- Facilitates capital formation
- Supports corporate growth
- Improves transparency in public issues
- Enhances investor confidence

---

- Treating merchant banks as deposit-taking banks
- Ignoring SEBI regulation
