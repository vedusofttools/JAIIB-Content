RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme, 2021

MODULE A – GENERAL BANKING OPERATIONS  
JAIIB – Detailed Legal & Regulatory Notes (Conceptual + Procedural + Case-Oriented)

---

## 1. Introduction

The RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme, 2021 was introduced by the Reserve Bank of India to strengthen and simplify the grievance redressal mechanism for customers of regulated entities.

It replaced:

- Banking Ombudsman Scheme, 2006
- Ombudsman Scheme for NBFCs, 2018
- Ombudsman Scheme for Digital Transactions, 2019

The objective was to create:

“One Nation – One Ombudsman”

The scheme provides a cost-free, impartial, and quasi-judicial mechanism for resolving customer complaints.

---

## 2. Legal Basis

The Scheme is issued by RBI under powers conferred by:

- Section 35A of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949
- Section 45L of the RBI Act, 1934
- Section 18 of the Payment and Settlement Systems Act, 2007

Thus, it has statutory backing.

All Regulated Entities (REs) must comply.

---

## 3. Applicability

The Scheme applies to:

- All Commercial Banks
- Regional Rural Banks
- Small Finance Banks
- Payment Banks
- Cooperative Banks
- NBFCs (deposit-taking and certain non-deposit taking)
- Payment System Participants

Both public and private sector banks are covered.

---

## 4. Objective of the Scheme

The Scheme aims to:

- Provide a centralized grievance redressal system
- Simplify complaint filing process
- Eliminate territorial jurisdiction issues
- Ensure uniformity in complaint handling

It promotes transparency and customer protection.

---

## 5. Grounds for Complaint

A complaint can be filed for deficiency in service, including:

- Non-payment or delay in payment
- ATM/debit card issues
- Credit card disputes
- Loan processing delays
- Failure to follow RBI instructions
- Excess charges without notice
- Non-adherence to fair practices code
- Digital transaction failures

Deficiency must relate to regulated entity’s service.

---

## 6. Pre-Condition for Filing Complaint

Before approaching Ombudsman:

1. Customer must file complaint with the bank.
2. Bank must be given 30 days to respond.
3. If:
    - Bank rejects complaint, OR
    - Customer not satisfied, OR
    - No response within 30 days  
      Then complaint can be filed with Ombudsman.

This ensures internal grievance mechanism is exhausted first.

---

## 7. Filing Procedure

Complaint can be filed:

- Online through RBI portal
- By email
- By post

No fee is charged.

Complaints are handled by a Centralized Receipt and Processing Centre (CRPC).

There is no territorial jurisdiction restriction.

---

## 8. Powers of the Ombudsman

The Ombudsman may:

- Facilitate settlement through mediation
- Call for information from bank
- Examine records
- Pass an award if settlement not reached

The process is summary in nature (not full court procedure).

---

## 9. Award by Ombudsman

If complaint is upheld:

Ombudsman may award:

- Compensation for actual loss
- Compensation for mental agony (within prescribed limit)

Award is binding on bank if accepted by complainant.

If complainant does not accept award within prescribed time, it lapses.

---

## 10. Rejection of Complaint

Complaint may be rejected if:

- Frivolous or malicious
- Outside jurisdiction
- Already settled
- Filed after limitation period
- No deficiency in service

Ombudsman must record reasons for rejection.

---

## 11. Appeal Mechanism

If either party is aggrieved:

- Appeal lies to Appellate Authority (Deputy Governor of RBI).

Appeal must be filed within prescribed time.

Decision of Appellate Authority is final.

---

## 12. Compensation Limits

Compensation awarded is subject to monetary caps specified in the Scheme.

It may include:

- Direct financial loss
- Loss of opportunity
- Mental harassment (within limit)

Punitive damages are not normally granted.

---

## 13. Responsibilities of Regulated Entities

Banks must:

- Display details of Ombudsman scheme at branches.
- Display nodal officer contact details.
- Publish grievance redressal mechanism on website.
- Submit periodic reports to RBI.

Failure may invite supervisory action.

---

## 14. Role of Principal Nodal Officer

Each bank must appoint:

- Principal Nodal Officer (PNO)

PNO coordinates with Ombudsman offices.

PNO ensures:

- Timely submission of information.
- Proper handling of complaints.

---

## 15. Advantages of Integrated Scheme

- Single complaint portal
- No confusion regarding jurisdiction
- Faster disposal
- Uniform standards
- Covers multiple regulated entities

This improves customer accessibility.

---

## 16. Case-Based Understanding

Case 1:

Customer’s ATM transaction failed. Amount debited but not reversed for long time.  
Customer complains to bank. No reply for 30 days.  
Customer can approach Ombudsman.

Case 2:

Bank delays loan sanction without reason.  
Customer may file complaint alleging deficiency.

Case 3:

Customer files complaint without first approaching bank.  
Ombudsman may reject.

Case 4:

Bank complies with RBI guidelines and explains decision properly.  
Complaint may be dismissed if no deficiency.

---

## 17. Important JAIIB Points

- Introduced in 2021.
- Replaced earlier Ombudsman schemes.
- Covers banks, NBFCs, payment systems.
- 30-day rule before filing complaint.
- No fee for complaint.
- Appeal lies to Deputy Governor of RBI.
- Centralized processing centre handles complaints.

---

## 18. Conceptual Clarity

The RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme is:

- A consumer protection mechanism
- A regulatory accountability tool
- A quasi-judicial forum

It strengthens trust between customers and banks.
