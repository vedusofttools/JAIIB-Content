### PART A – CONCEPT & STRUCTURE (1–15)
############################

## 1.
NBFC stands for:

A. National Banking Finance Company  
B. Non-Banking Financial Company  
C. Non-Borrowing Financial Corporation  
D. National Business Finance Corporation

Answer: B

Explanation:
NBFC means Non-Banking Financial Company registered under Companies Act and regulated by RBI.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
NBFCs are regulated primarily by:

A. SEBI  
B. RBI  
C. Ministry of Finance  
D. NABARD

Answer: B

Explanation:
RBI regulates NBFCs under RBI Act, 1934 (Chapter III-B).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
NBFCs cannot accept:

A. Term loans  
B. Public deposits (unless permitted)  
C. Bank loans  
D. Debentures

Answer: B

Explanation:
Only deposit-taking NBFCs (NBFC-D) can accept public deposits subject to RBI approval.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Minimum Net Owned Fund (NOF) requirement for NBFC registration is prescribed by:

A. SEBI  
B. RBI  
C. Companies Act  
D. IRDAI

Answer: B

Explanation:
RBI prescribes minimum NOF for NBFCs.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
NBFCs cannot:

A. Lend money  
B. Invest in shares  
C. Issue demand deposits  
D. Provide hire purchase

Answer: C

Explanation:
NBFCs are not allowed to accept demand deposits.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
NBFC-ICC refers to:

A. Infrastructure Credit Company  
B. Investment and Credit Company  
C. International Credit Company  
D. Insurance Credit Company

Answer: B

Explanation:
NBFC-ICC is Investment and Credit Company.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Housing Finance Companies are regulated by:

A. SEBI  
B. RBI  
C. IRDAI  
D. NHB only

Answer: B

Explanation:
RBI regulates HFCs (earlier NHB, later transferred to RBI).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Systemically Important NBFCs are those with asset size above:

A. ₹100 crore  
B. ₹200 crore  
C. ₹500 crore  
D. ₹1000 crore

Answer: C

Explanation:
NBFCs with asset size ₹500 crore and above are systemically important (NBFC-ND-SI).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
NBFCs are different from banks because they:

A. Cannot lend  
B. Cannot accept demand deposits

NBFCs cannot accept demand deposits like banks.

------------------------------------------------------------

NBFCs contribute significantly to:

## 11.

NBFCs follow capital adequacy norms similar to:

## 14.

## 16.
If NBFC’s CRAR falls below prescribed level, RBI may:

A. Ignore  
B. Impose restrictions  
C. Increase lending  
D. Reduce supervision

Answer: B

Explanation:
RBI can impose corrective measures.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
If NBFC borrows short-term and lends long-term, it faces:

A. Market risk  
B. Liquidity risk  
C. Operational risk  
D. Legal risk

Answer: B

Explanation:
Asset-liability mismatch creates liquidity risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
If NBFC classified as NPA after 90 days overdue, it follows norms similar to:

A. Banks  
B. SEBI  
C. IRDAI  
D. Companies

Answer: A

Explanation:
NBFC NPA recognition norms align with banks (90-day norm).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.

C. Same
