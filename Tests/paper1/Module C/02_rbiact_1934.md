## 1.
The Reserve Bank of India was established under:

A. Banking Regulation Act, 1949  
B. RBI Act, 1934  
C. Companies Act, 1956  
D. SEBI Act, 1992

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
The RBI was established under the Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 and commenced operations on 1 April 1935. The Act provides the legal framework for currency issuance, monetary policy, and regulation of credit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Under the RBI Act, the sole authority to issue banknotes in India is:

A. Ministry of Finance  
B. RBI  
C. Government of India  
D. SBI

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Section 22 of the RBI Act grants RBI the sole right to issue banknotes in India. This centralizes currency control to maintain monetary stability.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
RBI follows which system of note issue?

A. Proportional Reserve System  
B. Minimum Reserve System  
C. Gold Standard  
D. Fixed Exchange Standard

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Under Section 33, RBI follows the Minimum Reserve System, requiring minimum reserve of ₹200 crore, including ₹115 crore in gold. This provides flexibility in currency issuance.

------------------------------------------------------------

RBI has two departments — Issue Department and Banking Department. Issue Department exclusively handles currency issuance and backing.

A. Banking Regulation Act

Section 42 of RBI Act mandates scheduled banks to maintain CRR with RBI as a percentage of NDTL.
