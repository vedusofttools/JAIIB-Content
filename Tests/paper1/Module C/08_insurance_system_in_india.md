# Insurance System in India

## Meaning of Insurance
Insurance is a **risk management mechanism** where:
- Risk is transferred from an individual to an insurer
- Losses of a few are shared by many

It provides **financial protection** against uncertain future events.

---

## Basic Principle of Insurance
Insurance works on:
- Law of large numbers
- Risk pooling
- Risk sharing

The insured pays a **premium**, and the insurer compensates in case of loss.

---

## History and Development of Insurance in India

### Early Phase
- Life Insurance Act enacted in **1956**
- General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Act enacted in **1972**

Insurance sector was **nationalised** to protect policyholders and expand coverage.

---

### Nationalisation Phase
- Life insurance under **LIC** (1956)
- General insurance under **GIC** and its subsidiaries (1972)

Focus:
- Social security
- Rural penetration
- Policyholder protection

---

### Liberalisation and Privatisation

#### Malhotra Committee
- Appointed in **1993**
- Recommended opening insurance sector to private players

---

#### Establishment of IRDAI
- IRDA Act enacted in **1999**
- IRDAI became regulator in **2000**

---

## Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) in Insurance
- FDI allowed to improve capital availability
- FDI limit increased to **74% (2021)** with safeguards

---

## Insurance Business in India

### Life Insurance
- Covers risk of death
- Provides savings and protection
- Long-term contracts

---

### General Insurance
- Covers non-life risks
- Includes health, motor, fire, marine, etc.
- Short-term contracts

---

## Insurance Penetration and Density

### Insurance Penetration
- Ratio of insurance premium to GDP
- Indicates importance of insurance in economy

---

### Insurance Density
- Per capita insurance premium
- Indicates level of insurance coverage

These metrics are used for **international comparison**.

---

## Insurance Penetration in India (Approx.)
- Life insurance: ~3%
- Non-life insurance: ~1%
- Overall penetration lower than global average

---

## Insurance Density in India
- Significantly lower than developed economies
- Indicates growth potential

---

## Insurance Companies Operating in India

### Life Insurance Companies
- Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC)
- Private life insurers

---

### General Insurance Companies
- Public sector general insurers
- Private sector general insurers
- Standalone health insurers

---

## Legislations Governing Insurance
- Insurance Act, **1938**
- IRDA Act, **1999**
- LIC Act, **1956**
- General Insurance Business Act, **1972**

These laws regulate operations, solvency, and policyholder protection.

---

## Insurance Intermediaries

Insurance intermediaries facilitate insurance distribution.

They include:
- Insurance agents
- Corporate agents
- Insurance brokers
- Web aggregators
- Third Party Administrators (TPAs)

Intermediaries are regulated by IRDAI.

---

## Reinsurance

## Meaning
Reinsurance is insurance of insurance companies.

Insurers transfer part of their risk to reinsurers to:
- Reduce risk exposure
- Improve capacity
- Enhance financial stability

---

## Reinsurance in India
- General Insurance Corporation of India (GIC Re) is national reinsurer
- Foreign reinsurers also permitted under regulation
