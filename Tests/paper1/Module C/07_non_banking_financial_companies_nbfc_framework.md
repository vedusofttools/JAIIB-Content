# Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) – Framework and Regulation

## Meaning of NBFC
A Non-Banking Financial Company (NBFC) is a company registered under the Companies Act that:
- Carries on financial business
- Does not hold a banking licence

NBFCs provide bank-like financial services but **are not banks**.

---

## What NBFCs Can and Cannot Do

### NBFCs Can
- Provide loans and advances
- Invest in securities
- Offer asset finance, leasing, hire purchase
- Provide microfinance and housing finance

### NBFCs Cannot
- Accept demand deposits
- Issue cheques drawn on themselves
- Participate directly in payment and settlement systems

---

## Evolution of NBFCs in India
- NBFCs expanded rapidly after financial sector liberalisation
- Filled credit gaps left by banks
- Focused on retail, MSME, housing, vehicle finance, microfinance

NBFCs complement banks by serving **niche and underserved segments**.

---

## Role of NBFCs in Inclusive Growth
- Credit access to MSMEs
- Financing of small traders and self-employed
- Microfinance to low-income households
- Support to housing and vehicle ownership
- Financial inclusion in semi-urban and rural areas

---

## Regulators of NBFCs
- **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** is the primary regulator
- Certain NBFCs also regulated by sectoral regulators:
    - Housing Finance Companies – RBI
    - Infrastructure finance entities – RBI

---

## Classification of NBFCs (Functional)

NBFCs are classified based on their activities, such as:
- NBFC-Investment and Credit Companies (NBFC-ICC)
- NBFC-Micro Finance Institutions (NBFC-MFI)
- NBFC-Factors
- NBFC-Infrastructure Finance Companies
- NBFC-Housing Finance Companies
- NBFC-Core Investment Companies

---

## Owned Funds and Net Owned Funds (NOF)

### Owned Funds
Owned funds include:
- Paid-up equity capital
- Free reserves
- Share premium

Minus:
- Accumulated losses
- Deferred revenue expenditure

---

### Net Owned Funds (NOF)
NOF = Owned Funds – Investments in subsidiaries and group companies

NOF is used to:
- Determine registration eligibility
- Assess financial strength of NBFCs

---

## Bank Finance to NBFCs
- Banks provide loans and credit facilities to NBFCs
- Subject to RBI exposure norms
- Helps NBFCs expand lending

Excessive dependence on bank funding can create **systemic risk**.

---

## Regulatory Oversight by RBI
RBI regulates NBFCs through:
- Registration and licensing
- Prudential norms
- Capital adequacy requirements
- Asset classification and provisioning
- Governance and disclosure norms

---

## Fair Practices Code for NBFCs
NBFCs must:
- Be transparent in loan pricing
- Avoid unfair recovery practices
- Disclose all terms and conditions
- Provide grievance redressal mechanisms

---

## Ombudsman Scheme for NBFCs
- NBFCs are covered under RBI Ombudsman Scheme
- Customers can file complaints for service deficiencies
- Improves consumer protection

---

## Scale Based Regulation (SBR) – Revised Framework

### Introduced
- RBI introduced Scale Based Regulation in **2021**

---

### Objective
- Align regulation with size and risk
- Strengthen financial stability
- Prevent systemic risks

---

### Four Regulatory Layers
- Base Layer
- Middle Layer
- Upper Layer
- Top Layer

Regulatory intensity increases with systemic importance.

---

## Importance of SBR Framework
- Proportionate regulation
- Better supervision
- Reduced regulatory arbitrage
- Improved resilience of NBFC sector

---

## Present Challenges of NBFC Sector
- Liquidity risk
- Asset-liability mismatch
- Credit concentration
- Governance issues

---

## Relevance for Banking and JAIIB
- NBFCs are frequently tested conceptually
- SBR framework is exam-important
- Differences between banks and NBFCs are common MCQs
- NOF-based questions are frequent

---

## Key Facts for JAIIB
- NBFCs regulated by RBI
- NBFCs cannot accept demand deposits
- NOF is key eligibility criterion
- SBR framework introduced in **2021**
- NBFCs complement banks, not replace them

---

## Common Exam Traps
- Treating NBFCs as banks
- Assuming NBFCs can issue cheques
- Ignoring SBR framework
- Confusing NOF with total capital
