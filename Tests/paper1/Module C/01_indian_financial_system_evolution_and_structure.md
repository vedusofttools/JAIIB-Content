# Indian Financial System – Evolution and Structure

## Meaning of Financial System
A financial system is a **network of institutions, markets, instruments and services** that facilitates the **mobilisation of savings and allocation of funds** to productive uses.

It enables:
- Transfer of funds from savers to borrowers
- Capital formation
- Risk management
- Efficient payment and settlement

---

## Components of Indian Financial System
The Indian financial system consists of:
- Financial institutions
- Financial markets
- Financial instruments
- Financial services

Together, they support economic growth and financial stability.

---

## Evolution of Indian Financial System

The Indian financial system has evolved in **three distinct phases** based on economic priorities and policy orientation.

---

## Phase I: Pre-1951 Organisation (Before Planned Development)

### Period
- Before 1951 (Pre-Independence and early post-Independence)

### Key Features
- Banking system dominated by private banks
- Limited regulation
- Focus on urban and commercial activities
- Negligible role in rural and agricultural credit

### Issues
- Frequent bank failures
- Lack of depositor protection
- Absence of institutional credit for agriculture and small industries

This phase highlighted the **need for regulation and state intervention**.

---

## Phase II: 1951 to Mid-1980s Organisation (Planned Development Phase)

### Period
- 1951 to mid-1980s

### Background
- Adoption of Five Year Plans (from 1951)
- Emphasis on planned economic development
- State-led development strategy

---

### Major Developments

#### Establishment of Regulatory Framework
- Banking Regulation Act, **1949**
- RBI strengthened as central monetary authority

---

#### Nationalisation of Banks
- First phase: **1969** (14 major banks)
- Second phase: **1980** (6 more banks)

### Purpose of Nationalisation
- Expansion of banking to rural areas
- Credit to agriculture and small industries
- Financial inclusion
- Support to planned development

---

#### Expansion of Institutional Credit
- Priority Sector Lending introduced (1972)
- Growth of Regional Rural Banks (1975)
- Strengthening of cooperative banking

---

#### Development Financial Institutions (DFIs)
Established to provide **long-term finance**:
- IFCI (1948)
- ICICI (1955)
- IDBI (1964)

---

### Outcome of Phase II
- Rapid branch expansion
- Increased credit flow to priority sectors
- Improved financial inclusion
- However, issues of inefficiency and poor asset quality emerged

---

## Phase III: Post-1990s Organisation (Reform Phase)

### Period
- Post-1991 onwards

### Trigger
- Balance of Payments crisis, **1991**
- Low foreign exchange reserves
- High fiscal deficit

---

### Narasimham Committee (1991)
- First major banking sector reform committee
- Recommended:
    - Reduction in SLR and CRR
    - Introduction of prudential norms
    - Entry of private sector banks
    - Capital adequacy requirements

---

### Key Reforms Introduced

#### Liberalisation of Banking
- Entry of new private sector banks
- Increased competition
- Market-oriented interest rates

---

#### Prudential Norms
- Asset classification
- Income recognition
- Provisioning norms
- Capital adequacy norms (CRAR)

---

#### Technology Adoption
- Core Banking Solutions
- Electronic payment systems
- Digital banking

---

#### Strengthening Regulation and Supervision
- Risk-based supervision
- Improved disclosure and transparency
- Better governance standards

---

## Present Structure of Indian Financial System

### Banking Segment
- Public sector banks
- Private sector banks
- Regional Rural Banks
- Cooperative banks
- Payment Banks
- Small Finance Banks

---

### Non-Banking Segment
- Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs)
- Microfinance Institutions
- Housing Finance Companies

---

### Financial Markets
- Money market
- Capital market
- Government securities market
- Foreign exchange market

---

### Financial Regulators
- RBI – banking and monetary policy
- SEBI – capital markets
- IRDAI – insurance
- PFRDA – pensions

---

## Present Status of Indian Financial System

### Strengths
- Wide banking network
- Strong regulatory framework
- Growing digital payment ecosystem
- Improved financial inclusion

---

### Challenges
- Asset quality issues
- Credit growth cycles
- Cyber security risks
- Financial stability concerns

---

## Role of Indian Financial System in Economic Growth
- Mobilises savings
- Facilitates investment
- Supports government borrowing
- Promotes inclusive growth
- Enables monetary policy transmission

---

## Key Facts for JAIIB
- Five Year Plans began in **1951**
- Bank nationalisation: **1969 & 1980**
- Narasimham Committee: **1991**
- Financial sector reforms began in **1991**
- RBI is the apex monetary authority

---

## Common Exam Traps
- Confusing pre-1951 and post-1951 phases
- Assuming reforms started before 1991
- Treating DFIs as commercial banks
- Ignoring role of planning in Phase II
