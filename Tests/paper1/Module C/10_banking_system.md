### PART A – INDIAN BANKING SYSTEM (1–15)
############################

## 1.
The apex monetary authority in India is:

A. Ministry of Finance  
B. RBI  
C. SEBI  
D. NABARD

Answer: B

Explanation:
Reserve Bank of India (RBI), established under RBI Act, 1934, regulates currency, credit, and monetary policy.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Nationalisation of major commercial banks in India first took place in:

A. 1956  
B. 1969  
C. 1975  
D. 1980

Answer: B

Explanation:
14 major banks were nationalised in 1969 to ensure credit flow to priority sectors.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Second phase of bank nationalisation occurred in:

A. 1975  
B. 1980  
C. 1985  
D. 1991

Answer: B

Explanation:
6 more banks were nationalised in 1980.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
The primary objective of bank nationalisation was:

A. Increase private ownership  
B. Promote financial inclusion  
C. Reduce foreign investment  
D. Increase profits

Answer: B

Explanation:
Nationalisation aimed to expand banking to rural areas and priority sectors.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Scheduled Commercial Banks are those included in:

A. RBI Act Schedule  
B. Second Schedule of RBI Act  
C. Banking Regulation Act  
D. SEBI Act

Answer: B

Explanation:
Banks listed in the Second Schedule of RBI Act, 1934 are Scheduled Banks.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Regional Rural Banks (RRBs) were established in:

A. 1969  
B. 1975

D. 1991

Explanation:
RRBs were established under RRB Act, 1976 (ordinance 1975) to serve rural areas.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
