### PART A – STRUCTURE OF INDIAN BANKING SYSTEM (1–15)
############################

## 1.
The Indian banking system is primarily regulated by:

A. Ministry of Finance  
B. RBI  
C. SEBI  
D. NABARD

Answer: B

Explanation:
RBI regulates banks under RBI Act, 1934 and Banking Regulation Act, 1949.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Banks included in the Second Schedule of RBI Act are called:

A. Cooperative Banks  
B. Nationalised Banks  
C. Scheduled Banks  
D. Private Banks

Answer: C

Explanation:
Scheduled Banks are listed in the Second Schedule of RBI Act and must satisfy capital and reserve requirements.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Scheduled Banks are required to maintain:

A. Only SLR  
B. Only CRR  
C. CRR with RBI  
D. No reserves

Answer: C

Explanation:
Scheduled Banks must maintain CRR with RBI.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Public Sector Banks are those in which Government holding is:

A. Below 25%  
B. Below 50%  
C. Above 50%  
D. 100% only

Government of India holds more than 50% stake in PSBs.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.

A. RBI  
B. State Government

D. SEBI

Ownership lies mainly with private individuals/entities.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Foreign banks operating in India are regulated by:

A. Only home country  
B. Only RBI  
C. RBI and home regulator  
D. SEBI

Explanation:
Foreign banks are regulated by RBI and their home country regulators.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Regional Rural Banks (RRBs) are jointly owned by:

A. RBI, SEBI, Government  
B. Central Govt, State Govt, Sponsor Bank

Explanation:
Ownership: 50% Central Govt, 15% State Govt, 35% Sponsor Bank.
