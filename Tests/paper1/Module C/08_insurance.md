## 1.
The insurance sector in India is regulated by:

A. RBI  
B. SEBI  
C. IRDAI  
D. NABARD

Answer: C

Explanation:
Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) regulates insurance companies under IRDAI Act, 1999.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Life Insurance Corporation (LIC) was established in:

A. 1948  
B. 1956  
C. 1969  
D. 1991

Answer: B

Explanation:
LIC was formed in 1956 after nationalisation of life insurance companies.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
General Insurance business was nationalised in:

A. 1956  
B. 1969  
C. 1972  
D. 1999

Answer: C

Explanation:
General Insurance Business (Nationalisation) Act, 1972 nationalised general insurance.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Insurance is primarily a mechanism of:

A. Profit generation  
B. Risk transfer  
C. Capital market trading  
D. Tax collection

Answer: B

Explanation:
Insurance transfers risk from insured to insurer in exchange for premium.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Principle of utmost good faith is called:

A. Indemnity  
B. Subrogation  
C. Uberrimae fidei  
D. Contribution

Answer: C

Explanation:
Both parties must disclose all material facts.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Principle of indemnity applies mainly to:

A. Life insurance  
B. General insurance  
C. Pension schemes  
D. Mutual funds

Answer: B

Explanation:
Indemnity ensures compensation equals actual loss (not profit).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
In life insurance, indemnity principle does not apply because:

A. Risk is measurable  
B. Human life cannot be exactly valued  
C. Premium fixed  
D. RBI regulates

Answer: B

Explanation:
Life cannot be valued precisely; hence fixed sum assured paid.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Reinsurance means:

A. Insurer insures policyholder  
B. Insurer insures another insurer  
C. Customer insures again  
D. RBI insures banks

Answer: B

Explanation:
Reinsurance spreads risk among insurers.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
IRDAI ensures solvency of insurers through:

A. CRR  
B. SLR  
C. Solvency margin requirements  
D. Repo rate

Answer: C

Explanation:
Insurers must maintain required solvency margin.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Insurance penetration refers to:

A. Number of agents

C. Number of claims  
D. Foreign investment

Explanation:
Insurance penetration = Total premium / GDP.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
Insurance density refers to:

A. Premium per capita  
B. Total policies

D. Claim ratio

Explanation:
Insurance density measures per capita premium.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Bancassurance refers to:

A. Bank insurance

Banks act as corporate agents for insurers.

## 13.
Third-party motor insurance is:

## 14.
Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) limit in insurance sector is:

## 15.
Insurance contracts are contracts of:

A. Guarantee

## 16.
