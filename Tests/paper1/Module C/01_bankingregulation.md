## 1.
Under Section 5(b) of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949, “banking” means:

A. Lending money for profit  
B. Accepting deposits for lending/investment, repayable on demand or otherwise  
C. Accepting deposits and trading goods  
D. Investing only in government securities

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Section 5(b) defines banking as accepting deposits from the public for the purpose of lending or investment, repayable on demand or otherwise, and withdrawable by cheque/draft/order.  
Option A is incomplete (deposit acceptance missing).  
Option C is incorrect because Section 8 prohibits trading in goods.  
Option D is restrictive and does not include lending.

## 2.
A company accepting deposits but not allowing withdrawal by cheque is:

A. Automatically a banking company  
B. Not a banking company under Section 5(c)  
C. Required to maintain SLR  
D. Violating Section 24

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Section 5(c) defines a banking company as one transacting banking business as per Section 5(b).  
If withdrawal by cheque is not allowed, it fails the statutory definition and may be treated as an NBFC, not a banking company.
