# Indian Banking Structure and Types of Banks

## Indian Banking Structure – Overview
The Indian banking structure refers to the **organised framework of banks** operating under regulation to provide:
- Deposit services
- Credit
- Payment and settlement facilities

It is designed to meet the needs of:
- Agriculture
- Industry
- Trade
- Services
- Households

---

## Classification of Banks in India
Banks in India are broadly classified into:
- Scheduled Commercial Banks
- Cooperative Banks
- Regional Rural Banks
- Specialised Banks

---

## Scheduled Commercial Banks (SCBs)

## Meaning
Scheduled Commercial Banks are banks listed in the **Second Schedule of the RBI Act, 1934**.

They are eligible for:
- Borrowing from RBI
- Membership of clearing houses

---

## Types of Scheduled Commercial Banks

### Public Sector Banks
- Government holds majority stake
- Major providers of banking services
- Focus on social and developmental banking

---

### Private Sector Banks
- Majority ownership with private shareholders
- Operate on commercial principles
- Strong technology adoption

---

### Foreign Banks
- Banks incorporated outside India
- Operate through branches in India
- Focus on niche and corporate banking

---

## Functions of Scheduled Commercial Banks
- Accept deposits
- Provide loans and advances
- Credit creation
- Payment and settlement
- Agency services
- Utility services

---

## Regional Rural Banks (RRBs)

## Background
- Established under RRB Act, **1976**
- Objective: Provide credit to rural and semi-urban areas

---

## Ownership Structure
- Central Government
- State Government
- Sponsor Bank

---

## Role of RRBs
- Agricultural credit
- MSME financing
- Financial inclusion
- Support to weaker sections

---

## Cooperative Banks

## Meaning
Cooperative banks operate on **cooperative principles** of mutual help.

---

## Types of Cooperative Banks

### Urban Cooperative Banks
- Operate mainly in urban areas
- Serve small traders and businesses

---

### Rural Cooperative Banks
- State Cooperative Banks
- District Central Cooperative Banks
- Primary Agricultural Credit Societies

---

## Strengths and Limitations
- Strong local presence
- Limited capital base
- Governance challenges

---

## Local Area Banks (LABs)

## Meaning
Local Area Banks are small private banks operating in a **limited geographical area**.

---

## Objective
- Promote rural savings
- Provide credit to local sectors

LABs have **restricted operations** and limited presence.

---

## Payment Banks

## Background
- Introduced based on RBI guidelines, **2014**
- Operational from **2015**

---

## Key Features
- Can accept deposits up to prescribed limits
- Cannot lend
- Provide payment and remittance services

---

## Purpose
- Promote financial inclusion
- Facilitate digital payments

---

## Small Finance Banks (SFBs)

## Background
- Introduced in **2015**
- Converted from NBFCs, MFIs, or LABs

---

## Key Features
- Accept deposits
- Provide loans
- Focus on small borrowers and MSMEs

---

## Role
- Financial inclusion
- Credit to underserved segments

---

## Non-Banking Financial Companies (NBFCs) – Brief Overview

## Meaning
NBFCs are financial institutions that provide bank-like services but:
- Do not accept demand deposits
- Are not part of payment system

---

## Role
- Complement banking system
- Support credit to niche segments

---

## Role of RBI in Banking Structure
- Licensing of banks
- Regulation and supervision
- Prudential norms
- Consumer protection

---

## Key RBI Guidelines (Broad)
- Capital adequacy norms
- Asset classification and provisioning
- Exposure norms
- Governance standards

---

## Importance of Diverse Banking Structure
- Meets varied credit needs
- Promotes financial inclusion
- Reduces regional imbalance
- Enhances financial stability

---

## Key Facts for JAIIB
- SCBs listed in Second Schedule of RBI Act
- RRB Act: **1976**
- Payment Banks and SFBs operational from **2015**
- Cooperative banks operate on cooperative principles
- NBFCs complement banks, not replace them

---

## Common Exam Traps
- Treating NBFCs as banks
- Assuming Payment Banks can lend
- Confusing RRBs with cooperative banks
- Ignoring ownership pattern of RRBs
