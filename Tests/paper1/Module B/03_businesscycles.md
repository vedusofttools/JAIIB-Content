### PART A – THEORY (1–10)
############################

## 1.
A business cycle refers to:

A. Seasonal fluctuations in agriculture  
B. Long-term economic growth  
C. Recurrent fluctuations in economic activity  
D. Inflation only

Answer: C

Explanation:
A business cycle represents recurring fluctuations in aggregate economic activity over time.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
The main phases of business cycle are:

A. Growth and recession  
B. Boom and bust  
C. Expansion, Peak, Recession, Trough  
D. Inflation and deflation

Answer: C

Explanation:
The cycle includes expansion → peak → recession → trough.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
The highest point of economic activity in a cycle is called:

A. Trough  
B. Peak  
C. Recession  
D. Depression

Answer: B

Explanation:
Peak is the maximum level of output before contraction begins.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
The lowest point in business cycle is:

A. Peak  
B. Trough  
C. Boom  
D. Inflation

Answer: B

Explanation:
Trough is the lowest level of economic activity before recovery begins.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
During expansion phase, output and employment:

A. Decrease  
B. Increase  
C. Remain constant  
D. Collapse

Answer: B

Explanation:
Expansion phase is characterized by rising output and employment.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Recession is generally defined as:

A. Two consecutive quarters of negative GDP growth  
B. Inflation above 6%  
C. High employment  
D. High exports

Answer: A

Explanation:
Recession commonly refers to two consecutive quarters of declining GDP.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Depression is:

A. Mild slowdown  
B. Severe and prolonged recession  
C. Inflation  
D. Growth phase

Answer: B

Explanation:
Depression is a deep and long-lasting contraction.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Leading indicators of business cycle include:

A. GDP (current)  
B. Unemployment rate  
C. Stock prices  
D. Past inflation

Answer: C

Explanation:
Stock prices often change before overall economic activity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Lagging indicators include:

A. Share market  
B. New orders  
C. Unemployment rate  
D. Consumer expectations

Answer: C

Explanation:
Unemployment responds after economic slowdown begins.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
During boom phase, inflationary pressure is generally:

A. Low  
B. High  
C. Zero  
D. Negative

Answer: B

Explanation:
High demand during boom leads to inflation.
