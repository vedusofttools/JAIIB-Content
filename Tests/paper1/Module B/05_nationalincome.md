############################
### PART A – THEORY (1–10)
############################

## 1.
National Income refers to:

A. Total tax revenue  
B. Total income earned by residents of a country in a year  
C. Government expenditure  
D. Export earnings

Answer: B

Explanation:
National income is the total income earned by residents of a country from all sources in a given period.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
GDP stands for:

A. Gross Domestic Product  
B. Gross Development Product  
C. General Domestic Price  
D. Government Domestic Product

Answer: A

Explanation:
GDP measures total value of goods and services produced within domestic territory.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
GNP differs from GDP because it includes:

A. Depreciation  
B. Net factor income from abroad  
C. Taxes  
D. Exports only

Answer: B

Explanation:
GNP = GDP + Net Factor Income from Abroad (NFIA).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Net National Product (NNP) is:

A. GNP – Depreciation  
B. GDP + Depreciation  
C. GDP – Taxes  
D. GNP + Taxes

Answer: A

Explanation:
NNP = GNP – Depreciation.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Personal Income excludes:

A. Transfer payments  
B. Undistributed profits  
C. Wages  
D. Interest

Answer: B

Explanation:
Undistributed corporate profits are not received by individuals.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Disposable Income equals:

A. Personal Income – Direct Taxes  
B. GDP – Taxes  
C. GNP – Depreciation  
D. NNP – Subsidy

Answer: A

Explanation:
Disposable income is income available for consumption and saving.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Depreciation is also called:

A. Capital gain  
B. Capital consumption  
C. Income tax  
D. Net income

Answer: B

Explanation:
Depreciation represents consumption of fixed capital.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Nominal GDP is measured at:

A. Constant prices  
B. Current prices  
C. Wholesale prices only  
D. Consumer prices only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Nominal GDP uses current year prices.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Real GDP is measured at:

A. Current prices  
B. Constant prices  
C. Market prices only  
D. Wholesale prices only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Real GDP removes effect of inflation using base year prices.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
GDP deflator measures:

A. Tax  
B. Inflation  
C. Export growth  
D. Income tax

Answer: B

Explanation:
GDP deflator measures overall price change in economy.

### PART B – CONCEPTUAL APPLICATION (11–20)
############################

## 11.
If GDP increases but population grows faster, per capita income:

A. Increases  
B. Decreases  
C. Same  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
Per capita income = GDP / population.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Transfer payments are included in:

A. GDP  
B. National income  
C. Personal income  
D. Net exports

Answer: C

Explanation:
Transfer payments are included in personal income but not GDP.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
Indirect taxes increase:

A. Factor cost  
B. Market price  
C. Depreciation  
D. NFIA

Answer: B

Explanation:
GDP at market price includes indirect taxes.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
GDP at factor cost equals:

A. GDP at market price – Net indirect taxes  
B. GDP + depreciation  
C. GDP – depreciation  
D. GDP + exports

Answer: A

Explanation:
GDP (Factor Cost) = GDP (Market Price) – Indirect Taxes + Subsidies.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
If NFIA is positive, then:

A. GNP < GDP  
B. GNP = GDP  
C. GNP > GDP  
D. GNP = NNP

Answer: C

Explanation:
Positive NFIA increases GNP above GDP.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
Primary sector includes:

A. Manufacturing  
B. Services  
C. Agriculture  
D. Banking

Answer: C

Explanation:
Primary sector extracts natural resources.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
Value added method avoids:

A. Inflation  
B. Double counting  
C. Taxation  
D. Exports

Answer: B

Explanation:
Only value added at each stage is counted.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Income method measures:

A. Output  
B. Expenditure  
C. Factor incomes  
D. Prices

Answer: C

Explanation:
Income method sums wages, rent, interest, and profit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
Expenditure method includes:

A. Wages only  
B. C + I + G + (X – M)  
C. Taxes only  
D. Exports only

Answer: B

Explanation:
GDP = Consumption + Investment + Government spending + Net exports.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
If exports > imports, net exports:

A. Negative  
B. Zero  
C. Positive  
D. Doubles

Answer: C

Explanation:
Net exports = Exports – Imports.

## 21.
GDP = ₹5,000 crore; NFIA = ₹200 crore. GNP =

A. 4,800  
B. 5,000  
C. 5,200  
D. 5,500

Answer: C

Explanation:
GNP = GDP + NFIA = 5,000 + 200 = 5,200.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 22.
GNP = ₹6,000 crore; Depreciation = ₹500 crore. NNP =

A. 5,000  
B. 5,200  
C. 5,500  
D. 6,500

Answer: C

Explanation:
NNP = 6,000 – 500 = 5,500.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 23.
Consumption = 2,000; Investment = 1,000; Govt spending = 800; Net exports = -200. GDP =

A. 3,400  
B. 3,500  
C. 3,600  
D. 3,700

Answer: C

Explanation:
GDP = 2,000 + 1,000 + 800 – 200 = 3,600.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 24.
Nominal GDP = ₹10,000; Real GDP = ₹8,000. GDP deflator =

A. 100  
B. 110  
C. 120  
D. 125

Answer: D

Explanation:
Deflator = (Nominal / Real) × 100  
= (10,000 / 8,000) × 100 = 125.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 25.
GDP rises from 5,000 to 5,500. Growth rate =

A. 5%  
B. 8%  
C. 10%  
D. 12%

Answer: C

Explanation:
(5,500 – 5,000)/5,000 × 100 = 10%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 26.
Per capita income = ₹1,00,000; population = 1 crore. National income =

A. ₹1,00,000 crore  
B. ₹10,000 crore  
C. ₹1,000 crore  
D. ₹5,000 crore

Answer: A

Explanation:
1,00,000 × 1 crore = 1,00,000 crore.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 27.
GDP at market price = 5,000; Net indirect taxes = 200. GDP at factor cost =

A. 4,800  
B. 5,000  
C. 5,200  
D. 4,500

Answer: A

Explanation:
5,000 – 200 = 4,800.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 28.
If imports increase while other components constant, GDP:

A. Increases  
B. Decreases  
C. Same  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher imports reduce net exports.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 29.
If depreciation increases, NNP:

A. Increases  
B. Decreases  
C. Same  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
NNP = GNP – Depreciation.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 30.
If GDP growth positive but income inequality rising, growth is:

A. Inclusive  
B. Exclusive  
C. Balanced  
D. Sustainable

Answer: B

Explanation:
Growth without equitable distribution is not inclusive.
