############################
### PART A – THEORY (1–10)
############################

## 1.
Balance of Payments (BoP) records:

A. Only exports  
B. Only imports  
C. All economic transactions with rest of the world  
D. Only capital inflows

Answer: C

Explanation:
BoP records all economic transactions between residents of a country and the rest of the world during a specific period.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
BoP consists of:

A. Current Account only  
B. Capital Account only  
C. Current Account and Capital Account  
D. Fiscal Account

Answer: C

Explanation:
BoP has two main components: Current Account and Capital Account (financial flows).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
The Current Account includes:

A. FDI  
B. Portfolio investment  
C. Trade in goods and services  
D. RBI reserves

Answer: C

Explanation:
Current Account includes trade in goods, services, income, and transfers.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Capital Account primarily includes:

A. Export of goods  
B. Import of services  
C. Foreign investments and loans  
D. Remittances

Answer: C

Explanation:
Capital account records financial transactions such as FDI, FPI, loans, and banking capital.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Trade Balance refers to:

A. Exports – Imports (goods only)  
B. Services – Imports  
C. Income – Transfers  
D. Capital flows

Answer: A

Explanation:
Trade balance measures difference between exports and imports of goods.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
If exports exceed imports, trade balance is:

A. Deficit  
B. Surplus  
C. Zero  
D. Negative

Answer: B

Explanation:
Exports greater than imports create trade surplus.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Current Account Deficit (CAD) occurs when:

A. Exports > Imports  
B. Imports > Exports  
C. Capital inflow > Outflow  
D. Reserves increase

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher imports than exports result in CAD.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Remittances received from abroad are recorded in:

A. Capital Account  
B. Current Account  
C. Financial Account  
D. Trade Account

Answer: B

Explanation:
Remittances are part of secondary income under Current Account.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
FDI is recorded under:

A. Current Account  
B. Capital/Financial Account  
C. Trade Account  
D. Reserves

Answer: B

Explanation:
FDI is a capital inflow under financial account.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
If BoP shows overall deficit, RBI may:

A. Increase imports  
B. Use foreign exchange reserves  
C. Reduce exports  
D. Increase inflation

Answer: B

Explanation:
RBI can draw down forex reserves to finance deficit.
