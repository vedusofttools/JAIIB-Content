############################
### PART A – THEORY (1–10)
############################

## 1.
Monetary policy in India is formulated by:

A. Ministry of Finance  
B. RBI  
C. SEBI  
D. NABARD

Answer: B

Explanation:
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is responsible for formulating and implementing monetary policy.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
The Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) in India consists of:

A. 4 members  
B. 5 members  
C. 6 members  
D. 7 members

Answer: C

Explanation:
MPC has 6 members — 3 from RBI (including Governor) and 3 external members appointed by Government.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
The primary objective of monetary policy in India is:

A. GDP maximization  
B. Exchange rate control  
C. Price stability while supporting growth  
D. Export promotion

Answer: C

Explanation:
Under flexible inflation targeting, RBI aims at price stability while supporting growth.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Inflation target under India’s framework is:

A. 2%  
B. 4% ± 2%  
C. 6% fixed  
D. 5% ± 1%

Answer: B

Explanation:
Target CPI inflation is 4% with tolerance band of 2%–6%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Repo rate is the rate at which:

A. Banks lend to RBI  
B. RBI lends to banks  
C. Public borrows  
D. Corporates borrow

Answer: B

Explanation:
Repo rate is the rate at which RBI lends short-term funds to banks.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Reverse repo rate is the rate at which:

A. RBI borrows from banks  
B. Banks borrow from RBI  
C. Government borrows  
D. Public lends

Answer: A

Explanation:
Reverse repo is the rate at which RBI borrows funds from banks.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
CRR stands for:

A. Cash Reserve Ratio  
B. Credit Reserve Ratio  
C. Capital Reserve Rate  
D. Currency Reserve Ratio

Answer: A

Explanation:
CRR is the percentage of deposits banks must keep with RBI in cash.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
SLR stands for:

A. Statutory Lending Rate  
B. Statutory Liquidity Ratio  
C. Standard Liquidity Rate  
D. Special Liquidity Ratio

Answer: B

Explanation:
SLR is the percentage of deposits banks must maintain in liquid assets.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Open Market Operations (OMO) involve:

A. Direct taxation  
B. Buying and selling of government securities  
C. Loan waiver  
D. Export regulation

Answer: B

Explanation:
RBI buys/sells government securities to regulate liquidity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
An increase in repo rate generally leads to:

A. Increase in inflation  
B. Decrease in borrowing  
C. Increase in liquidity  
D. Lower interest rates

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher repo makes borrowing costlier, reducing credit demand.

############################

## 11.
If RBI reduces repo rate, bank lending rates likely:

A. Increase  
B. Decrease  
C. Same  
D. Double

Answer: B

Explanation:
Lower repo reduces cost of funds for banks.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
If CRR increases, bank liquidity:

A. Increases  
B. Decreases  
C. Same  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher CRR reduces lendable funds.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
Expansionary monetary policy involves:

A. Increase repo  
B. Reduce money supply  
C. Reduce repo rate  
D. Increase CRR

Answer: C

Explanation:
Lower repo increases liquidity and credit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
Contractionary monetary policy involves:

A. Reduce repo  
B. Increase repo  
C. Increase money supply  
D. Reduce CRR

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher repo reduces liquidity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
Transmission of monetary policy mainly affects:

A. Tax rates  
B. Interest rates and credit  
C. Government spending  
D. Exports only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Policy rate changes influence lending and deposit rates.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
If inflation exceeds 6%, RBI is required to:

A. Ignore it  
B. Write explanation to government  
C. Increase CRR automatically  
D. Reduce repo

Answer: B

Explanation:
If inflation breaches tolerance band for 3 consecutive quarters, RBI must explain to Government.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
MSF (Marginal Standing Facility) rate is:

A. Lower than repo  
B. Higher than repo  
C. Equal to CRR  
D. Equal to SLR

Answer: B

Explanation:
MSF is typically higher than repo and acts as penal rate.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Liquidity Adjustment Facility (LAF) includes:

A. Repo and Reverse Repo  
B. CRR and SLR  
C. OMO only  
D. Inflation targeting

Answer: A

Explanation:
LAF manages short-term liquidity via repo and reverse repo.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
If RBI buys government securities, liquidity:

A. Falls  
B. Rises  
C. Same  
D. Zero

Answer: B

Explanation:
Buying securities injects liquidity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
Higher interest rates reduce aggregate demand through:

A. Higher investment  
B. Lower borrowing  
C. Higher wages  
D. Higher exports

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher cost of credit reduces consumption and investment.
