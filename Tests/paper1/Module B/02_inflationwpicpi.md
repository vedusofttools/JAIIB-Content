############################
### PART A – THEORY (1–10)
############################

## 1.
Inflation refers to:

A. Fall in general price level  
B. Continuous rise in general price level  
C. Increase in GDP  
D. Increase in exports

Answer: B

Explanation:
Inflation is a sustained increase in the general price level of goods and services over time.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Deflation refers to:

A. Continuous rise in prices  
B. Continuous fall in prices  
C. Increase in income  
D. Increase in supply

Answer: B

Explanation:
Deflation is a sustained decline in the general price level.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
CPI stands for:

A. Cost Price Index  
B. Consumer Price Index  
C. Commodity Price Indicator  
D. Corporate Price Index

Answer: B

Explanation:
CPI measures changes in retail prices faced by consumers.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
WPI stands for:

A. Wholesale Price Index  
B. World Price Index  
C. Weighted Price Indicator  
D. Welfare Price Index

Answer: A

Explanation:
WPI measures changes in prices at wholesale level.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
In India, CPI is used for:

A. Corporate tax calculation  
B. Monetary policy targeting  
C. Export calculation  
D. Foreign exchange

Answer: B

Explanation:
RBI uses CPI (Combined) as the nominal anchor for inflation targeting.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
WPI mainly captures prices at:

A. Retail level  
B. Producer/wholesale level  
C. Consumer level  
D. International level

Answer: B

Explanation:
WPI measures price changes at the wholesale stage.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Demand-pull inflation occurs due to:

A. Increase in supply  
B. Increase in aggregate demand  
C. Fall in cost  
D. Decrease in money supply

Answer: B

Explanation:
When aggregate demand exceeds supply, prices rise.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Cost-push inflation occurs due to:

A. Increase in production cost  
B. Decrease in wages  
C. Increase in supply  
D. Lower demand

Answer: A

Explanation:
Rising input costs increase prices of goods and services.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Headline inflation refers to:

A. Core inflation only  
B. Overall inflation including food and fuel  
C. Only food inflation  
D. Only fuel inflation

Answer: B

Explanation:
Headline inflation includes all items including volatile food and fuel.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Core inflation excludes:

A. Food and fuel  
B. Services  
C. Manufactured goods  
D. Housing

Answer: A

Explanation:
Core inflation removes volatile food and fuel components.

## 21.
Price index increases from 150 to 165. Inflation rate?

A. 5%  
B. 8%  
C. 10%  
D. 12%

Answer: C

Explanation:
(165 – 150)/150 × 100 = 10%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 22.
CPI was 200 last year and 220 this year. Inflation?

A. 8%  
B. 10%  
C. 12%  
D. 15%

Answer: B

Explanation:
(220 – 200)/200 × 100 = 10%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 23.
If nominal interest rate = 8% and inflation = 5%, real interest rate:

A. 2%  
B. 3%  
C. 4%  
D. 5%

Answer: B

Explanation:
Real rate = Nominal – Inflation = 8 – 5 = 3%.

------------------------------------------------------------
