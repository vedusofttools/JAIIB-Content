### PART A – MICROECONOMICS (1–10)
############################

## 1.
Microeconomics primarily studies:

A. National income  
B. Inflation  
C. Individual economic units  
D. Balance of payments

Answer: C

Explanation:
Microeconomics focuses on individual consumers, firms, and specific markets.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Price determination of a single commodity is a subject of:

A. Macroeconomics  
B. Public finance  
C. Microeconomics  
D. International economics

Answer: C

Explanation:
Price of an individual good is analyzed under demand and supply in microeconomics.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Law of demand states that:

A. Price and quantity demanded move together  
B. Price and quantity demanded move inversely  
C. Demand is fixed  
D. Supply determines demand

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher price leads to lower quantity demanded, ceteris paribus.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Elasticity of demand measures:

A. Change in supply  
B. Responsiveness of quantity demanded to price  
C. Income distribution  
D. Inflation

Answer: B

Explanation:
Elasticity measures percentage change in quantity due to percentage change in price.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Opportunity cost refers to:

A. Monetary cost  
B. Explicit cost  
C. Next best alternative forgone  
D. Accounting profit

Answer: C

Explanation:
Opportunity cost is the value of the next best alternative sacrificed.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Perfect competition is characterized by:

A. Single seller  
B. Few sellers  
C. Many buyers and sellers  
D. Government control

Answer: C

Explanation:
Perfect competition has many buyers and sellers with homogeneous products.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
A monopolist is:

A. Price taker  
B. Price maker  
C. Government authority  
D. Small firm

Answer: B

Explanation:
A monopolist has power to influence price.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Marginal cost is:

A. Total cost  
B. Average cost  
C. Cost of producing one additional unit  
D. Fixed cost

Answer: C

Explanation:
Marginal cost is additional cost of producing one more unit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
If demand increases and supply constant, price:

A. Falls  
B. Rises  
C. Same  
D. Zero

Answer: B

Explanation:
Excess demand pushes prices up.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Consumer equilibrium occurs where:

A. MU = 0  
B. MUx/Px = MUy/Py  
C. Total utility maximum only  
D. Price equals income

Answer: B

Explanation:
Consumer equilibrium occurs when marginal utility per rupee is equal across goods.
