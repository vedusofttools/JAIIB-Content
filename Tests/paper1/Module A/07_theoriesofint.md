### PART A – THEORY (1–10)
############################

## 1.
According to Classical theory, rate of interest is determined by:

A. Money supply  
B. Savings and investment  
C. Government spending  
D. Inflation only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Classical theory states that interest rate is determined by equilibrium between savings (supply of capital) and investment (demand for capital).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
In Classical theory, savings are primarily a function of:

A. Income  
B. Interest rate  
C. Money supply  
D. Taxes

Answer: B

Explanation:
Classical economists assume savings increase with higher interest rates.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Keynes’ Liquidity Preference theory states that interest rate is determined by:

A. Savings and investment  
B. Money supply and money demand  
C. Government spending  
D. Exports

Answer: B

Explanation:
Keynes argued that interest is a monetary phenomenon determined by demand and supply of money.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Liquidity preference arises due to:

A. Speculation, transaction, precaution motives  
B. Taxation  
C. Investment  
D. Production

Answer: A

Explanation:
Keynes identified three motives for holding money: transaction, precautionary, and speculative.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Transaction demand for money depends mainly on:

A. Interest rate  
B. Income level  
C. Inflation only  
D. Money supply

Answer: B

Explanation:
Transaction demand increases with income.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Speculative demand for money depends mainly on:

A. Income  
B. Interest rate  
C. GDP  
D. Tax

Answer: B

Explanation:
Speculative demand varies inversely with interest rate.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Loanable funds theory combines:

A. Classical and Keynesian views  
B. Only classical view  
C. Only monetary view  
D. Inflation theory

Answer: A

Explanation:
Loanable funds theory includes savings, investment, and hoarding, combining real and monetary factors.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
In money market equilibrium:

A. Savings = Investment  
B. Money demand = Money supply  
C. Exports = Imports  
D. GDP = GNP

Answer: B

Explanation:
Money market equilibrium occurs when demand for money equals supply.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
If money supply increases (other factors constant), interest rate:

A. Rises  
B. Falls  
C. Same  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher money supply reduces interest rate in liquidity preference theory.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Fisher’s theory relates interest rate to:

A. Inflation  
B. Savings  
C. Investment  
D. Money demand

Answer: A

Explanation:
Fisher equation: Nominal interest rate = Real interest rate + Expected inflation.
