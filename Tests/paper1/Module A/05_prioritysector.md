### PART A – THEORY (1–10)
############################

## 1.
Priority Sector Lending (PSL) norms are prescribed by:

A. SEBI  
B. RBI  
C. Ministry of Agriculture  
D. NABARD only

Answer: B

Explanation:
RBI prescribes PSL targets under its master directions to ensure credit flow to priority sectors.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Overall PSL target for Domestic Scheduled Commercial Banks is:

A. 30% of ANBC  
B. 35% of ANBC  
C. 40% of ANBC  
D. 50% of ANBC

Answer: C

Explanation:
Banks are required to lend 40% of Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC) or Credit Equivalent of Off-Balance Sheet Exposure (CEOBSE), whichever is higher, to priority sectors.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Target for Agriculture under PSL is:

A. 10% of ANBC  
B. 15% of ANBC  
C. 18% of ANBC  
D. 25% of ANBC

Answer: C

Explanation:
Agriculture sub-target is 18% of ANBC for domestic banks.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Within agriculture target, small and marginal farmers sub-target is:

A. 5%  
B. 8%  
C. 10%  
D. 12%

Answer: B

Explanation:
Banks must lend at least 8% of ANBC to Small and Marginal Farmers (SMFs).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Priority sector includes:

A. Large corporates  
B. Agriculture, MSME, education, housing  
C. Only agriculture  
D. Government borrowing

Answer: B

Explanation:
PSL covers agriculture, MSMEs, education, housing, renewable energy, etc.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC) includes:

A. Only advances  
B. Advances plus certain investments  
C. Deposits  
D. Capital

Answer: B

Explanation:
ANBC includes net bank credit plus eligible investments, as defined by RBI.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Kisan Credit Card (KCC) scheme is meant for:

A. Industrial loans  
B. Farmers for crop production  
C. Corporate credit  
D. Export finance

Answer: B

Explanation:
KCC provides working capital to farmers for crop production.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Crop loans under PSL fall under:

A. Indirect agriculture  
B. Direct agriculture

D. Housing

Answer: B

Explanation:
Loans directly given to farmers for crop production are direct agriculture.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
NABARD mainly supports:

A. Urban retail banking  
B. Agriculture and rural development  
C. Corporate banking  
D. Forex

Answer: B

Explanation:
NABARD promotes sustainable rural development and agriculture finance.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
If a bank fails to meet PSL target, it may need to:

A. Pay tax  
B. Contribute to RIDF  
C. Increase CRR  
D. Close branches

Answer: B

Explanation:
Shortfall in agriculture lending may require contribution to Rural Infrastructure Development Fund (RIDF) managed by NABARD.
