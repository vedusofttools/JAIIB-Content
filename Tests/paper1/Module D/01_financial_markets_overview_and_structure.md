# Financial Markets – Overview and Structure

## Meaning of Financial Market

A financial market is a platform where:
- Financial assets are bought and sold
- Funds flow from surplus units to deficit units
- Prices of financial instruments are determined

It connects:
- Savers (households, institutions)
- Borrowers (corporates, government)

Financial markets are essential for efficient capital allocation.

---

## Evolution of Financial Markets in India

### Pre-1991 Phase
- Highly regulated
- Administered interest rates
- Limited instruments
- Underdeveloped capital markets

---

### Post-1991 Reform Phase
- Financial liberalisation
- Market-determined interest rates
- Entry of private players
- Development of money and capital markets
- Introduction of new instruments (CP, CD, derivatives)

---

### Modern Phase
- Electronic trading platforms
- Screen-based trading
- Dematerialisation of securities
- Integration with global markets
- Increased retail participation

---

## Segments of Financial Markets

Financial markets are broadly classified into:

### Money Market
Deals in:
- Short-term funds (up to 1 year)

Purpose:
- Liquidity management
- Working capital financing

---

### Capital Market
Deals in:
- Long-term funds (more than 1 year)

Purpose:
- Capital formation
- Long-term investment

---

### Forex Market
Deals in:
- Foreign currencies
- Exchange rate determination

Purpose:
- International trade and payments

---

### Derivatives Market
Deals in:
- Contracts derived from underlying assets
- Risk management instruments

---

## Functions of Financial Markets

Financial markets perform critical economic functions:

### Mobilisation of Savings
- Collects savings from individuals and institutions
- Channels them into productive investments

---

### Capital Formation
- Supports infrastructure and industrial growth
- Encourages long-term investment

---

### Liquidity Provision
- Allows buying and selling of securities
- Converts assets into cash easily

---

### Risk Sharing and Diversification
- Enables hedging
- Spreads financial risk across participants

---

### Efficient Allocation of Resources
- Funds flow to sectors with higher returns
- Promotes economic efficiency

---

## Price Discovery

Price discovery refers to:
- The process of determining the price of a financial asset
- Based on demand and supply forces

In efficient markets:
- Prices reflect available information
- Investors make informed decisions
- Mispricing is corrected quickly

---

## Participants in Financial Markets

Key participants include:
- Banks
- Financial institutions
- Corporates
- Government
- Retail investors
- Institutional investors
- Foreign investors

Each participant plays a different role in liquidity and stability.

---

## Financial Market Infrastructure

Financial markets operate through:
- Stock exchanges
- Clearing corporations
- Depositories
- Payment and settlement systems

These ensure:
- Transparency
- Efficiency
- Safety of transactions

---

## Importance of Financial Markets in Economic Development

- Support GDP growth
- Promote investment
- Improve financial inclusion
- Facilitate monetary policy transmission
- Attract foreign investment

A developed financial market indicates economic maturity.

---

## Relevance for Banking

Banks participate in:
- Money market operations
- Government securities market
- Forex transactions
- Capital market distribution

Financial markets influence:
- Interest rates
- Liquidity
- Credit conditions

---

## Key Concepts to Remember

- Money market → short-term
- Capital market → long-term
- Price discovery → demand and supply mechanism
- Financial markets improve capital allocation

---

## Common Exam Traps

- Confusing money market with capital market
- Assuming price is fixed by regulator
- Treating financial markets as only stock exchanges
- Ignoring role of forex and derivatives markets
