# Fixed Income / Debt Markets

## Meaning of Fixed Income Market

The Fixed Income Market, also called the Debt Market or Bond Market, is a segment of the financial market where:

- Debt instruments are issued and traded
- Borrowers raise funds
- Investors earn fixed or predictable returns

It provides long-term and medium-term funds to:
- Government
- Corporates
- Financial institutions

---

## Types of Debt Instruments

- Government Securities (G-Secs)
- State Development Loans (SDLs)
- Corporate Bonds
- Debentures
- Inter-Corporate Deposits (ICDs)

---

# Government Securities (G-Secs)

## Meaning

Government Securities are:
- Debt instruments issued by Government of India
- Used to finance fiscal deficit
- Considered risk-free (sovereign guarantee)

---

## Types of Government Securities

### Treasury Bills
- Short-term (up to 1 year)

### Dated Securities
- Long-term (more than 1 year)
- Carry fixed interest (coupon)

---

## Coupon

Coupon is:
- The fixed interest rate paid on the face value
- Usually paid semi-annually

---

# Bond Valuation

## Meaning

Bond valuation determines:
- The present value of future cash flows
- Based on discounting coupon payments and principal

---

## Bond Price Relationship

- Bond price and interest rate move inversely
- If interest rate rises → bond price falls
- If interest rate falls → bond price rises

---

## Yield Concepts

### Coupon Yield
Coupon ÷ Face Value

### Current Yield
Annual Coupon ÷ Market Price

### Yield to Maturity (YTM)
Total return if bond held till maturity

---

# Bond Valuation Theorems

- Bond prices move inversely to interest rates
- Longer maturity bonds are more sensitive to interest rate changes
- Lower coupon bonds are more volatile

These are important exam concepts.

---

# Auction of Government Securities

Government securities are issued through:

- RBI auction mechanism

---

## Types of Auction

### Yield-Based Auction
- Investors bid yield
- Cut-off yield determined

### Price-Based Auction
- Investors bid price
- Cut-off price determined

---

## Participants

- Banks
- Primary Dealers
- Insurance companies
- Mutual funds

---

# Primary Dealers (PDs)

## Meaning

Primary Dealers are financial institutions authorised by RBI to:

- Underwrite government securities
- Participate in auctions
- Provide liquidity in secondary market

---

## Role

- Market making in G-Secs
- Ensure smooth borrowing for government
- Support debt market development

---

# FIMMDA

## Meaning

Fixed Income Money Market and Derivatives Association of India (FIMMDA)

---

## Role

- Provides pricing benchmarks
- Standardises market practices
- Publishes valuation guidelines
- Supports debt market development

---

# RBI Retail Direct Scheme (RDS)

## Launched

- Introduced in 2021

---

## Purpose

- Allow retail investors to:
    - Open gilt accounts
    - Invest directly in government securities

---

## Significance

- Democratises G-Sec market
- Encourages retail participation

---

# Corporate Bond Market

## Meaning

Corporate bonds are debt instruments issued by:

- Companies
- Financial institutions

---

## Features

- Higher yield than government securities
- Higher credit risk
- Credit rating required

---

## Importance

- Alternative source of corporate funding
- Reduces dependence on banks

---

# Inter-Corporate Deposits (ICDs)

## Meaning

Short-term loans given by one company to another.

---

## Features

- Unsecured
- Flexible tenure
- Based on mutual agreement

---

## Risk

- Higher credit risk
- Not regulated like bonds

---

# Importance of Debt Market

- Funds fiscal deficit
- Provides long-term capital
- Helps in interest rate transmission
- Provides safe investment avenue
- Supports infrastructure development

---

# Relevance for JAIIB

- Primary dealer role important

# Key Facts

- RBI Retail Direct Scheme: 2021
- Bond price moves inversely to interest rates
- Government securities considered risk-free
- Primary Dealers support government borrowing

- Confusing coupon rate with yield
- Ignoring inverse price-yield relationship
