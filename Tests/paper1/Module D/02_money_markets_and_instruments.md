# Money Markets and Instruments

## Meaning of Money Market

The Money Market is a segment of the financial market that deals in:
- Short-term funds
- Maturity up to 1 year

It is used for:
- Liquidity management
- Short-term borrowing and lending
- Monetary policy transmission

Major participants:
- RBI
- Scheduled Commercial Banks
- Primary Dealers
- Mutual Funds
- Financial Institutions
- Corporates

---

## Features of Money Market

- Short maturity (up to 1 year)
- High liquidity
- Low default risk (mostly)
- Large transaction size
- Wholesale market in nature

---

# Call Money, Notice Money and Term Money

## Call Money
- Maturity: 1 day (overnight)
- Used mainly by banks
- Interest rate: Call Rate

---

## Notice Money
- Maturity: 2 days to 14 days
- Notice required before repayment

---

## Term Money
- Maturity: 15 days to 1 year
- Used for relatively longer short-term funding

---

# Treasury Bills (T-Bills)

## Meaning
Treasury Bills are:
- Short-term government securities
- Issued by Government of India
- Issued at discount and redeemed at face value

---

## Types
- 91-day T-Bill
- 182-day T-Bill
- 364-day T-Bill

---

## Denomination
- Minimum: ₹25,000 (for retail via RBI Retail Direct)
- Generally traded in higher market lots

---

## Key Features
- No interest paid separately
- Issued at discount
- Considered risk-free (sovereign guarantee)

---

# Certificates of Deposit (CD)

## Meaning
Certificate of Deposit is:
- Negotiable money market instrument
- Issued by banks and financial institutions

---

## Issuers
- Scheduled Commercial Banks
- Select Financial Institutions

---

## Maturity
- Banks: 7 days to 1 year
- Financial Institutions: 1 year to 3 years

---

## Minimum Amount
- ₹5 lakh
- Issued in multiples of ₹5 lakh

---

## Nature
- Negotiable
- Issued in demat form
- Can be traded in secondary market

---

# Commercial Paper (CP)

## Meaning
Commercial Paper is:
- Unsecured short-term promissory note
- Issued by corporates, primary dealers, and financial institutions

---

## Purpose
- Meet working capital requirements
- Alternative to bank borrowing

---

## Maturity
- Minimum: 7 days
- Maximum: 1 year (365 days)

---

## Minimum Amount
- ₹5 lakh
- Issued in multiples of ₹5 lakh

---

## Requirements
- Must have minimum credit rating
- Issued in dematerialised form
- Issued at discount

---

## Nature
- Unsecured instrument
- Higher risk than T-Bills

---

# Repo (Repurchase Agreement)

## Meaning
Repo is:
- Short-term collateralised borrowing
- Sale of securities with agreement to repurchase

---

## Used By
- Banks
- RBI (monetary policy tool)

---

## Significance
- Injects liquidity
- Influences short-term interest rates
- Key tool of RBI

---

# Reverse Repo

- Banks park surplus funds with RBI
- RBI absorbs liquidity

---

# Tri-Party Repo (TREPS)

## Meaning
Tri-party repo involves:
- Borrower
- Lender
- Third-party intermediary (clearing corporation)

---

## Purpose
- Reduces counterparty risk
- Improves transparency
- Ensures collateral management

---

# Bill Rediscounting Scheme (BRDS)

## Meaning
- Banks discount bills of exchange
- Rediscount them with other banks or financial institutions

---

## Purpose
- Improve liquidity
- Promote trade finance
- Encourage bill culture

---

# Long-Term Repo Operations (LTRO)

## Introduced
- Introduced by RBI in 2020

---

## Purpose
- Provide long-term liquidity to banks
- Reduce borrowing cost
- Encourage lending to productive sectors

---

## Significance
- Used during economic slowdown
- Supports financial stability

---

# Importance of Money Market

- Maintains liquidity in banking system
- Assists monetary policy transmission
- Provides short-term funding
- Reduces systemic risk
- Supports smooth functioning of financial system

---

# Key Comparison – CP vs CD vs T-Bill

T-Bill:
- Issuer: Government
- Risk: Lowest
- Secured by sovereign

CD:
- Issuer: Banks/FIs
- Negotiable
- Secured instrument

CP:
- Issuer: Corporates
- Unsecured
- Higher risk than CD and T-Bill

---

# Key Facts for JAIIB

- CP minimum: ₹5 lakh
- CD minimum: ₹5 lakh
- CP maturity: 7 days to 1 year
- CD maturity (banks): 7 days to 1 year
- T-Bills: 91, 182, 364 days
- LTRO introduced in 2020
- Repo is collateralised borrowing

---

# Common Exam Traps

- Confusing CP with CD
- Assuming T-Bills pay periodic interest
- Treating CP as secured
- Ignoring denomination limits
- Confusing repo with reverse repo
