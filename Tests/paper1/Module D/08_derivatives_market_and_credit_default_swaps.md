# Derivatives Market and Credit Default Swaps (CDS)

## Meaning of Derivative

A Derivative is a financial contract whose value is derived from:

- An underlying asset
- Interest rate
- Currency
- Commodity
- Equity index

Derivatives are used primarily for:

- Hedging risk
- Speculation
- Arbitrage

---

## History of Derivatives

- Informal forward contracts existed centuries ago
- Modern exchange-traded derivatives began in 1970s
- Derivatives introduced in India in 2000
  (Index futures launched on NSE)

---

## Size of Derivatives Market

- Globally one of the largest financial markets
- In India, derivatives turnover exceeds cash market turnover
- NSE is among the largest derivatives exchanges globally

---

## Underlying Assets

Derivatives may be based on:

- Equity shares
- Stock indices
- Currencies
- Interest rates
- Commodities
- Credit instruments

---

## Exchange Traded vs OTC Derivatives

### Exchange Traded Derivatives

- Traded on recognised exchanges
- Standardised contracts
- Clearing corporation guarantees settlement
- Lower counterparty risk

---

### Over-The-Counter (OTC) Derivatives

- Privately negotiated contracts
- Customised terms
- Higher counterparty risk
- Used mainly by banks and institutions

---

## Participants in Derivatives Market

- Hedgers (risk reduction)
- Speculators (profit from price movements)
- Arbitrageurs (price difference exploitation)
- Banks and financial institutions

---

# Types of Derivatives

---

## Forward Contracts

- Private agreement
- Buy/sell asset at future date
- Price fixed today
- OTC contract
- Customised

---

## Futures Contracts

- Standardised contract
- Traded on exchange
- Marked-to-market daily
- Lower counterparty risk

---

## Options

Gives right but not obligation to:

- Buy (Call option)
- Sell (Put option)

Buyer pays premium to seller.

---

## Swaps

Agreement between two parties to:

- Exchange cash flows
- Based on different interest rates or currencies

Common types:
- Interest rate swap
- Currency swap

---

# Credit Default Swaps (CDS)

## Meaning

CDS is a derivative contract that:

- Transfers credit risk
- Protects lender against default

---

## Structure

- Protection buyer pays premium
- Protection seller compensates in case of default

---

## Purpose

- Hedge credit risk
- Improve risk management
- Increase market liquidity

---

## RBI Guidelines on CDS

- Permitted under regulatory framework
- Used mainly by institutional participants
- Risk management and capital adequacy norms apply
