## 1.
The money market primarily deals in:

A. Long-term securities  
B. Equity shares  
C. Short-term funds (up to 1 year)  
D. Foreign direct investment

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
The money market deals in short-term funds and instruments with maturity up to one year. It is used to manage liquidity and working capital needs. Long-term securities belong to the capital market.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
The main objective of the money market is to:

A. Finance infrastructure  
B. Provide long-term capital  
C. Ensure short-term liquidity  
D. Promote exports

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
Money market ensures liquidity adjustment in the financial system and facilitates short-term borrowing and lending.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Treasury Bills (T-Bills) are issued by:

A. RBI  
B. SEBI  
C. Government of India  
D. Commercial Banks

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
T-Bills are short-term government securities issued by the Government of India, but they are auctioned and managed by RBI.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Treasury Bills are issued at:

A. Par  
B. Premium  
C. Discount  
D. Floating rate only

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
T-Bills are zero-coupon instruments issued at discount and redeemed at face value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Which of the following is NOT a money market instrument?

A. Commercial Paper  
B. Certificate of Deposit  
C. Equity shares  
D. Call money

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
Equity shares are capital market instruments. CP, CD, and call money are short-term money market instruments.

## 10.
