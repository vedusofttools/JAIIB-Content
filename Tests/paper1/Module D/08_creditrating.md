SECTION A – Credit Rating Agencies (1–15)
------------------------------------------------------------

## 1.
A company plans to issue ₹500 crore debentures. Investors demand an independent assessment of default risk before subscribing. Which intermediary is most relevant?

A. Merchant banker  
B. Credit Rating Agency  
C. Registrar  
D. Stock broker

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Credit Rating Agencies (CRAs) assess the creditworthiness of debt instruments and issuers. Their rating helps investors evaluate probability of default. Merchant bankers manage issue; CRAs assess risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
A bond rated “AAA” indicates:

A. High default risk  
B. Moderate safety  
C. Highest degree of safety  
D. No liquidity

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
AAA represents highest credit quality with lowest default probability. Ratings decline in safety as they move downward (AA, A, BBB, etc.).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
If a company’s rating is downgraded from A to BBB, bond yield will likely:

A. Fall  
B. Rise  
C. Remain same  
D. Become fixed

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Downgrade implies higher perceived risk. Investors demand higher yield, leading to fall in bond price and rise in yield.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Which risk is primarily assessed by credit rating agencies?

A. Market risk  
B. Liquidity risk  
C. Credit/default risk  
D. Inflation risk

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
CRAs evaluate the issuer’s ability and willingness to repay debt obligations (default risk).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
If a ₹1000 bond with 8% coupon is downgraded, its price will:

A. Increase  
B. Decrease  
C. Remain unchanged  
D. Double

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Downgrade raises required yield; since bond prices move inversely with yield, price falls.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Credit rating does NOT indicate:

**Explanation:**  
Rating does not guarantee price performance or returns; it only assesses credit risk.

SECTION B – Mutual Funds (16–30)
------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
A mutual fund pools money from investors to invest in:

A. Single asset only  
B. Diversified securities portfolio  
C. Repo only  
D. Gold only

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Mutual funds diversify investments across equities, debt, or hybrid instruments to reduce risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
Net Asset Value (NAV) is calculated as:

A. Total income / shares  
B. (Assets – Liabilities) / Units outstanding  
C. Market price / EPS  
D. Dividend / Price

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
NAV = (Total Assets – Liabilities) / Total Units.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
If fund assets ₹200 crore and liabilities ₹20 crore with 10 crore units, NAV:

A. ₹15  
B. ₹18  
C. ₹20  
D. ₹25

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
NAV = (200 – 20) / 10 = 180 / 10 = ₹18.
