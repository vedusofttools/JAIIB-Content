## 1.
The debt market primarily deals in:

A. Equity shares  
B. Ownership instruments  
C. Fixed income securities  
D. Derivatives only

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
Debt market deals in fixed income instruments such as government bonds, corporate bonds, debentures, and treasury bills. Investors receive fixed or floating interest, unlike equity where ownership is transferred.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Government Securities (G-Secs) are issued by:

A. RBI  
B. SEBI  
C. Government of India  
D. Commercial Banks

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
G-Secs are issued by the Government of India to finance fiscal deficit. RBI manages issuance and auction as debt manager.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
A bond issued at par means:

A. Issued above face value  
B. Issued below face value  
C. Issued equal to face value  
D. Issued without coupon

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
When issue price equals face value, it is issued at par. Premium means above face value; discount means below.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
If interest rates in the market rise, bond prices:

A. Rise  
B. Fall  
C. Remain constant  
D. Double

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Bond prices and interest rates are inversely related. When market rates rise, existing bonds with lower coupons become less attractive, reducing their price.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Yield to Maturity (YTM) represents:

A. Coupon rate  
B. Total return if held till maturity  
C. Face value  
D. Dividend rate

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
YTM is the internal rate of return assuming the bond is held till maturity and coupons are reinvested at same rate.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.

Zero-coupon bonds do not pay periodic interest and are issued at a discount, redeemed at face value.
