## 1.
An MSME supplies goods worth ₹25 lakh to a large corporate with 90-day credit. The MSME needs immediate funds without increasing bank borrowing. The most suitable option is:

A. Term loan  
B. Factoring  
C. Equity issue  
D. Fixed deposit

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Factoring allows the MSME to sell receivables and obtain immediate liquidity without increasing balance sheet debt. Term loan increases liabilities; equity is long-term and impractical.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
A company enters into recourse factoring. After 120 days, the buyer defaults. Who ultimately bears the loss?

A. Factor  
B. RBI  
C. Seller  
D. Buyer’s bank

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
In recourse factoring, credit risk remains with the seller. The factor can recover the amount from the client.

## 3.

A. Domestic factoring  
B. Forfaiting  
C. Cash credit

**Explanation:**  
Forfaiting is suitable for medium-term export receivables and is typically non-recourse, eliminating credit and country risk.

## 4.
An MSME uploads a ₹10 lakh invoice on TReDS. Three financiers bid, and the lowest discount rate is 1.8%. What is the approximate amount MSME receives?

A. ₹9.82 lakh  
B. ₹9.80 lakh  
C. ₹9.75 lakh  
D. ₹9.50 lakh

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**  
Discount = 1.8% of 10,00,000 = ₹18,000  
Net received = 10,00,000 – 18,000 = ₹9,82,000.

## 25.
If receivable amount ₹20 lakh, factor fee 2% and advance 80%, net initial cash:

B. ₹16 lakh  
C. ₹19.6 lakh
