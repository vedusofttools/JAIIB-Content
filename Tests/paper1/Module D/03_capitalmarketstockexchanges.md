## 1.
A company wants to raise ₹800 crore for expansion with no repayment obligation. The most suitable instrument is:

A. Debentures  
B. Term loan  
C. Equity shares  
D. Commercial paper

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
Equity shares do not require repayment and do not create fixed financial liability.  
Debentures and loans require interest and repayment.  
Commercial paper is short-term and unsuitable for expansion funding.

## 2.
If a company's EPS is ₹20 and market price is ₹400, P/E ratio is:

A. 10  
B. 15  
C. 20  
D. 25

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
P/E = Market Price / EPS = 400 / 20 = 20.  
Higher P/E reflects higher growth expectations or overvaluation.

## 3.
A company with 10 lakh shares (₹10 face value) issues 1:4 bonus shares. Total shares after issue:

A. 12 lakh  
B. 12.5 lakh  
C. 14 lakh  
D. 15 lakh

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
1:4 means 1 bonus share for every 4 shares held.  
Bonus shares = 10,00,000 / 4 = 2,50,000  
Total shares = 10,00,000 + 2,50,000 = 12,50,000 (12.5 lakh).  
No fresh capital is raised.

## 4.
If interest rates rise sharply, equity valuation generally:

A. Improves  
B. Declines  
C. Remains unaffected  
D. Doubles

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Higher interest rates increase discount rate used in valuation models (DCF), reducing present value of future earnings. Hence equity prices fall.
