# Credit Rating Agencies and Mutual Funds

# Credit Rating

## Meaning of Credit Rating

Credit Rating is:

- An independent assessment
- Of creditworthiness of a borrower
- Or a debt instrument

It indicates the ability to repay debt on time.

---

## History of Credit Rating

- Originated in USA
- Introduced in India in late 1980s
- CRISIL established in 1987 (India’s first CRA)

---

## Credit Rating Agencies (CRAs) in India

- CRISIL
- ICRA
- CARE
- India Ratings
- Others registered with SEBI

CRAs are regulated by SEBI.

---

## Characteristics of Credit Rating

- Forward-looking opinion
- Based on detailed analysis
- Expressed through rating symbols
- Subject to periodic review

---

## Importance of Credit Rating

- Helps investors assess risk
- Reduces information asymmetry
- Improves investor confidence
- Facilitates capital market development

---

## Factors Considered in Credit Rating

- Financial performance
- Debt servicing ability
- Management quality
- Industry outlook
- Business risk
- Economic environment

---

## Process of Credit Rating

1. Request by issuer
2. Information collection
3. Analysis and evaluation
4. Rating committee decision
5. Communication and publication
6. Continuous monitoring

---

## Credit Rating Symbols

Investment Grade:
- AAA (Highest safety)
- AA
- A
- BBB

Below Investment Grade:
- BB
- B
- C
- D (Default)

---

## Rating Outlook

Indicates future direction:
- Positive
- Stable
- Negative

---

## Fees for Credit Rating

- Paid by issuer
- Based on size and complexity of issue

---

# Credit Scoring

## Meaning

Credit Score is:

- Numerical score
- Reflecting individual’s credit behaviour

Used mainly for retail lending decisions.

---

## Credit Information Companies (CICs) in India

- CIBIL
- Experian
- Equifax
- CRIF High Mark

Regulated by RBI under Credit Information Companies Act, 2005.

---

## Difference Between Credit Rating and Credit Score

Credit Rating:
- For companies/instruments
- Alphabetical symbols
- Used in capital market

Credit Score:
- For individuals
- Numerical score
- Used in retail lending

---

# Mutual Funds

## Meaning

A Mutual Fund is:

- A pooled investment vehicle
- Managed by professional fund managers
- Invests in diversified portfolio

Regulated by SEBI.

---

## Evolution of Mutual Funds in India

- Unit Trust of India (UTI) – 1963
- Entry of public sector funds
- Private sector entry in 1993
- Rapid expansion post liberalisation

---

## Functions of Mutual Funds

- Mobilise savings
- Diversify investment
- Professional fund management
- Provide liquidity

---

## Classification of Mutual Funds

### Based on Structure
- Open-ended funds
- Close-ended funds

---

### Based on Investment Objective
- Equity funds
- Debt funds
- Hybrid funds
- Index funds
- Sectoral funds

---

## Management of Mutual Funds

- Sponsor
- Trustee
- Asset Management Company (AMC)
- Custodian

---

## Net Asset Value (NAV)

NAV = (Total Assets – Liabilities) / Number of Units

NAV indicates per-unit value.

---

## Expense Ratio

- Annual operating cost of fund
- Expressed as percentage of assets

Lower expense ratio improves investor returns.

---

## Load / No-Load Funds

Load:
- Entry or exit charge

No-Load:
- No entry or exit fee

---

## Risk Depiction – Riskometer

SEBI introduced Riskometer to:

- Show risk level
- Help investor decision making

---

## New Fund Offer (NFO)

- Initial offer of mutual fund units
- Similar to IPO in equity market

---

## Risks Associated with Mutual Funds

- Market risk
- Credit risk
- Interest rate risk
- Liquidity risk

---

## Alternative Investment Funds (AIF)

AIFs are privately pooled funds:

- Venture capital funds
- Private equity funds
- Hedge funds

Regulated by SEBI.

---

# Importance of Mutual Funds

- Promotes financial inclusion
- Channels savings to capital markets
- Encourages retail participation

---

- CRISIL established: 1987

- Assuming AAA means zero risk
