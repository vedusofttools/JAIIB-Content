# Capital Markets and Stock Exchanges

## Meaning of Capital Market

The Capital Market is a segment of the financial market where:

- Long-term funds are raised
- Securities with maturity above 1 year are traded
- Capital formation takes place

It facilitates funding for:
- Corporates
- Government
- Infrastructure projects

---

## Components of Capital Market

Capital Market is divided into:

- Primary Market
- Secondary Market

---

# Primary Market

## Meaning

The Primary Market is where:

- New securities are issued
- Funds are raised directly from investors

---

## Types of Capital Issues

### Initial Public Offer (IPO)
- First issue of shares by a company to the public

---

### Follow-on Public Offer (FPO)
- Additional shares issued by already listed company

---

### Rights Issue
- Offered to existing shareholders

---

### Bonus Issue
- Free shares to existing shareholders

---

### Qualified Institutional Placement (QIP)
- Issue of shares to qualified institutional buyers
- Faster capital raising method

---

## Applications Supported by Blocked Amount (ASBA)

ASBA is:

- Application method for IPO
- Funds remain blocked in bank account
- Debited only if shares allotted

Improves efficiency and transparency.

---

## Eligibility Norms for Capital Issues

Companies must comply with:

- SEBI regulations
- Disclosure requirements
- Minimum track record norms
- Financial eligibility criteria

---

## Intermediaries in Primary Market

- Merchant bankers
- Underwriters
- Registrars
- Bankers to the issue
- Brokers
- Credit rating agencies (for debt issues)

---

# Secondary Market

## Meaning

Secondary Market is where:

- Existing securities are bought and sold
- Investors trade among themselves

Funds do not go to issuing company.

---

## Stock Exchanges in India

Major stock exchanges:

- National Stock Exchange (NSE)
- Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)

Both operate through:

- Screen-based electronic trading
- Dematerialised settlement

---

## Functions of Stock Exchanges

- Provide liquidity
- Facilitate price discovery
- Ensure transparent trading
- Protect investor interests

---

# Financial Instruments in Secondary Market

- Equity shares
- Preference shares
- Debentures
- Bonds
- Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)
- Derivatives (futures and options)

---

# Regulatory Requirements by SEBI for Corporate Debt Securities

- Mandatory credit rating
- Disclosure of risk factors
- Listing compliance
- Periodic reporting
- Debenture trustee appointment

---

# Commonly Used Terms in Capital Market

## Face Value
Nominal value of share.

---

## Market Value
Current trading price.

---

## Dividend
Profit distributed to shareholders.

---

## Book Building
Price discovery process during IPO.

---

## Oversubscription
Demand exceeds shares offered.

---

## Underwriting
Guarantee of subscription by underwriter.

---

## Listing
Admission of securities to stock exchange.

---

# Role of SEBI

- Regulates capital markets
- Protects investors
- Prevents insider trading
- Regulates intermediaries
- Enforces disclosure norms

SEBI Act enacted in 1992.

---

# Importance of Capital Market

- Promotes industrial growth
- Facilitates investment
- Mobilises long-term savings
- Supports economic development
