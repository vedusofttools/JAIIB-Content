## SECTION A – Venture Capital (1–10)

## 1.
A technology startup with no stable cash flows but high growth potential seeks funding without fixed repayment obligation. The most suitable source is:

A. Term loan  
B. Venture capital  
C. Hire purchase  
D. Factoring

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Venture capital (VC) finances high-risk, high-growth startups in exchange for equity participation. Unlike loans, it does not require fixed repayment, making it suitable for startups with uncertain cash flows.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Venture capital investors primarily earn returns through:

A. Fixed interest  
B. Dividend guarantee  
C. Capital appreciation at exit  
D. Repo transactions

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
VCs invest in equity and exit via IPO, strategic sale, or buyback. Returns come from increase in company valuation, not fixed interest.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
A VC invests ₹5 crore for 25% equity. If company later valued at ₹40 crore, value of VC stake:

A. ₹5 crore  
B. ₹8 crore  
C. ₹10 crore  
D. ₹20 crore

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
25% of ₹40 crore = ₹10 crore.  
VC earns ₹5 crore capital gain.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Which stage financing involves funding prototype development?

A. Expansion stage  
B. Seed stage  
C. Buyout stage  
D. Maturity stage

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Seed capital is earliest stage funding for idea/prototype development before commercial production.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Venture capital differs from bank loan because VC:

A. Requires collateral  
B. Takes equity risk  
C. Guarantees repayment  
D. Is short-term

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
VC investors assume business risk and share ownership; banks require repayment with interest.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
If startup fails, VC’s recovery is:

A. Guaranteed  
B. Fixed interest  
C. Limited to equity value  
D. RBI-supported

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
VC investment is equity-based; if company fails, investor may lose entire investment.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
An exit through IPO allows VC to:

A. Increase debt

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.

## 9.

A. ₹20 crore  
B. ₹22 crore

## 10.

## SECTION B – Leasing (11–20)

In a finance lease, ownership risk is substantially borne by:

A. Lessor  
B. Lessee  
C. RBI  
D. SEBI

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Finance lease transfers most risks and rewards of ownership to lessee.

Excess over asset cost reflects interest and profit of lessor.

## SECTION C – Hire Purchase (21–30)

## 21.
In hire purchase, ownership transfers:

A. At agreement signing  
B. At final installment payment  
C. Immediately  
D. Never

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Ownership transfers only after last installment is paid.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 22.
If asset cost ₹10 lakh, down payment ₹2 lakh, balance paid in 4 equal installments (ignoring interest), installment amount:

A. ₹1.5 lakh  
B. ₹2 lakh  
C. ₹2.5 lakh  
D. ₹3 lakh

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Balance = 10 – 2 = 8 lakh  
8 / 4 = ₹2 lakh per installment.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 23.
Difference between lease and hire purchase is:

A. Ownership timing  
B. Interest rate  
C. Repo linkage  
D. Tax only

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**  
In lease, ownership stays with lessor; in hire purchase, ownership transfers after final payment.
