## 1.
Under FEMA, the primary objective of India’s forex framework is to:

A. Strictly control all foreign exchange  
B. Facilitate external trade and orderly development of forex market  
C. Fix exchange rate permanently  
D. Eliminate capital flows

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
FEMA, 1999 replaced FERA and shifted from a control-based regime to a management-based regime. Its objective is to facilitate external trade and payments and promote orderly development of the foreign exchange market.

## 2.
The authority empowered to regulate foreign exchange transactions in India is:

A. SEBI  
B. RBI  
C. Ministry of Commerce  
D. IRDAI

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Under FEMA, RBI regulates forex transactions and authorizes banks as Authorized Dealers (ADs).

## 29.
If rupee moves from ₹78/$ to ₹83/$, percentage depreciation approximately:

A. 4%  
B. 5%  
C. 6%  
D. 8%

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
Change = 83 − 78 = 5  
Percentage = (5 / 78) × 100 ≈ 6.4% (approx. 6%).
