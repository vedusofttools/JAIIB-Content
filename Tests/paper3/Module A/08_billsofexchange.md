# Bills of Exchange
Total Questions: 30  
Each Question Carries 1 Mark  
Choose the most appropriate answer.  
Detailed explanation provided after each question.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 1.
A Bill of Exchange is:

A. An unconditional order in writing  
B. A conditional promise to pay  
C. A receipt of goods  
D. A bank statement

Answer: A

Explanation:
A Bill of Exchange is an unconditional order in writing directing a person to pay a certain sum of money to another person or bearer.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
The person who draws the bill is called:

A. Drawee  
B. Payee  
C. Drawer  
D. Endorsee

Answer: C

Explanation:
Drawer is the person who prepares and issues the bill.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
The person on whom the bill is drawn is called:

A. Drawer  
B. Drawee  
C. Payee  
D. Holder

Answer: B

Explanation:
Drawee is the person directed to pay the amount mentioned in the bill.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
When the drawee signs the bill, it becomes:

A. Promissory Note  
B. Accepted Bill  
C. Dishonoured Bill  
D. Cancelled Bill

Answer: B

Explanation:
When drawee signs the bill agreeing to pay, it becomes an accepted bill.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
The time period after which bill becomes due is called:

A. Term  
B. Tenure  
C. Maturity  
D. Endorsement

Answer: C

Explanation:
Maturity is the date on which bill becomes payable.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Days of grace allowed on a bill are:

A. 2 days  
B. 3 days  
C. 5 days  
D. 7 days

Answer: B

Explanation:
Three days of grace are added to the due date to determine maturity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
If a bill is payable 2 months after date, and drawn on 1st Jan, maturity date will be:

A. 1st March  
B. 4th March  
C. 3rd March  
D. 2nd March

Answer: B

Explanation:
2 months → 1st March  
Add 3 days grace → 4th March.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Discounting of a bill means:

A. Cancelling the bill  
B. Selling the bill to bank before maturity  
C. Refusing payment  
D. Renewing bill

Answer: B

Explanation:
Holder can sell the bill to bank before maturity and receive amount after deducting discount.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
If bill is dishonoured, noting charges are paid by:

A. Drawer  
B. Drawee  
C. Payee  
D. Bank

Answer: B

Explanation:
Drawee (acceptor) is liable for noting charges upon dishonour.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Promissory Note differs from Bill of Exchange because it contains:

A. Order to pay  
B. Promise to pay  
C. Three parties  
D. Acceptance

Answer: B

Explanation:
Promissory Note contains unconditional promise, while bill contains order.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
When bill is retained till maturity, it is called:

A. Discounted Bill  
B. Endorsed Bill  
C. Bill sent for collection  
D. Dishonoured Bill

Answer: C

Explanation:
Bill may be sent to bank for collection instead of discounting.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Endorsement of bill means:

A. Cancellation  
B. Transferring rights to another person  
C. Refusal of payment  
D. Extending due date

Answer: B

Explanation:
Endorsement transfers ownership of bill to another person.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
If a bill of ₹10,000 is discounted at 12% for 3 months, discount is:

A. ₹300  
B. ₹600  
C. ₹900  
D. ₹1,200

Answer: A

Explanation:
Discount = 10,000 × 12% × 3/12  
= 10,000 × 0.12 × 0.25 = ₹300.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
Journal entry when bill is accepted by debtor:

A. Debit Bills Receivable, Credit Debtor  
B. Debit Debtor, Credit Bills Receivable  
C. Debit Cash, Credit Debtor  
D. Debit Debtor, Credit Cash

Answer: A

Explanation:
When bill is accepted, debtor account is replaced by Bills Receivable.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
If bill is dishonoured, entry in books of drawer is:

A. Debit Debtor, Credit Bills Receivable  
B. Debit Bills Receivable, Credit Debtor  
C. Debit Cash, Credit Debtor  
D. Debit Debtor, Credit Sales

Answer: A

Explanation:
On dishonour, debtor liability revives.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
Renewal of bill means:

A. Cancelling bill  
B. Extending time period by cancelling old and issuing new bill  
C. Discounting  
D. Endorsing

Answer: B

Explanation:
Old bill cancelled and new bill drawn for extended period.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
A bill payable on demand is payable:

A. After 3 days  
B. Immediately  
C. After one month  
D. After grace period

Answer: B

Explanation:
No grace days are allowed on demand bills.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Bills Receivable is shown in Balance Sheet under:

A. Liabilities  
B. Assets  
C. Income  
D. Capital

Answer: B

Explanation:
Bills Receivable is asset because it represents amount receivable.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
Bills Payable is shown as:

A. Asset  
B. Liability  
C. Income  
D. Expense

Answer: B

Explanation:
Bills Payable represents obligation to pay.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
If due date falls on public holiday, bill becomes payable on:

A. Same day  
B. Previous working day  
C. Next working day  
D. Ignored

Answer: B

Explanation:
If maturity falls on public holiday, bill is payable on preceding working day.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 21.
Accommodation bill is drawn:

A. For genuine trade transaction  
B. Without trade transaction  
C. Only for banks  
D. Only for government

Answer: B

Explanation:
Accommodation bill is drawn without actual trade, for mutual financial help.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 22.
If drawer discounts bill and it is dishonoured, liability falls on:

A. Bank only  
B. Drawee only  
C. Drawer  
D. Government

Answer: C

Explanation:
Drawer must compensate bank if bill dishonoured.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 23.
A bill drawn after sight becomes payable:

A. From date of drawing  
B. From date of acceptance  
C. Immediately  
D. After discount

Answer: B

Explanation:
After sight means after acceptance date.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 24.
Journal entry for discounting bill is:

A. Debit Cash, Debit Discount, Credit Bills Receivable  
B. Debit Bills Receivable, Credit Cash  
C. Debit Cash, Credit Debtor  
D. Debit Discount, Credit Cash

Answer: A

Explanation:
Cash received minus discount; Bills Receivable credited fully.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 25.
If bill of ₹20,000 is endorsed to creditor, entry is:

A. Debit Creditor, Credit Bills Receivable  
B. Debit Bills Receivable, Credit Creditor  
C. Debit Cash, Credit Creditor  
D. Debit Creditor, Credit Cash

Answer: A

Explanation:
Liability to creditor reduced; Bills Receivable transferred.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 26.
On maturity, if bill is honoured, entry is:

A. Debit Cash, Credit Bills Receivable  
B. Debit Debtor, Credit Bills Receivable  
C. Debit Bills Receivable, Credit Cash  
D. Debit Cash, Credit Debtor

Answer: A

Explanation:
Cash received and Bills Receivable closed.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 27.
Noting charges are:

A. Income  
B. Asset  
C. Expense recoverable from drawee  
D. Capital

Answer: C

Explanation:
Noting charges paid by holder but recoverable from drawee.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 28.
Promissory Note has:

A. Three parties  
B. Two parties  
C. Four parties  
D. No parties

Answer: B

Explanation:
Maker and Payee only.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 29.
A bill drawn for 30 days on 10th April matures on:

A. 10th May  
B. 13th May  
C. 12th May  
D. 11th May

Answer: B

Explanation:
30 days from 10th April → 10th May  
Add 3 days grace → 13th May.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 30.
Main advantage of bill of exchange is:

A. Cash payment only  
B. Legal evidence of debt  
C. No need of accounting  
D. No maturity date

Answer: B

Explanation:
Bill of Exchange is legally enforceable document evidencing debt.
