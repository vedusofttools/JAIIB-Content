# Bank Reconciliation Statement
Total Questions: 30  
Each Question Carries 1 Mark  
Choose the most appropriate answer.  
Detailed explanation provided after each question.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 1.
The primary purpose of preparing a Bank Reconciliation Statement (BRS) is to:

A. Calculate profit  
B. Detect fraud only  
C. Reconcile differences between Cash Book and Pass Book balances  
D. Close ledger accounts

Answer: C

Explanation:
BRS is prepared to reconcile the balance as per Cash Book (bank column) with the balance as per Pass Book due to timing differences or errors.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Cheques issued but not yet presented for payment will:

A. Increase Pass Book balance  
B. Decrease Pass Book balance  
C. Increase Cash Book balance  
D. Decrease Cash Book balance

Answer: D

Explanation:
When cheque is issued, Cash Book (bank column) is credited immediately, reducing its balance. Pass Book will reduce only when cheque is presented.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Cheques deposited but not yet collected will:

A. Increase Pass Book balance  
B. Decrease Pass Book balance  
C. Increase Cash Book balance  
D. Decrease Cash Book balance

Answer: C

Explanation:
When cheque is deposited, Cash Book is debited immediately. Pass Book will increase only when bank actually collects it.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Bank charges entered in Pass Book but not in Cash Book will:

A. Increase Cash Book balance  
B. Decrease Cash Book balance  
C. Increase Pass Book balance  
D. Have no effect

Answer: B

Explanation:
Bank charges reduce balance as per Pass Book. To reconcile, they must be credited in Cash Book, reducing Cash Book balance.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Interest credited by bank but not recorded in Cash Book will:

A. Increase Cash Book balance  
B. Increase Pass Book balance  
C. Decrease Pass Book balance  
D. Decrease Cash Book balance

Answer: B

Explanation:
Interest credited increases balance as per Pass Book but Cash Book remains unchanged until recorded.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
If balance as per Cash Book is ₹50,000 (debit) and cheques issued but not presented ₹5,000, balance as per Pass Book will be:

A. ₹55,000  
B. ₹45,000  
C. ₹50,000  
D. ₹60,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
Cheques issued reduce Cash Book immediately but not Pass Book.  
Pass Book balance = 50,000 + 5,000 = ₹55,000.
