## 1.
The fundamental accounting equation under the double entry system is:

A. Profit = Income – Expense  
B. Assets = Liabilities + Capital  
C. Cash = Assets – Liabilities  
D. Income = Assets – Expenses

Answer: B

Explanation:
The double entry system is based on the accounting equation:
Assets = Liabilities + Capital.
Every transaction affects at least two accounts so that this equation remains balanced.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Under the Business Entity Concept, the capital introduced by the owner is treated as:

A. Income  
B. Liability of the business  
C. Expense  
D. Asset

Answer: B

Explanation:
Business is treated as separate from the owner. Hence, capital introduced is treated as a liability of the business towards the owner.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
When goods are purchased for cash, which accounts are affected?

A. Purchase and Cash  
B. Cash and Capital  
C. Sales and Cash  
D. Purchase and Capital

Answer: A

Explanation:
Purchase (expense) increases → Debit.  
Cash (asset) decreases → Credit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
The rule “Debit what comes in, Credit what goes out” applies to:

A. Personal Accounts  
B. Nominal Accounts  
C. Real Accounts  
D. Capital Accounts

Answer: C

Explanation:
Real accounts relate to assets. When asset comes in → Debit; when it goes out → Credit.

------------------------------------------------------------

Salary is an expense → Debit.  
Cash decreases → Credit.
