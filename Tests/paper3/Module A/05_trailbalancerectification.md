# Trial Balance and Rectification of Errors
Total Questions: 30  
Each Question Carries 1 Mark  
Choose the most appropriate answer.  
Detailed explanation provided after each question.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 1.
The primary objective of preparing a Trial Balance is to:

A. Ascertain profit or loss  
B. Check arithmetical accuracy of ledger posting  
C. Prepare cash flow statement  
D. Detect all types of errors

Answer: B

Explanation:
Trial Balance ensures total debits equal total credits. It checks arithmetical accuracy but does not guarantee absence of all errors.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Trial Balance is prepared from:

A. Journal  
B. Cash Book  
C. Ledger balances  
D. Balance Sheet

Answer: C

Explanation:
Balances of all ledger accounts are extracted and listed in Trial Balance.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Which of the following errors will NOT be disclosed by Trial Balance?

A. Error in totaling  
B. Posting on wrong side  
C. Complete omission of a transaction  
D. Unequal debit and credit

Answer: C

Explanation:
If a transaction is completely omitted, both debit and credit are missing, so Trial Balance will still tally.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
If the Trial Balance shows excess debit of ₹5,000, it is temporarily transferred to:

A. Capital Account  
B. Profit & Loss Account  
C. Suspense Account  
D. Cash Account

Answer: C

Explanation:
Difference in Trial Balance is temporarily placed in Suspense Account until errors are rectified.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
An error of commission occurs when:

A. Transaction is omitted  
B. Wrong amount is posted  
C. Transaction is recorded twice  
D. Capital is wrongly calculated

Answer: B

Explanation:
Error of commission arises due to wrong posting, wrong amount, or wrong side but correct account.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
An error of omission means:

A. Partial recording  
B. No recording of transaction  
C. Double recording  
D. Wrong balancing

Answer: B

Explanation:
Complete omission means transaction is not recorded at all.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
If ₹2,000 paid for repairs is wrongly debited to Machinery Account, it is:

A. Error of omission  
B. Error of principle  
C. Error of commission  
D. Compensating error

Answer: B

Explanation:
Capital expenditure and revenue expenditure are confused. This violates accounting principle.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Compensating errors are those which:

A. Increase difference  
B. Decrease profit  
C. Cancel the effect of another error  
D. Affect only assets

Answer: C

Explanation:
Two or more errors offset each other, so Trial Balance still tallies.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
If sales of ₹10,000 are recorded as ₹1,000, it is:

A. Error of principle  
B. Error of commission  
C. Error of omission  
D. Compensating error

Answer: B

Explanation:
Wrong amount recorded is an error of commission.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Suspense Account balance is shown in:

A. Trading Account  
B. Balance Sheet  
C. Journal  
D. Cash Book

Answer: B

Explanation:
Until errors are rectified, Suspense Account appears in Balance Sheet.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
If purchase of ₹5,000 is not posted to supplier’s account, it is:

A. Error of omission (partial)  
B. Error of commission  
C. Error of principle  
D. No error

Answer: A

Explanation:
Transaction recorded in one place but omitted in another is partial omission.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Rectification of errors after preparing Trial Balance requires:

A. No entry  
B. Adjusting entry  
C. Capital adjustment  
D. Cash adjustment

Answer: B

Explanation:
Rectification is done by passing journal entries to correct affected accounts.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
If excess credit of ₹2,000 in Sales Account is discovered before Trial Balance, correction is:

A. Debit Sales ₹2,000  
B. Credit Sales ₹2,000  
C. Debit Suspense ₹2,000  
D. Ignore

Answer: A

Explanation:
Excess credit must be reduced by debiting Sales Account.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
If Trial Balance tallies, it means:

A. No errors exist  
B. Only arithmetic accuracy is confirmed  
C. Profit is correct  
D. Ledger is complete

Answer: B

Explanation:
Tallying only ensures debit equals credit; errors like omission may still exist.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
Rectification entry for crediting Ram ₹500 instead of ₹5,000 is:

A. Debit Ram ₹4,500  
B. Credit Ram ₹4,500  
C. Debit Suspense ₹4,500  
D. Ignore

Answer: A

Explanation:
Ram was under-credited by ₹4,500. To correct, debit Ram ₹4,500.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
If an asset purchase is wrongly debited to Purchases Account, it affects:

A. Profit  
B. Liability  
C. Income only  
D. Cash only

Answer: A

Explanation:
Purchases is expense; wrongly debiting increases expense and reduces profit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
One-sided error affects:

A. One account only  
B. Two accounts equally  
C. No account  
D. Capital only

Answer: A

Explanation:
If only one side is affected, Trial Balance will not tally.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
If difference in Trial Balance is placed on debit side, it means:

A. Debit total is short  
B. Credit total is short  
C. No error  
D. Profit is high

Answer: B

Explanation:
If difference placed on debit side, credit side is short.

## 19.
An error detected after final accounts are prepared is corrected through:

A. Trading Account  
B. Profit & Loss Adjustment Account  
C. Cash Book  
D. Sales Book

After final accounts, rectification affects prior period and may require P&L adjustment.

A. Error of omission  
B. Error of principle

Correct side but wrong account → Error of commission.
