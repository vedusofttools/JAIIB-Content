# Books of Accounts and Journalising
Total Questions: 30  
Each Question Carries 1 Mark  
Choose the most appropriate answer.  
Detailed explanation provided after each question.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 1.
The book in which transactions are first recorded in chronological order is called:

A. Ledger  
B. Trial Balance  
C. Journal  
D. Cash Book

Answer: C

Explanation:
The Journal is the book of original entry where transactions are recorded first in chronological order before posting to ledger.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Posting refers to:

A. Recording transactions in Journal  
B. Transferring journal entries to ledger accounts  
C. Preparing Trial Balance  
D. Closing accounts

Answer: B

Explanation:
Posting means transferring debit and credit amounts from the journal to respective ledger accounts.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
A Cash Book is a:

A. Subsidiary book only  
B. Ledger only  
C. Both Journal and Ledger  
D. Trial Balance

Answer: C

Explanation:
Cash Book records cash transactions and also serves as a ledger account for cash and bank.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Purchase Book records:

A. All purchases  
B. Only cash purchases  
C. Only credit purchases of goods  
D. Purchase of assets

Answer: C

Explanation:
Purchase Book records only credit purchases of goods meant for resale. Cash purchases go to Cash Book.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
When goods are sold on credit to Ram for ₹10,000, the journal entry is:

A. Debit Sales, Credit Ram  
B. Debit Ram, Credit Sales  
C. Debit Cash, Credit Sales  
D. Debit Sales, Credit Cash

Answer: B

Explanation:
Ram (debtor) increases → Debit.  
Sales increases → Credit.

## 11.

A. Debit Cash, Credit Salary  
B. Debit Salary, Credit Cash  
C. Debit Capital, Credit Cash
