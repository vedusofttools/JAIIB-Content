## 1.
Ratio analysis is mainly used for:

A. Recording transactions  
B. Interpreting financial statements  
C. Preparing journal entries  
D. Auditing accounts

Answer: B

Explanation:
Ratio analysis helps interpret financial statements by establishing relationships between financial figures.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Current Ratio measures:

A. Profitability  
B. Liquidity  
C. Solvency  
D. Efficiency

Answer: B

Explanation:
Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities, indicating short-term liquidity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Ideal Current Ratio is generally considered:

A. 1:1  
B. 2:1  
C. 3:1  
D. 1:2

Answer: B

Explanation:
Traditionally, 2:1 is considered satisfactory for liquidity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Quick Ratio excludes:

A. Cash  
B. Debtors  
C. Inventory  
D. Bank balance

Answer: C

Explanation:
Quick assets exclude inventory as it is less liquid.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Debt-Equity Ratio measures:

A. Profitability  
B. Liquidity  
C. Long-term solvency  
D. Efficiency

Answer: C

Explanation:
Debt-Equity Ratio = Total Debt / Shareholders’ Equity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Gross Profit Ratio indicates:

A. Operational efficiency  
B. Liquidity  
C. Solvency  
D. Capital structure

Answer: A

Explanation:
Gross Profit Ratio = Gross Profit / Net Sales × 100.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Net Profit Ratio measures:

A. Liquidity  
B. Overall profitability  
C. Capital adequacy  
D. Working capital

Answer: B

Explanation:
Net Profit Ratio = Net Profit / Net Sales × 100.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Inventory Turnover Ratio measures:

A. Profit margin  
B. Speed of inventory movement  
C. Solvency  
D. Capital structure

Answer: B

Explanation:
Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Return on Capital Employed (ROCE) indicates:

A. Liquidity  
B. Long-term profitability  
C. Working capital  
D. Sales volume

Answer: B

Explanation:
ROCE = EBIT / Capital Employed × 100.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Proprietary Ratio measures:

A. Debt proportion  
B. Equity proportion in total assets  
C. Liquidity  
D. Inventory

Answer: B

Explanation:
Proprietary Ratio = Shareholders’ Funds / Total Assets.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
Interest Coverage Ratio is:

A. EBIT / Interest  
B. Interest / EBIT  
C. Profit / Sales  
D. Debt / Equity

Answer: A

Explanation:
Measures ability to pay interest obligations.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Working Capital Turnover Ratio is:

A. Sales / Working Capital  
B. Working Capital / Sales  
C. COGS / Inventory  
D. Profit / Capital

Answer: A

Explanation:
Indicates efficiency of working capital usage.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
High Current Ratio may indicate:

A. Poor liquidity  
B. Idle funds  
C. High profit  
D. Low assets

Answer: B

Explanation:
Excessive liquidity may indicate inefficient use of funds.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
If Debt-Equity Ratio is high, financial risk is:

A. Low  
B. Moderate  
C. High  
D. Nil

Answer: C

Explanation:
Higher debt increases financial risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
Ratio analysis is limited because:

A. It uses financial statements  
B. It ignores qualitative factors  
C. It shows trends  
D. It measures performance

Answer: B

Explanation:
Ratios do not capture qualitative aspects like management quality.

## 30.
Working Capital ₹2,00,000; Current Ratio 2:1. Current Liabilities?

A. ₹1,00,000  
B. ₹2,00,000  
C. ₹4,00,000  
D. ₹50,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
Let CL = x  
CA = 2x  
Working Capital = CA – CL = x  
Given WC = 2,00,000  
So x = 1,00,000.
