############################
### PART A – Conceptual (1–15)
############################

## 1.
Capital structure refers to:

A. Total assets of a firm  
B. Mix of debt and equity financing  
C. Profit level  
D. Working capital

Answer: B

Explanation:
Capital structure represents the proportion of debt and equity used to finance the company’s operations and assets.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Financial leverage arises due to:

A. Use of equity  
B. Use of retained earnings  
C. Use of fixed cost debt financing  
D. Use of reserves

Answer: C

Explanation:
Financial leverage occurs when fixed interest-bearing debt is used, magnifying returns to equity shareholders.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
A highly leveraged firm has:

A. High equity  
B. High debt  
C. Low assets  
D. No liabilities

Answer: B

Explanation:
High leverage means greater proportion of debt in capital structure.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Optimal capital structure is achieved when:

A. Debt is maximum  
B. Equity is maximum  
C. Cost of capital is minimum  
D. Profit is zero

Answer: C

Explanation:
Optimal structure minimizes Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) and maximizes firm value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Debt is cheaper than equity because:

A. No risk  
B. Interest is tax-deductible  
C. Dividends are compulsory  
D. No repayment

Answer: B

Explanation:
Interest on debt is tax-deductible, reducing effective cost.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) is:

A. Cost of equity only  
B. Cost of debt only  
C. Weighted average of cost of all sources  
D. Profit rate

Answer: C

Explanation:
WACC considers proportionate cost of debt, equity, and preference capital.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Increase in debt increases:

A. Business risk  
B. Financial risk  
C. Operating risk  
D. Asset turnover

Answer: B

Explanation:
More debt increases obligation to pay fixed interest, increasing financial risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Modigliani-Miller (without tax) theory suggests:

A. Capital structure affects value  
B. Capital structure irrelevant  
C. Debt increases value  
D. Equity reduces value

Answer: B

Explanation:
In absence of taxes and imperfections, capital structure does not affect firm value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Interest Coverage Ratio measures:

A. Liquidity  
B. Ability to pay interest  
C. Asset turnover  
D. Profit margin

Answer: B

Explanation:
Interest Coverage = EBIT / Interest.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Trading on equity means:

A. Using retained earnings  
B. Using debt to increase EPS  
C. Selling shares  
D. Paying dividend

Answer: B

Explanation:
Using debt to enhance returns on equity is trading on equity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
If ROI > Cost of debt, leverage is:

A. Negative  
B. Positive  
C. Neutral  
D. Zero

Answer: B

Explanation:
When return on investment exceeds debt cost, shareholders benefit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Cost of equity is generally:

A. Lower than debt  
B. Higher than debt  
C. Equal to debt  
D. Zero

Answer: B

Explanation:
Equity is riskier than debt; hence higher required return.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
Capital gearing refers to:

A. Asset ratio  
B. Relationship between fixed interest capital and equity  
C. Profitability  
D. Liquidity

Answer: B

Explanation:
High gearing means more fixed cost capital (debt/preference shares).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
An unlevered firm has:

A. Debt and equity  
B. Only debt  
C. Only equity  
D. No capital

Answer: C

Explanation:
Unlevered firm has no debt; financed entirely by equity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
The main goal of capital structure decision is:

A. Maximize debt  
B. Maximize equity  
C. Maximize shareholders’ wealth  
D. Reduce assets

Answer: C

Explanation:
Capital structure aims to maximize firm value and shareholders’ wealth.

Equity ₹5,00,000; Debt ₹5,00,000. Debt-Equity Ratio?

Answer: B

Explanation:
5,00,000 / 5,00,000 = 1:1.

## 17.
EBIT ₹4,00,000; Interest ₹1,00,000. Interest Coverage?

A. 2 times  
B. 3 times  
C. 4 times  
D. 5 times

Answer: C

Explanation:
4,00,000 / 1,00,000 = 4 times.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
ROI 15%; Cost of debt 10%. Leverage effect?

A. Negative  
B. Positive  
C. Zero  
D. Loss

Answer: B

Explanation:
ROI > Cost of debt → positive financial leverage.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
Total capital ₹10,00,000; Debt ₹4,00,000; Cost of debt 8%; Cost of equity 15%. WACC?

A. 11.2%  
B. 12.2%  
C. 10%  
D. 15%

Answer: B

Explanation:
Weight debt = 0.4 × 8% = 3.2%  
Weight equity = 0.6 × 15% = 9%  
WACC = 12.2%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
EBIT ₹6,00,000; Debt ₹10,00,000 at 10%; Equity ₹20,00,000. Net income?

A. ₹5,00,000  
B. ₹6,00,000  
C. ₹4,00,000  
D. ₹3,00,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
Interest = 1,00,000  
Net income = 6,00,000 – 1,00,000 = ₹5,00,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 21.
If firm replaces equity with debt and ROI remains same above debt cost, EPS will:

A. Decrease  
B. Increase  
C. Remain same  
D. Zero

Answer: B

Explanation:
Positive leverage increases EPS.

------------------------------------------------------------

EBIT-EPS analysis helps determine:

## 24.
If cost of equity 16%; cost of debt 8%; more debt generally:

A. Increases WACC  
B. Reduces WACC (initially)  
C. No effect  
D. Doubles WACC

Answer: B

Since debt cheaper, moderate increase reduces WACC until risk increases significantly.

## 25.
Financial break-even point is where:

A. EBIT = 0

D. Sales zero

At EBIT equal to interest, EPS is zero.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 26.
Company A has no debt; Company B has debt. If EBIT fluctuates, EPS of B:

A. Less volatile  
B. More volatile  
C. Same  
D. Zero

Leverage magnifies EPS fluctuations.

## 27.

0.5×8% = 4%  
0.5×14% = 7%  
Total = 11%.

High fixed interest burden increases:

Under MM with tax, debt creates tax shield, increasing firm value.
