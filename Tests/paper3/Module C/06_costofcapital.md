## 1.
Cost of capital represents:

A. Historical cost of assets  
B. Minimum required rate of return  
C. Profit margin  
D. Dividend rate

Answer: B

Explanation:
Cost of capital is the minimum rate of return required by investors for providing funds to the firm.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Cost of debt after tax is lower because:

A. Debt is risk-free  
B. Interest is tax deductible  
C. Principal is repaid  
D. Equity is cheaper

Answer: B

Explanation:
Interest on debt reduces taxable income, lowering effective cost.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Cost of equity is generally:

A. Lower than debt  
B. Equal to debt  
C. Higher than debt  
D. Zero

Answer: C

Explanation:
Equity carries higher risk than debt, so investors demand higher return.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Retained earnings have:

A. No cost  
B. Explicit cost  
C. Opportunity cost  
D. Negative cost

Answer: C

Explanation:
Retained earnings have opportunity cost equal to cost of equity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
WACC stands for:

A. Weighted Average Capital Cost  
B. Weighted Average Cost of Capital  
C. Working Asset Capital Cost  
D. Weighted Asset Capital Cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
WACC is weighted average cost of all sources of finance.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Cost of preference shares is calculated as:

A. Dividend / Net proceeds  
B. Interest / Principal  
C. Profit / Capital  
D. Sales / Assets

Answer: A

Explanation:
Cost of preference share = Annual Dividend / Net proceeds.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
CAPM formula for cost of equity is:

A. Ke = Rf + β (Rm – Rf)  
B. Ke = Dividend/Price  
C. Ke = EBIT/Capital  
D. Ke = Interest rate

Answer: A

Explanation:
CAPM estimates cost of equity based on risk-free rate and market risk premium.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
If tax rate increases, after-tax cost of debt:

A. Increases  
B. Decreases  
C. Same  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
After-tax cost = Kd (1 – Tax rate). Higher tax reduces effective cost.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
Debt ₹5,00,000 at 10%; tax 30%. After-tax cost of debt?

A. 10%  
B. 7%  
C. 13%  
D. 3%

Answer: B

Explanation:
Kd(1 – t) = 10% × (1 – 0.30) = 7%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
Preference shares ₹1,00,000; dividend 12%; issued at par. Cost?

A. 10%  
B. 12%  
C. 14%  
D. 8%

Answer: B

Explanation:
12% dividend → cost = 12%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Equity dividend ₹5; market price ₹50. Cost of equity (Dividend yield)?

A. 5%  
B. 8%  
C. 10%  
D. 12%

Answer: C

Explanation:
5 / 50 = 10%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
Risk-free rate 6%; Market return 12%; Beta 1.2. Cost of equity?

A. 12%  
B. 13.2%  
C. 14%  
D. 15%

Answer: B

Explanation:
Ke = 6 + 1.2 (12 – 6)  
= 6 + 1.2×6  
= 6 + 7.2 = 13.2%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
Capital structure: Debt 40%, Equity 60%. Kd after tax 8%, Ke 14%. WACC?

A. 11.6%  
B. 12%  
C. 10%  
D. 9%

Answer: A

Explanation:
0.4×8% = 3.2%  
0.6×14% = 8.4%  
Total = 11.6%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 21.
Irredeemable debt ₹1,00,000; interest 9%; issued at ₹90,000. Cost?

B. 10%

Explanation:

Cost = 9,000 / 90,000 = 10%.
