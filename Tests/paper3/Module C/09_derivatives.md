## 1.
A derivative derives its value from:

A. Accounting profit  
B. Underlying asset  
C. Dividend only  
D. Interest rate only

Answer: B

Explanation:
A derivative is a financial instrument whose value is based on an underlying asset such as stock, bond, currency, commodity, or index.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Common types of derivatives include:

A. Shares and debentures  
B. Futures, options, swaps, forwards  
C. Deposits and loans  
D. Reserves

Answer: B

Explanation:
Major derivative instruments are forwards, futures, options, and swaps.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
A forward contract is:

A. Standardized contract traded on exchange  
B. Private agreement between two parties  
C. Option contract  
D. Equity instrument

Answer: B

Explanation:
Forward contracts are customized OTC agreements between two parties.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Futures contracts are traded on:

A. OTC market  
B. Stock exchanges  
C. Banks only  
D. Private firms

Answer: B

Explanation:
Futures are standardized and traded on organized exchanges.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
An option gives the holder:

A. Obligation to buy or sell  
B. Right but not obligation  
C. Ownership immediately  
D. Dividend

Answer: B

Explanation:
Option holder has right but not obligation to buy (call) or sell (put).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
A call option gives right to:

A. Sell asset  
B. Buy asset  
C. Borrow asset  
D. Cancel contract

Answer: B

Explanation:
Call option gives right to buy at predetermined price.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
A put option gives right to:

A. Buy asset  
B. Sell asset  
C. Convert asset  
D. Hold asset

Answer: B

Explanation:
Put option gives right to sell underlying asset.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Strike price is:

A. Market price  
B. Exercise price  
C. Future price  
D. Premium

Answer: B

Explanation:
Strike price is the price at which option can be exercised.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Premium in option is:

A. Face value  
B. Price paid to acquire option  
C. Dividend  
D. Margin

Option premium is the cost paid by buyer to seller.

## 10.
Hedging aims to:

Hedging protects against adverse price movements.

## 11.
Speculation involves:

A. Avoiding risk

Speculators take risk expecting price movement benefits.

## 12.
Arbitrage means:

A. Buying low and selling high without risk

Swaps are generally:

## 21.

120 – 100 = ₹20.

Explanation:
150 – 130 = ₹20.

Call premium ₹25; Intrinsic value ₹20. Time value?

B. ₹20

## 24.
Futures contract price ₹1,000; Margin 10%. Initial margin?

A. ₹1,000  
B. ₹100  
C. ₹10  
D. ₹900

Answer: B

Explanation:

------------------------------------------------------------

## 25.
Investor buys call strike ₹100; Premium ₹10; Spot ₹130 at expiry. Profit?

A. ₹30  
B. ₹20  
C. ₹10  
D. ₹40

Answer: B

Explanation:
Intrinsic = 30  
Net profit = 30 – 10 = ₹20.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 26.
Investor buys put strike ₹200; Premium ₹15; Spot ₹170. Profit?

A. ₹30  
B. ₹15  
C. ₹45  
D. ₹20

Answer: B

Explanation:
Intrinsic = 200 – 170 = 30  
Profit = 30 – 15 = ₹15.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 27.
If spot ₹80; futures ₹85; Arbitrage opportunity?

A. Sell spot, buy futures  
B. Buy spot, sell futures  
C. No arbitrage  
D. Hold

Answer: B

Explanation:
Buy at lower spot (80) and sell at higher futures (85).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 28.

A. ₹6

Explanation:
Importer pays ₹84 instead of ₹90 → gain ₹6.

## 29.
Delta of call option measures:

A. Risk-free rate

Delta measures change in option price for change in underlying.

## 30.
If option expires out-of-the-money, payoff:

A. Positive  
B. Zero  
C. Negative infinite  
D. Equal to premium

Answer: B

Explanation:
Out-of-the-money option has zero intrinsic value; buyer loses premium only.
