## 1.
Working capital refers to:

A. Total assets  
B. Fixed assets  
C. Current assets – Current liabilities  
D. Share capital

Answer: C

Explanation:
Net Working Capital = Current Assets – Current Liabilities.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Gross working capital means:

A. Current assets  
B. Current liabilities  
C. Fixed assets  
D. Capital employed

Answer: A

Explanation:
Gross working capital refers to total current assets.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Permanent working capital is:

A. Seasonal requirement  
B. Minimum level of current assets always required  
C. Temporary surplus  
D. Cash only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Permanent working capital is the minimum current assets needed for smooth operations.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Temporary working capital arises due to:

A. Regular operations  
B. Seasonal fluctuations  
C. Fixed assets  
D. Long-term loans

Answer: B

Explanation:
Temporary working capital varies with seasonal or cyclical demand.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Excessive working capital may lead to:

D. Loss of sales

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Inadequate working capital may cause:

## 21.
Current Assets ₹6,00,000; Current Liabilities ₹4,00,000. Working Capital?

A. ₹2,00,000  
B. ₹10,00,000  
C. ₹4,00,000  
D. ₹6,00,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
6,00,000 – 4,00,000 = ₹2,00,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 22.
Current Assets ₹5,00,000; Current Liabilities ₹2,50,000. Current Ratio?

A. 1:1  
B. 2:1  
C. 3:1  
D. 0.5:1

Answer: B

Explanation:
5,00,000 / 2,50,000 = 2:1.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 23.
Inventory ₹2,00,000; Current Assets ₹5,00,000; Current Liabilities ₹2,50,000. Quick Ratio?

A. 1:1  
B. 1.2:1  
C. 1.5:1  
D. 2:1

Answer: B

Explanation:
Quick assets = 5,00,000 – 2,00,000 = 3,00,000  
Quick ratio = 3,00,000 / 2,50,000 = 1.2:1.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 24.
Credit sales ₹12,00,000; Average receivables ₹3,00,000. Receivables turnover?

B. 3 times

Explanation:

EOQ formula is:
