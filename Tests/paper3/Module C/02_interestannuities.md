## 1.
Simple Interest is calculated on:

A. Principal only  
B. Principal + Interest  
C. Accumulated amount  
D. Discounted value

Answer: A

Explanation:
Simple Interest is calculated only on the original principal amount.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Compound Interest differs from Simple Interest because it is calculated on:

A. Principal only  
B. Principal plus accumulated interest  
C. Discounted value  
D. Net amount

Answer: B

Explanation:
Compound Interest is calculated on principal plus accumulated interest.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
The formula for Simple Interest is:

A. PRT  
B. P + RT  
C. PRT/100  
D. P × R + T

Answer: C

Explanation:
Simple Interest = (P × R × T) / 100.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Compounding increases total interest because:

A. Rate increases  
B. Time increases  
C. Interest earns interest  
D. Principal decreases

Answer: C

Explanation:
In compound interest, interest is added to principal and earns further interest.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
An annuity is:

A. Single payment  
B. Irregular payments  
C. Equal payments at regular intervals  
D. Lump sum investment

Answer: C

Explanation:
An annuity is a series of equal payments made at regular intervals.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
An annuity payable at the beginning of each period is called:

A. Ordinary annuity  
B. Perpetuity  
C. Annuity due  
D. Deferred annuity

Answer: C

Explanation:
Annuity due payments are made at the beginning of each period.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Future value of an annuity increases when:

A. Interest rate decreases  
B. Number of periods increases  
C. Payment decreases  
D. Compounding stops

Explanation:
More periods mean more compounding effect.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Present Value of ₹1 to be received after 1 year at 10% is:

A. ₹1.10  
B. ₹0.90  
C. ₹1  
D. ₹0.91 approx

Answer: D

Explanation:
PV = 1 / (1 + 0.10) = 0.909 ≈ ₹0.91.
