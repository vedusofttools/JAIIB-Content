### PART A – Theory (1–20)
############################

## 1.
Capital investment decisions relate to:

A. Short-term working capital  
B. Long-term investment in fixed assets  
C. Dividend payment  
D. Inventory purchase only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Capital investment decisions involve long-term investments in fixed assets and projects that generate future cash flows.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Capital budgeting decisions are important because they:

A. Affect only current year profit  
B. Involve large funds and long-term impact  
C. Are reversible  
D. Involve small amounts

Answer: B

Explanation:
These decisions require substantial investment and have long-term implications.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
The main objective of capital budgeting is to:

A. Increase sales  
B. Maximize shareholders’ wealth  
C. Reduce assets  
D. Increase expenses

Answer: B

Explanation:
Projects should be selected to maximize firm value and shareholder wealth.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Net Present Value (NPV) method considers:

A. Accounting profit  
B. Time value of money  
C. Sales volume  
D. Historical cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
NPV discounts future cash flows to present value using cost of capital.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Internal Rate of Return (IRR) is the rate at which:

A. Profit is maximum  
B. NPV becomes zero  
C. Sales double  
D. Cost equals revenue

Answer: B

Explanation:
IRR is the discount rate that makes NPV equal to zero.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Payback Period method ignores:

A. Initial investment  
B. Cash flows  
C. Time value of money  
D. Profit

Answer: C

Explanation:
Traditional payback does not consider time value of money.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Profitability Index (PI) is:

A. PV of cash inflows / Initial investment  
B. Initial investment / PV  
C. Profit / Sales  
D. Sales / Cost

Answer: A

Explanation:
PI > 1 indicates acceptable project.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
If NPV > 0, project should be:

A. Rejected  
B. Accepted  
C. Postponed  
D. Ignored

Answer: B

Explanation:
Positive NPV increases firm value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
If IRR > Cost of Capital:

A. Reject project  
B. Accept project  
C. No decision  
D. Delay project

Answer: B

Explanation:
If IRR exceeds required return, project is profitable.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Discount rate in capital budgeting usually equals:

A. Inflation rate  
B. Cost of capital  
C. Dividend rate  
D. Tax rate

Answer: B

Explanation:
Cost of capital is used as discount rate.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
Mutually exclusive projects mean:

A. Can be accepted together  
B. Only one can be selected  
C. Unlimited acceptance  
D. No restriction

Answer: B

Explanation:
Selection of one project excludes others.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Independent projects:

A. Compete with each other  
B. Can be accepted simultaneously  
C. Are rejected automatically  
D. Are short-term

Answer: B

Explanation:
Independent projects can be evaluated individually.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
Accounting Rate of Return (ARR) is based on:

A. Cash flows  
B. Accounting profit  
C. Market price  
D. Discounting

Answer: B

Explanation:
ARR uses average accounting profit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
NPV method is preferred because it:

A. Ignores risk  
B. Directly measures wealth addition  
C. Ignores time value  
D. Uses accounting profit

Answer: B

Explanation:
NPV shows absolute increase in firm value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
Capital rationing occurs when:

A. Funds unlimited  
B. Funds limited  
C. Projects small  
D. Sales low

Answer: B

Explanation:
Limited funds restrict number of projects undertaken.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
Risk in capital budgeting is handled by:

A. Ignoring it  
B. Increasing discount rate  
C. Reducing cost  
D. Avoiding projects

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher discount rate accounts for higher risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
Sunk cost should be:

A. Included  
B. Ignored  
C. Added to investment  
D. Treated as asset

Answer: B

Explanation:
Sunk costs are past costs and irrelevant to decision.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Opportunity cost is:

A. Historical cost  
B. Cost of next best alternative  
C. Fixed cost  
D. Tax

Answer: B

Explanation:
Opportunity cost reflects benefit sacrificed.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
Cash flows used in capital budgeting are:

A. Accounting profits  
B. Incremental cash flows  
C. Total revenue  
D. Depreciation only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Only incremental cash flows relevant.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
Depreciation is added back in NPV calculation because:

A. It is cash expense  
B. It is non-cash expense  
C. It increases profit  
D. It reduces tax only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Depreciation reduces profit but not actual cash outflow.

## 21.
Initial investment ₹1,00,000; Annual cash inflow ₹25,000. Payback period?

A. 2 years  
B. 3 years  
C. 4 years  
D. 5 years

Answer: C

Explanation:
1,00,000 / 25,000 = 4 years.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 22.
Initial ₹50,000; Inflow ₹20,000 yearly for 3 years. Total inflow?

A. ₹50,000  
B. ₹60,000  
C. ₹70,000  
D. ₹80,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
20,000 × 3 = ₹60,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 23.
Project cost ₹1,00,000; PV of inflows ₹1,20,000. NPV?

A. ₹20,000  
B. ₹1,20,000  
C. ₹1,00,000  
D. ₹10,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
1,20,000 – 1,00,000 = ₹20,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 24.
PV of inflows ₹90,000; Initial ₹1,00,000. NPV?

A. ₹10,000  
B. –₹10,000  
C. ₹1,00,000  
D. ₹90,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
90,000 – 1,00,000 = –10,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 25.
Initial ₹1,00,000; PV inflows ₹1,30,000. Profitability Index?

A. 1.3  
B. 0.77  
C. 1.0  
D. 0.3

Answer: A

Explanation:
1,30,000 / 1,00,000 = 1.3.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 26.
Project cost ₹2,00,000; Cash inflow ₹80,000 annually. Payback?

A. 2 years  
B. 2.5 years  
C. 3 years  
D. 4 years

Answer: B

Explanation:
2,00,000 / 80,000 = 2.5 years.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 27.
If IRR = 14% and Cost of capital = 12%, decision?

A. Reject  
B. Accept  
C. Indifferent  
D. Delay

Answer: B

Explanation:
IRR > Cost → Accept.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 28.
If IRR = 10% and Cost = 12%, decision?

A. Accept  
B. Reject  
C. Indifferent  
D. Invest more

Answer: B

Explanation:
IRR < Cost → Reject.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 29.
Initial ₹1,00,000; Cash inflow ₹40,000 yearly. Payback?

A. 2 years  
B. 2.5 years  
C. 3 years  
D. 4 years

Answer: B

Explanation:
1,00,000 / 40,000 = 2.5 years.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 30.
If two projects mutually exclusive and both positive NPV, select:

A. Lower NPV  
B. Higher NPV  
C. Higher payback  
D. Lower cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
For mutually exclusive projects, choose higher NPV to maximize wealth.
