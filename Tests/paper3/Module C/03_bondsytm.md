############################
### PART A – Conceptual (1–15)
############################

## 1.
A bond represents:

A. Ownership in company  
B. Long-term debt instrument  
C. Short-term liability  
D. Equity share

Answer: B

Explanation:
A bond is a long-term debt instrument through which investors lend money to issuer in return for periodic interest and principal repayment.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
The face value of a bond is also called:

A. Market value  
B. Nominal value  
C. Coupon value  
D. Book value

Answer: B

Explanation:
Face value (par value) is the amount repaid at maturity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Coupon rate refers to:

A. Market rate  
B. Interest rate on face value  
C. Yield to maturity  
D. Discount rate

Answer: B

Explanation:
Coupon rate is the fixed interest rate applied to face value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
If bond is issued at price above face value, it is issued at:

A. Discount  
B. Par  
C. Premium  
D. Loss

Answer: C

Explanation:
When market price > face value, bond sells at premium.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Yield to Maturity (YTM) represents:

A. Coupon rate  
B. Current yield  
C. Total return if held till maturity  
D. Dividend

Answer: C

Explanation:
YTM is the internal rate of return assuming bond is held till maturity and coupons reinvested.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
If coupon rate > YTM, bond will trade at:

A. Discount  
B. Par  
C. Premium  
D. Zero

Answer: C

Explanation:
Higher coupon than required return makes bond more valuable → premium.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
If coupon rate < YTM, bond trades at:

A. Premium  
B. Discount  
C. Par  
D. Zero

Answer: B

Explanation:
Lower coupon than market required return reduces bond price.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Zero coupon bond pays:

A. Annual interest  
B. Quarterly interest  
C. No periodic interest  
D. Dividend

Answer: C

Explanation:
Zero coupon bonds pay no periodic interest; issued at discount and redeemed at face value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Current yield is calculated as:

A. Coupon / Market Price  
B. Market Price / Coupon  
C. Face value / Coupon  
D. YTM / Price

Answer: A

Explanation:
Current Yield = Annual Coupon / Market Price.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Bond price and yield relationship is:

A. Direct  
B. Inverse  
C. No relation  
D. Fixed

Answer: B

Explanation:
When yields rise, bond prices fall and vice versa.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
Longer maturity bonds are:

A. Less sensitive to interest rate  
B. More sensitive to interest rate  
C. Not affected  
D. Fixed price

Answer: B

Explanation:
Longer duration increases interest rate risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
If market interest rate increases, bond price:

A. Increases  
B. Decreases  
C. Remains same  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher required return reduces present value of bond.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
Redemption value of bond is usually:

A. Market value  
B. Coupon  
C. Face value  
D. Dividend

Answer: C

Explanation:
Bond is typically redeemed at face value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
YTM is similar to:

A. Accounting profit  
B. Internal Rate of Return  
C. Coupon rate  
D. Dividend yield

Answer: B

Explanation:
YTM is IRR of bond cash flows.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
Callable bond allows issuer to:

A. Extend maturity  
B. Convert to equity  
C. Redeem before maturity  
D. Increase coupon

Answer: C

Explanation:
Callable bonds can be redeemed early by issuer.

## 16.
Face value ₹1,000; Coupon 10%. Annual interest?

A. ₹10  
B. ₹100  
C. ₹1,000  
D. ₹900

Answer: B

Explanation:
1,000 × 10% = ₹100.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
Bond face ₹1,000; Coupon 8%; Market price ₹800. Current yield?

A. 8%  
B. 10%  
C. 12%  
D. 6%

Answer: B

Explanation:
Coupon = 80  
Current yield = 80 / 800 = 10%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Zero coupon bond face ₹1,000; price ₹900; maturity 1 year. Yield?

A. 10%  
B. 11.11%  
C. 9%  
D. 8%

Answer: B

Explanation:
(1,000 – 900)/900 = 100/900 = 11.11%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
Bond face ₹1,000; Coupon 10%; YTM 10%. Price?

A. ₹900  
B. ₹1,000  
C. ₹1,100  
D. ₹950

Answer: B

Explanation:
When coupon = YTM, bond sells at par.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
Bond face ₹1,000; Coupon 12%; Market rate 10%. Price will be:

A. Above ₹1,000  
B. ₹1,000  
C. Below ₹1,000  
D. Zero

Answer: A

Explanation:
Coupon > required return → premium bond.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 21.
Bond face ₹1,000; Coupon 8%; Market rate 10%. Price will be:

A. Above ₹1,000  
B. ₹1,000  
C. Below ₹1,000  
D. Double

Answer: C

Explanation:
Coupon < market rate → discount bond.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 22.
Face ₹1,000; Coupon 10%; 2 years; YTM 10%. Total cash inflow over life?

A. ₹1,000  
B. ₹1,200  
C. ₹1,100  
D. ₹900

Answer: B

Explanation:
Coupons: 100 × 2 = 200  
Redemption = 1,000  
Total = 1,200.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 23.
Bond price formula includes:

A. Present value of coupons + PV of face value  
B. Only coupon  
C. Only principal  
D. Sales

Answer: A

Explanation:
Bond value = PV of coupon payments + PV of redemption value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 24.
If YTM rises from 8% to 10%, bond price:

A. Rises  
B. Falls  
C. Constant  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher yield reduces present value.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 25.
Current yield if bond ₹1,000 face, 12% coupon, price ₹1,200:

A. 12%  
B. 10%  
C. 8%  
D. 15%

Coupon = 120  
120 / 1,200 = 10%.

B. ₹100  
C. ₹950  
D. ₹0

Explanation:
Redemption 1,000 – purchase 950 = ₹50 gain.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 27.
Approximate YTM if coupon 10%, price below par:

A. Less than 10%  
B. Equal 10%  
C. Greater than 10%  
D. Zero

Explanation:
Discount bond → YTM > coupon rate.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 28.
If coupon paid semi-annually, compounding frequency:

A. 1  
B. 2  
C. 4  
D. 12

Answer: B

Explanation:
Semi-annual means 2 payments per year.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 29.
Bond duration increases when:

A. Coupon increases  
B. Maturity increases  
C. Yield increases  
D. Face value decreases

Answer: B

Explanation:
Longer maturity increases duration and interest rate risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 30.
YTM considers:

A. Coupon only  
B. Capital gain only  
C. Coupon + Capital gain/loss  
D. Dividend only

Answer: C

Explanation:
YTM includes both periodic interest and capital gain/loss.
