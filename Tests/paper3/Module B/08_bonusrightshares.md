### PART A – Conceptual (1–15)
############################

## 1.
Bonus shares are issued:

A. For cash consideration  
B. To raise fresh capital  
C. By capitalising free reserves  
D. At market price

Answer: C

Explanation:
Bonus shares are issued by converting free reserves or securities premium into share capital. No fresh funds are received.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Bonus issue results in:

A. Increase in total assets  
B. Increase in total shareholders’ funds  
C. No change in total shareholders’ funds  
D. Increase in liabilities

Answer: C

Explanation:
Bonus issue merely transfers reserves to share capital; total shareholders’ funds remain unchanged.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Rights issue is:

A. Free issue of shares  
B. Issue of shares to existing shareholders in proportion to holdings  
C. Public issue only  
D. Issue of debentures

Answer: B

Explanation:
Rights shares are offered to existing shareholders in proportion to their existing shareholding.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Rights shares are generally issued at:

A. Higher than market price  
B. Face value always  
C. Discounted price compared to market  
D. Free of cost

Answer: C

Explanation:
Rights shares are usually offered at a price lower than the prevailing market price.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
The main objective of bonus issue is:

A. Raise working capital  
B. Reward shareholders without cash outflow  
C. Repay loans  
D. Reduce share capital

Answer: B

Explanation:
Bonus issue rewards shareholders by increasing number of shares without affecting liquidity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
After bonus issue, Earnings Per Share (EPS) generally:

A. Increases  
B. Decreases  
C. Remains same  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
Since number of shares increases without change in profit, EPS decreases.

## 7.
Securities Premium Account can be used for:

Securities premium can be capitalised for issuing bonus shares.

## 8.
Rights issue increases:

## 16.
Company has 1,00,000 shares. It declares 1:1 bonus. Total shares after bonus?

A. 1,00,000  
B. 1,50,000  
C. 2,00,000  
D. 50,000

Answer: C

Explanation:
1:1 → 1 new share for every 1 held  
Total = 1,00,000 + 1,00,000 = 2,00,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
If EPS before bonus ₹20 and 1:1 bonus declared, new EPS becomes:

A. ₹20  
B. ₹10  
C. ₹40  
D. ₹5

Answer: B

Explanation:
Number of shares doubles → EPS halves.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Market price ₹200. 1:1 bonus declared. Theoretical ex-bonus price?

A. ₹200  
B. ₹100  
C. ₹150  
D. ₹400

Answer: B

Explanation:
Value spreads over double shares → 200 ÷ 2 = ₹100.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
Rights issue 1:4 at ₹120; Market price ₹160. Theoretical ex-right price?

A. ₹150  
B. ₹152  
C. ₹140  
D. ₹160

Answer: B

Explanation:
Value of 4 old shares = 4 × 160 = 640  
Add 1 new share = 120  
Total value = 760  
Total shares = 5  
Price = 760 ÷ 5 = ₹152.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
Value of one right in above case?

A. ₹8  
B. ₹10  
C. ₹12  
D. ₹20

Answer: A

Explanation:
Market price – ex-right price  
160 – 152 = ₹8.

------------------------------------------------------------
