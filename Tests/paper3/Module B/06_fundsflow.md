############################

## 1.
A Funds Flow Statement shows:

A. Cash inflows and outflows  
B. Changes in financial position between two balance sheet dates  
C. Profit for the year  
D. Bank transactions only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Funds Flow Statement explains changes in financial position by analysing sources and applications of funds between two dates.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
In Funds Flow Statement, the term “Fund” generally means:

A. Cash only  
B. Working Capital  
C. Bank balance  
D. Profit

Answer: B

Explanation:
In traditional funds flow analysis, “fund” refers to working capital (Current Assets – Current Liabilities).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Increase in working capital indicates:

A. Source of funds  
B. Application of funds  
C. No effect  
D. Profit

Answer: B

Explanation:
Increase in working capital means more funds blocked in current assets → application of funds.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Decrease in working capital represents:

A. Application  
B. Source  
C. Loss  
D. Expense

Answer: B

Explanation:
Decrease in working capital releases funds → source.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Issue of shares for cash is:

A. Application  
B. Source of funds  
C. Loss  
D. Current liability

Answer: B

Explanation:
Issue of shares increases long-term funds → source.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Purchase of fixed asset is:

A. Source

C. Working capital  
D. Revenue

Explanation:
Purchase of fixed asset consumes funds → application.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Depreciation is treated as:

A. Application

Answer: C

Explanation:
Depreciation does not involve flow of funds; it is non-fund adjustment.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.

Net profit is adjusted for non-cash and non-operating items to derive funds from operations.

## 16.
Current Assets ₹6,00,000; Current Liabilities ₹4,00,000. Working Capital?

A. ₹2,00,000  
B. ₹10,00,000  
C. ₹4,00,000  
D. ₹6,00,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
Working Capital = 6,00,000 – 4,00,000 = ₹2,00,000.

B. ₹1,20,000 application

D. ₹3,00,000 application

Increase in working capital = application of ₹1,20,000.
