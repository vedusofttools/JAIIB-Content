### PART A – Conceptual (1–15)
############################

## 1.
A share represents:

A. Loan given to company  
B. Ownership in the company  
C. Expense of company  
D. Liability

Answer: B

Explanation:
A share represents a unit of ownership in the company. Shareholders are owners, not creditors.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
The face value of a share is also called:

A. Market value  
B. Intrinsic value  
C. Nominal value  
D. Book value

Answer: C

Explanation:
Face value (or nominal value) is the value printed on the share certificate.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Authorised share capital means:

A. Capital issued to public  
B. Maximum capital company is permitted to issue  
C. Paid-up capital  
D. Subscribed capital

Answer: B

Explanation:
Authorised capital is the maximum share capital allowed by Memorandum of Association.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Issued capital is:

A. Maximum capital  
B. Capital offered to public  
C. Capital fully paid  
D. Reserve capital

Answer: B

Explanation:
Issued capital is the portion of authorised capital offered for subscription.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Subscribed capital is:

A. Shares applied and allotted  
B. Shares authorised  
C. Paid-up capital  
D. Uncalled capital

Answer: A

Explanation:
Subscribed capital is the portion of issued capital taken up by shareholders.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Paid-up capital represents:

A. Total authorised capital  
B. Amount actually received from shareholders  
C. Maximum possible capital  
D. Reserve fund

Answer: B

Explanation:
Paid-up capital is the amount received on shares issued.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Preference shares carry:

A. Higher voting rights  
B. Priority in dividend and repayment  
C. No dividend rights  
D. Equal risk as equity always

Answer: B

Explanation:
Preference shareholders get fixed dividend and priority during winding up.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Equity shareholders are entitled to:

A. Fixed dividend  
B. Residual profits  
C. Fixed interest  
D. No voting rights

Answer: B

Explanation:
Equity shareholders receive dividend after preference shareholders and bear residual risk.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Issue of shares above face value is called issue at:

A. Discount  
B. Premium  
C. Par  
D. Loss

Answer: B

Explanation:
Excess over nominal value is securities premium.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Securities premium can be used for:

A. Payment of dividend  
B. Writing off preliminary expenses  
C. Salary expenses  
D. Routine repairs

Answer: B

Explanation:
As per Companies Act, premium may be used for specific purposes like writing off preliminary expenses.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
Calls in arrears represent:

A. Liability  
B. Unpaid amount by shareholders  
C. Income  
D. Asset

Answer: B

Explanation:
It is amount due but not received from shareholders.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Calls in advance is treated as:

A. Asset  
B. Liability  
C. Income  
D. Capital

Answer: B

Explanation:
Amount received before due call is liability until adjusted.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
Forfeiture of shares happens when:

A. Shares issued  
B. Shareholder fails to pay call money  
C. Dividend declared  
D. Bonus shares issued

Answer: B

Explanation:
If call money unpaid, company may forfeit shares.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
Bonus shares are issued from:

A. Cash  
B. Borrowings  
C. Free reserves  
D. Debentures

Answer: C

Explanation:
Bonus shares are issued by capitalising reserves.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
Share capital appears in Balance Sheet under:

A. Current liabilities  
B. Non-current assets  
C. Shareholders’ funds  
D. Current assets

Answer: C

Explanation:
Share capital is shown under Equity and Liabilities.

## 16.
A company issues 10,000 shares of ₹10 each at par. Total share capital is:

A. ₹10,000  
B. ₹1,00,000  
C. ₹10,00,000  
D. ₹1,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
10,000 × 10 = ₹1,00,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
5,000 shares of ₹20 each issued at ₹25. Securities premium?

A. ₹25,000  
B. ₹1,25,000  
C. ₹5,000  
D. ₹20,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
Premium per share = ₹5  
Total premium = 5,000 × 5 = ₹25,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Company issued 20,000 shares of ₹10. Application ₹3; Allotment ₹4; Final call ₹3. Paid-up capital after full payment?

A. ₹60,000  
B. ₹2,00,000  
C. ₹20,000  
D. ₹3,00,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
20,000 × 10 = ₹2,00,000 paid-up capital.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
If 1,000 shares ₹10 each, ₹8 called and fully received, paid-up capital is:

A. ₹10,000  
B. ₹8,000  
C. ₹1,000  
D. ₹2,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
1,000 × 8 = ₹8,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
10,000 shares ₹10 each; ₹2 unpaid on 500 shares. Calls in arrears?

A. ₹500  
B. ₹1,000  
C. ₹2,000  
D. ₹5,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
500 × 2 = ₹1,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 21.
Shares ₹10 issued at ₹12; 2,000 shares. Total capital credited to Share Capital Account?

A. ₹20,000  
B. ₹24,000  
C. ₹4,000  
D. ₹12,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
Share capital credited at face value = 2,000 × 10 = ₹20,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 22.
If shares ₹10 issued at 10% discount, issue price is:

A. ₹9  
B. ₹11  
C. ₹10  
D. ₹8

Answer: A

Explanation:
10% of 10 = 1  
Issue price = ₹9.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 23.
Company forfeits 100 shares ₹10 each fully called. Share capital debited by:

A. ₹1,000  
B. ₹100  
C. ₹10  
D. Nil

Answer: A

Explanation:
100 × 10 = ₹1,000 removed from capital.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 24.
2,000 shares ₹10 each; ₹6 called; fully received. Called-up capital?

A. ₹20,000  
B. ₹12,000

D. ₹10,000

2,000 × 6 = ₹12,000.

## 25.
Company issued 5,000 shares ₹10 each at ₹15. Total amount received?

A. ₹50,000  
B. ₹75,000

D. ₹15,000

5,000 × 15 = ₹75,000.

## 26.
Subscribed capital 50,000 shares ₹10; 45,000 shares allotted. Subscribed capital amount?

A. ₹5,00,000

45,000 × 10 = ₹4,50,000.

## 27.
Company received ₹3,00,000 on issue of shares ₹10 each fully paid. Number of shares issued?

A. 30,000  
B. 3,000  
C. 300

Answer: A

Explanation:
3,00,000 ÷ 10 = 30,000 shares.

## 28.
If premium ₹2 per share on 10,000 shares, securities premium reserve:

A. ₹20,000  
B. ₹2,000  
C. ₹10,000  
D. ₹5,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
10,000 × 2 = ₹20,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 29.
Paid-up capital ₹8,00,000; Calls in arrears ₹50,000. Called-up capital?

A. ₹7,50,000  
B. ₹8,50,000  
C. ₹8,00,000  
D. ₹50,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
Called-up = Paid-up + Calls in arrears  
= 8,00,000 + 50,000 = ₹8,50,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 30.
Authorised capital ₹10 lakh; Issued capital ₹6 lakh; Subscribed ₹5 lakh. Unissued capital?

A. ₹4 lakh  
B. ₹5 lakh  
C. ₹6 lakh  
D. ₹10 lakh

Answer: A

Explanation:
Authorised – Issued = 10 – 6 = ₹4 lakh unissued.
