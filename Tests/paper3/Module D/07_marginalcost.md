### PART A – Theory (1–15)
############################

## 1.
Marginal costing considers:

A. Total cost  
B. Fixed cost only  
C. Variable cost only  
D. Historical cost

Answer: C

Explanation:
Marginal costing considers only variable costs as product cost. Fixed costs are treated as period cost.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Contribution is defined as:

A. Sales – Total cost  
B. Sales – Variable cost  
C. Sales – Fixed cost  
D. Profit + Variable cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
Contribution = Sales – Variable Cost. It contributes towards fixed cost and profit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
P/V ratio stands for:

A. Profit/Variable ratio  
B. Price/Value ratio  
C. Profit/Volume ratio  
D. Production/Value ratio

Answer: C

Explanation:
P/V ratio = Contribution / Sales × 100.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Break-even point is where:

A. Profit is maximum  
B. Contribution is zero  
C. Total revenue = Total cost  
D. Variable cost = Fixed cost

Answer: C

Explanation:
At BEP, there is no profit or loss because total cost equals total revenue.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Margin of Safety represents:

A. Total sales  
B. Excess of actual sales over break-even sales  
C. Fixed cost  
D. Contribution

Answer: B

Explanation:
Margin of Safety = Actual Sales – Break-even Sales.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
If fixed cost increases, BEP:

A. Decreases  
B. Increases  
C. Remains same  
D. Becomes zero

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher fixed cost increases break-even sales.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
If selling price increases (other factors constant), BEP:

A. Increases  
B. Decreases  
C. Remains same  
D. Doubles

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher selling price increases contribution, reducing BEP.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
In marginal costing, fixed cost is treated as:

A. Product cost  
B. Inventory cost  
C. Period cost  
D. Direct cost

Answer: C

Explanation:
Fixed costs are charged fully to Profit & Loss account.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
CVP analysis studies relationship among:

A. Cost, Volume, Profit  
B. Cost and tax  
C. Profit and capital  
D. Assets and liabilities

Answer: A

Explanation:
CVP analysis evaluates how changes in cost and volume affect profit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Contribution per unit equals:

A. Selling price – Variable cost per unit  
B. Selling price – Fixed cost per unit  
C. Total cost – Sales  
D. Profit – Variable cost

Answer: A

Explanation:
Contribution per unit = SP – VC per unit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
Angle of incidence in CVP graph shows:

A. Break-even point  
B. Rate of profit after BEP  
C. Fixed cost  
D. Variable cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
Larger angle of incidence indicates higher profit rate beyond BEP.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
High P/V ratio indicates:

A. Low profitability  
B. High contribution margin  
C. High variable cost  
D. High fixed cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher P/V ratio means greater contribution per rupee of sales.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
When contribution equals fixed cost:

A. Profit is maximum  
B. Profit is zero  
C. Loss occurs  
D. Sales zero

Answer: B

Explanation:
Contribution covers fixed cost fully → no profit, no loss.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
If variable cost ratio is 70%, contribution ratio is:

A. 70%  
B. 30%  
C. 100%  
D. 50%

Answer: B

Explanation:
Contribution ratio = 100% – Variable cost ratio.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
CVP analysis assumes:

A. Variable cost per unit constant  
B. Fixed cost per unit constant  
C. Selling price fluctuates  
D. Multi-product complexity

Answer: A

Explanation:
CVP assumes constant selling price and variable cost per unit within relevant range.

## 16.
Selling price ₹100; Variable cost ₹60. Contribution per unit?

A. ₹40  
B. ₹60  
C. ₹100  
D. ₹160

Answer: A

Explanation:
100 – 60 = ₹40.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
Fixed cost ₹2,00,000; Contribution per unit ₹50. BEP (units)?

A. 2,000  
B. 3,000  
C. 4,000  
D. 5,000

Answer: C

Explanation:
2,00,000 / 50 = 4,000 units.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Sales ₹10,00,000; Variable cost ₹6,00,000. Contribution?

A. ₹4,00,000  
B. ₹6,00,000  
C. ₹10,00,000  
D. ₹2,00,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
10,00,000 – 6,00,000 = ₹4,00,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
Contribution ₹4,00,000; Fixed cost ₹3,00,000. Profit?

A. ₹1,00,000  
B. ₹3,00,000  
C. ₹4,00,000  
D. ₹7,00,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
Profit = Contribution – Fixed cost.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
Fixed cost ₹1,50,000; P/V ratio 30%. BE sales?

A. ₹3,00,000  
B. ₹4,00,000  
C. ₹5,00,000  
D. ₹6,00,000

Answer: C

Explanation:
BEP Sales = Fixed Cost / P/V ratio  
= 1,50,000 / 0.30 = ₹5,00,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 21.

A. ₹3,00,000  
B. ₹5,00,000  
C. ₹8,00,000  
D. ₹2,00,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
8,00,000 – 5,00,000 = ₹3,00,000.

A. ₹1,00,000  
B. ₹2,00,000  
C. ₹3,00,000  
D. ₹4,00,000

Explanation:

## 23.
Selling price ₹80; Variable cost ₹50; Fixed cost ₹90,000. BEP units?

A. 3,000  
B. 2,000  
C. 4,000  
D. 1,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
Contribution per unit = 80 – 50 = 30  
90,000 / 30 = 3,000 units.

If fixed cost increases, profit at same sales:

B. ₹3,00,000

## 29.
Contribution per unit ₹20; Profit target ₹40,000; Fixed cost ₹1,00,000. Required units?

A. 5,000  
B. 7,000  
C. 8,000  
D. 6,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
Required contribution = Fixed cost + Target profit  
= 1,00,000 + 40,000 = 1,40,000  
Units = 1,40,000 / 20 = 7,000 units.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 30.
If sales equal BE sales, Margin of Safety is:

A. Positive  
B. Zero  
C. Negative  
D. Maximum

Answer: B

Explanation:
At BE sales, actual sales – BE sales = 0.
