## 1.
A budget is:

A. Historical statement  
B. Forecast of future income and expenditure  
C. Audit report  
D. Tax return

Answer: B

Explanation:
A budget is a financial plan prepared in advance for a specific future period.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Budgetary control means:

A. Ignoring variances  
B. Comparing actual performance with budget  
C. Preparing balance sheet  
D. Increasing cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
Budgetary control involves setting budgets and comparing actual results with them to take corrective action.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
The main objective of budgetary control is:

A. Tax compliance  
B. Cost control and coordination  
C. Profit declaration  
D. Audit

Answer: B

Explanation:
It ensures efficient utilization of resources and proper coordination among departments.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
Budgeted sales ₹10,00,000; Actual sales ₹11,50,000. Sales variance?

A. ₹1,50,000 F  
B. ₹1,50,000 A  
C. ₹10,00,000  
D. ₹11,50,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
11,50,000 – 10,00,000 = ₹1,50,000 Favorable.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
Budgeted cost ₹5,00,000; Actual cost ₹5,50,000. Cost variance?

A. ₹50,000 F  
B. ₹50,000 A  
C. ₹5,00,000  
D. ₹5,50,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
Actual > Budget → ₹50,000 Adverse.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 18.
Budgeted production 5,000 units; Actual 6,000 units. Production variance?

A. 1,000 F  
B. 1,000 A  
C. 5,000  
D. 6,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
Higher production than budget is favorable if demand exists.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 19.
Opening stock 1,000 units; Sales forecast 5,000 units; Closing stock desired 800 units. Production required?

A. 4,800  
B. 5,800  
C. 4,000  
D. 6,800

Answer: A

Explanation:
Production = Sales + Closing stock – Opening stock  
= 5,000 + 800 – 1,000 = 4,800 units.

------------------------------------------------------------

D. ₹5,50,000

## 22.
Budgeted fixed cost ₹2,00,000; Contribution ₹2,50,000. Budgeted profit?

A. ₹50,000  
B. ₹2,00,000  
C. ₹2,50,000  
D. ₹4,50,000

Explanation:
Profit = Contribution – Fixed cost  
= 2,50,000 – 2,00,000 = ₹50,000.
