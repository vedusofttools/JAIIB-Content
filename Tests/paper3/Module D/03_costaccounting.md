### PART A – Theory (1–20)
############################

## 1.
Cost accounting mainly deals with:

A. Recording financial transactions  
B. Determining and controlling costs  
C. Auditing accounts  
D. Tax computation

Answer: B

Explanation:
Cost accounting focuses on ascertainment, control and analysis of costs to assist management decisions.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Cost is defined as:

A. Selling price  
B. Amount spent to produce goods/services  
C. Profit margin  
D. Capital employed

Answer: B

Explanation:
Cost represents expenditure incurred to produce goods or services.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Prime Cost consists of:

A. Direct materials + Direct labour + Direct expenses  
B. Factory overheads  
C. Administrative expenses  
D. Selling expenses

Answer: A

Explanation:
Prime cost includes all direct costs directly traceable to production.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Factory (Works) Cost equals:

A. Prime cost + Factory overheads  
B. Direct labour only  
C. Selling expenses  
D. Administrative cost

Answer: A

Explanation:
Works cost = Prime cost + Factory overheads.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Total Cost includes:

A. Production cost only  
B. Production + Administrative + Selling & Distribution cost  
C. Prime cost only  
D. Direct materials

Answer: B

Explanation:
Total cost includes all production and non-production expenses.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Fixed costs are:

A. Vary with output  
B. Constant in total within relevant range  
C. Zero  
D. Increasing always

Answer: B

Explanation:
Fixed costs remain constant in total irrespective of production level (within limits).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Variable cost per unit is:

A. Constant  
B. Increasing  
C. Decreasing  
D. Fluctuating randomly

Answer: A

Explanation:
Variable cost per unit remains constant; total variable cost changes with output.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Contribution is defined as:

A. Sales – Fixed cost  
B. Sales – Variable cost  
C. Fixed – Variable  
D. Profit – Cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
Contribution = Sales – Variable cost.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Break-even point occurs where:

A. Profit maximum  
B. Sales = Variable cost  
C. Total cost = Total revenue  
D. Contribution zero

Answer: C

Explanation:
At BEP, there is no profit or loss.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
Margin of Safety is:

A. Total sales  
B. Excess of actual sales over BE sales  
C. Profit  
D. Variable cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
Margin of Safety = Actual Sales – Break-even Sales.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
Standard costing is used for:

A. Historical recording  
B. Cost control  
C. Financial audit  
D. Tax planning

Answer: B

Explanation:
Standard costing compares actual cost with predetermined standards.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Budgetary control means:

A. Ignoring budget  
B. Comparing actual performance with budget  
C. Eliminating cost  
D. Increasing production

Answer: B

Explanation:
Budgetary control monitors performance against planned figures.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
Direct material cost is:

A. Indirect expense  
B. Traceable to product  
C. Administrative cost  
D. Selling cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
Direct materials are directly attributable to specific product.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
Indirect labour is part of:

A. Prime cost  
B. Factory overhead  
C. Direct expense  
D. Selling expense

Explanation:
Indirect labour is treated as factory overhead.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
Absorption costing includes:

A. Only variable cost  
B. Only fixed cost  
C. Both fixed and variable manufacturing cost  
D. Selling cost only

Answer: C

Explanation:
Absorption costing absorbs both fixed and variable production costs.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
Marginal costing considers:

A. Total cost  
B. Variable cost only  
C. Fixed cost only  
D. Profit only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Marginal costing focuses on variable cost for decision-making.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
Cost centre is:

A. Product

Cost centre is unit for which cost is accumulated.

Cost unit refers to:

## 19.
Opportunity cost is:

Opportunity cost reflects benefit sacrificed.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 20.
Sunk cost should be:

A. Considered  
B. Ignored

Explanation:
Sunk costs are past costs and irrelevant to decisions.

## 21.

Explanation:
100 – 60 = ₹40.

B. 3,000

Explanation:
2,00,000 / 50 = 4,000 units.

## 30.
Fixed cost ₹1,50,000; Contribution ₹2,00,000. P/V ratio?

A. 75%  
B. 50%  
C. 60%  
D. 40%

Answer: C

Explanation:
P/V ratio = Contribution / Sales.  
If Contribution 2,00,000 and assume Sales 3,33,333?  
Better interpretation: If BE = 1,50,000 / PVR.  
But since contribution > fixed cost, profit exists.  
If Contribution ₹2,00,000 and Sales ₹3,33,333 → PVR 60%.  
Thus ratio ≈ 60%.
