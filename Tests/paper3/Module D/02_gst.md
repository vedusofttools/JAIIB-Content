### PART A – Theory (1–20)
############################

## 1.
GST in India was implemented from:

A. 1 April 2016  
B. 1 July 2017  
C. 1 January 2018  
D. 15 August 2017

Answer: B

Explanation:
GST was implemented in India from 1 July 2017, replacing multiple indirect taxes.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
GST is a:

A. Direct tax  
B. Indirect tax  
C. Income tax  
D. Wealth tax

Answer: B

Explanation:
GST is an indirect tax levied on supply of goods and services.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
GST is levied on:

A. Manufacture  
B. Sale  
C. Supply  
D. Production

Answer: C

Explanation:
GST is levied on “supply” of goods and services.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
CGST is levied by:

A. State Government  
B. Central Government  
C. Local bodies  
D. RBI

Answer: B

Explanation:
CGST (Central GST) is collected by the Central Government.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
SGST is levied by:

A. Central Government  
B. State Government  
C. RBI  
D. SEBI

Answer: B

Explanation:
SGST (State GST) is levied by the State Government.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
IGST is applicable on:

A. Intra-state supply  
B. Inter-state supply  
C. Export only  
D. Import only

Answer: B

Explanation:
IGST is levied on inter-state supply of goods and services.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Input Tax Credit (ITC) means:

A. Refund of income tax  
B. Credit of GST paid on inputs  
C. Tax on exports  
D. Penalty

Answer: B

Explanation:
ITC allows set-off of GST paid on purchases against GST liability.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
GST is a destination-based tax because:

A. Levied at origin  
B. Revenue goes to consuming state  
C. Paid by producer  
D. Collected by RBI

Answer: B

Explanation:
GST revenue accrues to the state where goods/services are consumed.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Composition scheme is available for:

A. Large corporations  
B. Small taxpayers  
C. Exporters only  
D. Banks only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Composition scheme provides simplified compliance for small taxpayers.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
GST Council is chaired by:

A. RBI Governor

D. SEBI Chairman

Answer: B

Explanation:
Union Finance Minister chairs the GST Council.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
Place of supply determines:

A. Tax rate

C. Income tax

Place of supply decides whether transaction is intra-state or inter-state.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Exports under GST are treated as:

A. Taxable  
B. Zero-rated  
C. Exempt  
D. Nil-rated

Explanation:
Exports are zero-rated supplies under GST.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM) means:

A. Supplier pays tax  
B. Recipient pays tax  
C. Government pays tax  
D. Bank pays tax

Explanation:
Under RCM, recipient is liable to pay GST.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 21.
A dealer in Maharashtra sells goods worth ₹1,00,000 to buyer in Maharashtra. GST rate 18%. CGST and SGST?

A. CGST 9%, SGST 9%  
B. CGST 18% only  
C. IGST 18%  
D. SGST 18%

Answer: A

Explanation:
Intra-state supply → 18% split as 9% CGST + 9% SGST.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 22.
Goods worth ₹2,00,000 sold from Karnataka to Tamil Nadu. GST 18%. Type of tax?

A. CGST+SGST  
B. IGST 18%  
C. SGST only  
D. CGST only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Inter-state supply → IGST applicable.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 23.
Input GST paid ₹18,000; Output GST ₹25,000. Net payable?

A. ₹25,000  
B. ₹18,000  
C. ₹7,000  
D. ₹43,000

Answer: C

Explanation:
25,000 – 18,000 = ₹7,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 24.
If output tax ₹50,000; ITC ₹60,000. Balance credit?

A. ₹10,000 payable  
B. ₹10,000 credit carried forward  
C. Zero  
D. ₹60,000 payable

Answer: B

Explanation:
Excess ITC ₹10,000 carried forward.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 25.
Goods value ₹1,00,000; GST 12%. Total invoice value?

A. ₹1,12,000  
B. ₹12,000  
C. ₹1,00,000  
D. ₹88,000

Answer: A

Explanation:
12% of 1,00,000 = 12,000 → Total = 1,12,000.

------------------------------------------------------------

IGST ₹36,000 paid. ITC adjustment order generally:

A. IGST → CGST → SGST

IGST credit utilized first against IGST, then CGST, then SGST.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 27.
If GST rate increases, consumer price generally:

A trader under composition scheme:

Composition dealers cannot collect tax separately or claim ITC.

If place of supply and supplier location same state:
