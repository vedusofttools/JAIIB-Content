### PART A – Theory (1–15)
############################

## 1.
Standard costing is mainly used for:

A. Financial reporting  
B. Cost control  
C. Tax planning  
D. Dividend policy

Answer: B

Explanation:
Standard costing sets predetermined costs and compares them with actual costs to control performance.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
A standard cost is:

A. Historical cost  
B. Predetermined cost  
C. Market price  
D. Selling price

Answer: B

Explanation:
Standard cost is fixed in advance based on technical estimates.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
Variance is defined as:

A. Standard cost  
B. Actual cost  
C. Difference between standard and actual  
D. Total cost

Answer: C

Explanation:
Variance = Standard cost – Actual cost (or vice versa depending on convention).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
If actual cost exceeds standard cost, variance is:

A. Favorable  
B. Adverse  
C. Neutral  
D. Zero

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher actual cost than standard leads to adverse variance.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Material price variance measures:

A. Efficiency of usage  
B. Difference in price paid  
C. Labour performance  
D. Output difference

Answer: B

Explanation:
Material price variance = (Standard price – Actual price) × Actual quantity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
Material usage variance measures:

A. Price difference  
B. Quantity efficiency  
C. Labour efficiency  
D. Sales volume

Answer: B

Explanation:
Material usage variance = (Standard quantity – Actual quantity) × Standard price.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Labour rate variance is caused by:

A. Change in wage rate  
B. Change in material price  
C. Machine breakdown  
D. Sales decline

Answer: A

Explanation:
Labour rate variance arises due to difference in actual wage rate from standard rate.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Labour efficiency variance measures:

A. Wage rate change

D. Material cost

Explanation:
Labour efficiency variance = (Standard hours – Actual hours) × Standard rate.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Overhead variance is divided into:

Explanation:
Overheads are classified as fixed and variable for variance analysis.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 16.
Standard price ₹10; Actual price ₹12; Actual quantity 1,000 units. Material price variance?

A. ₹2,000 F  
B. ₹2,000 A  
C. ₹12,000  
D. ₹10,000

Answer: B

Explanation:
(10 – 12) × 1,000 = –2,000 → ₹2,000 Adverse.
