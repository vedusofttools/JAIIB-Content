############################
### PART A – Theory (1–15)
############################

## 1.
Branch profitability refers to:

A. Total bank profit only  
B. Profit generated by an individual branch  
C. RBI surplus  
D. Government subsidy

Answer: B

Explanation:
Branch profitability measures income and expenses attributable to a specific branch to assess its performance.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 2.
Major income source for a bank branch is:

A. Dividend income  
B. Interest income from advances  
C. Sale of assets  
D. RBI grant

Answer: B

Explanation:
Interest earned on loans and advances is the primary revenue source.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 3.
CASA ratio directly impacts branch profitability by:

A. Increasing tax  
B. Reducing cost of funds  
C. Increasing NPA  
D. Reducing deposits

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher CASA deposits reduce cost of funds, improving margins.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 4.
Net Interest Margin (NIM) measures:

A. Operating cost  
B. Interest income minus interest expense as % of earning assets  
C. Total profit  
D. Fixed cost

Answer: B

Explanation:
NIM = (Interest earned – Interest paid) / Average earning assets.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 5.
Non-interest income of branch includes:

A. Loan interest  
B. Service charges and commission  
C. RBI interest  
D. Dividend

Answer: B

Explanation:
Fee income such as locker rent, processing fees, and commission contribute to non-interest income.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 6.
High operating expenses at branch level reduce:

A. CASA  
B. Profitability  
C. Deposits  
D. Assets

Answer: B

Explanation:
Higher costs lower net profit of branch.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 7.
Cost-to-Income ratio measures:

A. Profitability  
B. Efficiency  
C. Liquidity  
D. Capital adequacy

Answer: B

Explanation:
Cost-to-Income ratio indicates operational efficiency (lower ratio is better).

------------------------------------------------------------

## 8.
Return on Assets (ROA) indicates:

A. Liquidity  
B. Profitability relative to total assets  
C. Cost of funds  
D. Capital adequacy

Answer: B

Explanation:
ROA = Net profit / Total assets.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 9.
Return on Equity (ROE) measures:

A. Profitability for shareholders  
B. Liquidity  
C. Operating cost  
D. Asset turnover

Answer: A

Explanation:
ROE = Net profit / Shareholders’ equity.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 10.
A branch with high advances but high NPAs will:

A. Improve profitability  
B. Reduce profitability  
C. Increase CASA  
D. Increase ROA

Answer: B

Explanation:
High NPAs reduce interest income and require provisioning.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 11.
Provisioning for NPAs affects:

A. Interest income  
B. Net profit  
C. Deposits  
D. CASA

Answer: B

Explanation:
Provisioning reduces net profit.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 12.
Business per employee measures:

A. Efficiency of staff utilization  
B. Profit only  
C. Tax  
D. NPA

Answer: A

Explanation:
Business per employee = Total deposits + advances / Number of employees.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 13.
Credit-Deposit (CD) ratio reflects:

A. Profit  
B. Proportion of deposits deployed as loans  
C. NPA level  
D. Cost of funds

Answer: B

Explanation:
CD ratio = Advances / Deposits × 100.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 14.
A very high CD ratio may indicate:

A. Under-utilization of funds  
B. Liquidity risk  
C. Low profitability  
D. High CASA

Answer: B

Explanation:
Excessive lending relative to deposits may cause liquidity issues.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 15.
Branch performance evaluation includes:

A. Only profit  
B. Profit, asset quality, business growth, and efficiency  
C. RBI audit only  
D. Treasury income only

Answer: B

Explanation:
Comprehensive evaluation includes profitability, growth, and asset quality.

## 16.
Branch deposits ₹200 crore; Advances ₹150 crore. CD ratio?

A. 75%  
B. 80%  
C. 60%  
D. 50%

Answer: A

Explanation:
150 / 200 × 100 = 75%.

------------------------------------------------------------

## 17.
Interest income ₹20 crore; Interest expense ₹12 crore; Earning assets ₹200 crore. NIM?

A. 4%  
B. 5%  
C. 6%  
D. 8%

Answer: A

Explanation:
(20 – 12) / 200 = 8 / 200 = 4%.

------------------------------------------------------------
